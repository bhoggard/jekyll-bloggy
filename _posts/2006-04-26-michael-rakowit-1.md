<p>This is great.  I&#8217;ve always been amazed by the idea of free street parking in New York, especially in Manhattan, given the fact that a single space would be worth $1000 or more per month if it was part of an apartment, to say nothing of what decent storage space costs.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.michaelrakowitz.com/">Michael Rakowitz</a> has an interesting project, titled <em>P(<span class="caps">LOT</span>)</em>, meant as a commentary on the use of public space for storing private vehicles.</p>
<p>Found via <a href="http://www.we-make-money-not-art.com/archives/008398.php">we make money not art</a>.  They have more information on their site.  The artist&#8217;s web site is not set up for easy bookmarking to an individual project.  I first heard of Mr. Rakowitz at White Columns, when I saw documentation of his <a href="http://www.we-make-money-not-art.com/archives/003936.php">paraSITE</a> project involving portable and temporary shelters for homeless people.</p>
