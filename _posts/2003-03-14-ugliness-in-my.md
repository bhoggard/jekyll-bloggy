<p><a href="http://www.aclu.org/LesbianGayRights/LesbianGayRights.cfm?ID=12082&c=106"><span class="caps">ACLU</span> Warns Arkansas School to Stop Persecuting Gay Student</a>:</p>
<blockquote><span class="caps">JACKSONVILLE</span>, AR &#8212; The American Civil Liberties Union is challenging officials at Jacksonville Junior High School over repeated punishment of a 14-year-old student for being openly gay. In a letter to school officials sent today, the <span class="caps">ACLU</span> demanded that the school stop violating the student&#8217;s rights and remove all unconstitutional disciplinary actions taken against him from his record by March 21 or face legal action.<br />
<br />
In its letter, the <span class="caps">ACLU</span> said that school officials &#8220;outed&#8221; the gay student, Thomas McLaughlin, to his parents against his wishes and have since told him he must not discuss being gay while at school, forced him to read from the Bible and disciplined him for being open about his sexual orientation. <br />
<br />
&#8230;</blockquote>
