<p>The <a href="http://www.nypress.com/17/35/pagetwo/pagetwo2.cfm">New York Press</a> isn&#8217;t exactly a left-wing paper, but here is what they have to say about the barbed wire holding pens on the pier on the Hudson:</p>
<blockquote>By the time we hit the streets with this issue, we&#8217;re sure it will be common knowledge, but it wasn&#8217;t until late last week that the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> announced their plans to turn Pier 57, at 11th Ave. and 15th St., into a giant holding pen. It was originally intended, of course, for the thousands of protestors they&#8217;re intent on rounding up during the convention.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
The message sent by all the security preparations prior to the convention was clear, but never so perfectly encapsulated as this makes it. What the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> and the <span class="caps">GOP</span> are saying to protestors of every stripe (and New Yorkers in general) is this: &#8220;If you don&#8217;t agree with us, we look at you as enemy combatants, security threats and would-be terrorists. And we&#8217;re going to treat you like the diseased cattle you are.&#8221;<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
The most disturbing bit of information concerning the West Side holding pen, however, was buried in the Post&#8217;s account. Just a brief mention:<br />
<br />
&#8220;Cops fear some protesters might hang around after the convention to disrupt other events, like the U.S. Open, so the pen will remain open indefinitely.&#8221;<br />
<br />
The U.S. Open? Other events? Like what, the 3rd Ave. Street Fair? The grand opening celebration at a new Payless Shoe Source in Queens?<br />
<br />
In other words, a year-round internment camp is now part of the ongoing West Side development project. Does the Olympic Committee know about this?</blockquote>
