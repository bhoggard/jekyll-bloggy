<p>The <a href="http://www.whitney.org/information/press/140.html">press release</a> with the list of artists is out.  Am I too connected, or is the Whitney being insufficiently daring?  Should there really only be a couple of names I don&#8217;t know on the list?  This isn&#8217;t even my line of work.</p>
<p>One more thought.  I was amused when the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/10/27/arts/design/27BIEN.html">Times article</a> said this.</p>
<blockquote>Two years ago, while Ms. Iles was in charge of its film and video and Debra Singer, the Whitney&#8217;s associate curator of contemporary art, selected the performance and sound art, the biennial was primarily put together with one pair of eyes, those of Lawrence R. Rinder, the Whitney&#8217;s curator of contemporary art. Critics felt it tried too hard to look for little-known artists whose work turned out to be unremarkable.</blockquote>
<p>I thought that show had too many people I had heard of already, rather than too many &#8220;obscure&#8221; ones.</p>
