<p>I still have this flu-like thing going, but I went for a few hours to this <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> <a href="http://www.dumboartscenter.org/festival/">festival</a>.  It was pretty cool, and there were a <span class="caps">HUGE</span> number of people there.  I think <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> is already becoming Williamsburg-esque.</p>
<p>Highlights:</p>
<p>Nina Levy sculptures at <a href="http://www.metaphorcontemporaryart.com/">Metaphor</a></p>
<p>Fabulous design and furniture at a new architecture firm called <a href="http://www.frcll.com/"><span class="caps">FREECELL</span></a>.  Also, check out their <a href="http://www.mobilivre.org/">cool bookmobile project</a> with artist books and zines.  They will be at <a href="http://www.cooper.edu/">Cooper Union</a> October 29-31.</p>
<p>Open Studios (just for the festival) &#8212; especially Andrea Cohen, David Baskin, and <a href="http://www.eyebeam.org/divisions/mimage_burns_martin.html">Torsten Zenas Burns</a>; and the current exhibit, from <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/">Smack Mellon</a>.</p>
