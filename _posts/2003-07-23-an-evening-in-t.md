<p>I spent the evening in the East Village, meeting my friend Cynthia at Benny&#8217;s East, a regular meeting spot for us over the last 13 years.  As I was walking there, I was aware of how bourgeois (and old) I feel now, but then I see an abomination such as some <a href="https://bloggy.com/mt/archives/evillage-fratboy2.html" onclick="window.open('http://bloggy.com/mt/archives/evillage-fratboy2.html','popup','width=375,height=500,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false">frat boy</a> walking on 13th Street between 1st and A and I realize I&#8217;m not much of an intruder after all.</p>
<p>I ran into a certain blogger on the way there, but I used his secret agent name rather than his <a href="http://info.astrian.net/jargon/terms/m/meatspace.html">meatspace</a> name, and he didn&#8217;t realize I was talking to him at first.</p>
<p>After Benny&#8217;s we went to our favorite East Village music store, <a href="http://www.ethereaonline.com/">Etherea</a>.  I was somewhat stunned to notice the tattoo on the Williamsburg-esque young man working there:  <a href="http://www.rudyfoto.com/hol/arbeit.html">Arbeit Macht Frei</a>, accompanied by a yellow star.</p>
