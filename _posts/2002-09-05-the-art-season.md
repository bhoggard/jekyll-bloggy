<p>It was a bit warm, so rather than wear something fabulous as my <a href="http://www.eastwest.nu/blog.shtml" title="Choire">hero</a> would have done, I attended a few openings dressed in my art uniform of shorts and an <a href="http://www.mta.info/museum/items-tshirt.htm"><span class="caps">MTA</span> shirt</a>.</p>
<p>Our first visit was to see <a href="http://www.artnet.com/Magazine/people/robinson/robinson5-20-21.asp" title="yes, she looks that waspy in person">Ann Craven</a> [<a href="http://www.curtmarcus.com/artists/artists_detail.asp?artistID=6">images</a>] at Klemens Gasser, which apparently doesn&#8217;t have a web site.  She makes beautiful, not quite real, paintings of birds and flowers &#8212; like greeting cards only better.  We had to go check it out since we acquired a watercolor of hers at <a href="/2002/08/barry-does-will.html">Bellwether&#8217;s party</a>.  Oh &#8212; here&#8217;s a <a href="http://www.e-flux.com/welcome/kg/index.html">page</a> that shows a photo that was in the back, and gives the gallery address.</p>
<p>The second opening was <a href="http://silversteingallery.com/CurrentExPage/mainEx.html">Andrew Guenther</a> at Silverstein Gallery &#8212; <b><i>great</i></b> show!  There&#8217;s a wall of beautiful-and-political-at-the-same-time drawings and watercolors, some of his paintings, some works by guest artists (indicated by big stars on the wall above), and most fun of all, a (moving) sculpture that &quot;is made up of a custom made coffin, built by the artist to fit his individual proportions, mounted on top of a mechanical rodeo bull. &quot;  Not surprisingly, the crowd at this opening was much more cool.  The gay boys were skinny and geeky, not the posey-muscley kind I saw at the other opening.</p>
<p>As we left, people were getting ice cream (to go with their beer) from the <a href="http://www.ucomics.com/misterboffo/2002/09/05/">truck</a> outside &#8212; playing that damn &#8220;Turkey in the Straw&#8221; rendition.</p>
