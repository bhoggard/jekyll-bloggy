<p>We spent a few hours in Williamsburg yesterday, lookin&#8217; at art as one of my college friends always said.</p>
<p>There are 2 cool shows at <a href="http://www.pierogi2000.com/">Pierogi 2000</a> &#8211; a French artist named <a href="http://www.pierogi2000.com/current.html">Christophe Cuzin</a> (conceptual alert &#8211; check out the info on him and the drawings in the office), and <a href="http://www.pierogi2000.com/currentnorthgallery.html">Lisa Kereszi</a> &#8212; a <span class="caps">GREAT</span> photography exhibit.  She goes on the &#8220;to buy&#8221; list once I recover from the list of recent benefits.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.eyewash.cc/">Eyewash</a> is presenting &#8220;Window Shopping&#8221; &#8212; 30 Brooklyn artists displaying work in shop windows along Bedford.  We picked up a map at Pierogi, or you can get the list at Eyewash&#8217;s web site.</p>
<p>Finally, there&#8217;s a big smart show at <a href=http://www.momentaart.org">Momenta</a>, curated by <a href="http://www.artretran.com/newsite/WORKS/JKL/KASS/Kass.html">Deborah Kass</a>.  It&#8217;s one of the best group shows I&#8217;ve seen in a while, ranging from 70s conceptual and feminist work to <a href="http://www.lagalerie.de/lagalerie_english_deutsch/l_a_galerie_artists/hiroshi_sunairi_english/hiroshiworke/hiroshiworke.html">Hiroshi Sunairi</a> and <a href="http://www.ericdoeringer.com/">Eric Doeringer</a>.  Go!</p>
