<p>I haven&#8217;t had much time to post lately, because I&#8217;ve been working on my new biz for hosting artist web sites.  The working name for the project is &#8220;ArtCat&#8221;, but there&#8217;s nothing corporate to show you yet.</p>
<p>I have the beta version of the first artist site up &#8212; <a href="http://www.meredithallen.com/home">Meredith Allen</a>.  I wasn&#8217;t completely ready to launch, but something big was about to happen!  Meredith&#8217;s photo on the homepage appeared this week in the September 1st issue of the New Yorker, as the illustration for a Dave Eggers story titled <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/fiction/content/?030901fi_fiction">Measuring the Jump</a>.</p>
<p>News Flash!  It&#8217;s now on the New Yorker <a href="http://www.newyorker.com/">home page</a>.</p>
<p>If any of you can link to her site or mention it in your weblogs, I would really apppreciate it.  I&#8217;m trying to get it to show up in Google so that people seeing her image in the New Yorker will end up at Meredith&#8217;s site.</p>
<p>The site will have more content, images, plus some design changes over the next days and weeks.  Her site is the test case for my new system, and I&#8217;m still working out some things.</p>
