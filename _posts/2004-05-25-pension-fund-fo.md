<p>Cool idea!  A new company called <a href="http://www.mutualart.com/">Mutual Art</a> has created a <a href="http://www.artistpensiontrust.org/">pension fund</a> for artists, which they fund by &#8220;investing&#8221; artworks rather than cash.</p>
<p>The advisory board includes John Baldessari and Kiki Smith.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://www.artistpensiontrust.org/fundartists.asp?ID=2">list of artists</a> accepted into the initial trust includes Tim Lokiec, Jules de Balincourt, Aida Ruilova, and Anthony Goicolea.</p>
<p>Here is the description from their <a href="http://www.artistpensiontrust.org/faq.asp"><span class="caps">FAQ</span></a> of how it works:</p>
<blockquote>A region-specific selection committee comprised of individuals who are broadly experienced in working with emerging artists will identify artists to be invited to apply for participation in a Trust. The committee will review each applicant&#8217;s work and select those who will participate in a Trust. Only self-employed artists may participate in <span class="caps">APT</span>.<br />
<br />
The Selection Committee members for the <span class="caps">APT</span>, New York are Clarissa Dalrymple, Jeffrey Deitch, David A. Ross, Jack Tilton and Simon Watson.<br />
<br />
<span class="caps">APT</span> is a barter-based program. Rather than cash, participants will contribute works of art to their Trust, investing 20 works over a 20-year period. Artists will start to receive income 20 years after the inception of their <span class="caps">APT</span>.<br />
<br />
Income for payments to the participants will come from the sale of works held by a Trust. Fifty percent of the net proceeds from these sales accrue to the retirement benefit accounts of all participants and the remaining fifty percent will accrue to each artist&#8217;s own retirement benefit account. Each artist receives an equal share of the pooled funds generated by the sale of the works held in a Trust, thereby benefiting from the collective success of all of the artists in their Trust. Additionally, each artist is rewarded proportionally to individual market success, since 50% of the net proceeds of the sale of his/her work are invested in individual benefit accounts. </blockquote>
