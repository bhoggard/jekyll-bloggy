<p>My iBook has been in <a href="http://www.tekserve.com/">the shop</a> since last Thursday, so I&#8217;m not posting too often.  I&#8217;m relying on my Dell desktop running, yes, Windows 98 until it comes back.  What follows is a random assortment of my adventures and observations over the last few days.</p>
<p>On Sunday afternoon we went to Greenpoint to see Meredith Allen&#8217;s show of Williamsburg art world people at <a href="http://www.imnil.com/">im in iL</a>.  Since the G is not a train to be used lightly, we took the L to Bedford and walked the mile or so to the gallery in Greenpoint.  I hadn&#8217;t spent much time in Greenpoint, and was pleasantly surprised by the number of 19th century buildings, plus stores and streets that still have some integrity.  We even saw a Greek Revival house just off Manhattan Avenue!  There are several independent Polish language bookstores in the neighborhood, when Chelsea doesn&#8217;t even have one independent &#8220;new books&#8221; bookstore.  Another cool discovery was &#8220;Java and Wood&#8221;, a furniture / coffee shop at 1011 Manhattan Ave.  Check it out when you&#8217;re in the area.</p>
<p>When we were waiting in the subway, I saw a young man with sunglasses, Capri pants, and an Aunt Jemima-style kerchief on his head.  Fabulous!  It was a welcome antidote to seeing a Chelsea boy at the gym earlier in the weekend wearing a du rag with his Abercrombie &amp; Fitch cargo pants.  Ugh.</p>
<p>Recently seen art of note:</p>
<ul><li>Stas Orlovski at <a href="http://www.mixedgreens.com/mixedgreens/features/features_articles_template.jhtml?groupId=232">Mixed Greens</a></li>
<li>&#8220;Undesire&#8221;, curated by Vasif Kortun, at <a href="http://www.apexart.org/kortun.htm">Apex Art</a> &#8212; great, great show.  There is a video of two young boys singing and dancing in an <span class="caps">ATM</span> room, by an artist named Fikret Atay.  He lives close to the Iraqi border in Turkey, in a depressed village called Batman.  I had trouble tearing myself away from the video to look at anything else in the show.  The video by the Irish artist Phil Collins (not the singer), titled <a href="http://www.temple-bar.ie/eova.asp">Baghdad Screen Tests</a>, consists of several citizens of Baghdad sitting silently, looking at the camera &#8212; filmed before the war started.  It is accompanied by recordings of various pop songs, ranging from Elvis to The Smiths.  Finally, the drawings of <a href="http://www.invitation.kunsthaus-essen.de/artists/dan/dan2.html">Dan Perjovschi</a>, which you have probably seen on the invitation, are sweet and scathing at the same time.</li>
<li>Carl Scholz&#8217;s flawlessly &#8220;smoothed out&#8221; Jaguar at <a href="http://www.momentaart.org/cur_pro/artist2.html">Momenta</a></li>
</ul>
<p>One last art item is <a href="http://www.emilynoellelambert.com/">Emily Noelle Lambert</a> at the Mini minimarket at 218 Bedford (near Earwax, etc.).  We walked into the shop for the first time, and noticed Emily&#8217;s work on exhibit in the store.  After a few minutes we decided we had to purchase one of the (very affordable) works.  I don&#8217;t see something too close to what we bought on the web site, but check out a few interesting things I found on her site: <a href="http://www.emilynoellelambert.com/html/castleball.html">Castle</a>, <a href="http://www.emilynoellelambert.com/html/bird.html">Bird</a>, and <a href="http://www.emilynoellelambert.com/html/charlesandsmokey.html">Charles and Smokey</a>.</p>
