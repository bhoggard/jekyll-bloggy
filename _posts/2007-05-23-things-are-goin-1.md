<p>These links are a good introduction to an article at <a href="http://www.tomdispatch.com/index.mhtml?pid=194902">TomDispatch</a> by Chalmers Johnson, author of <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0805079114/tristanmedial-20"><em>Nemesis: The Last Days of the American Republic</em></a>.  Here is an excerpt:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>According to an <a href="http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/18312789/"><span class="caps">NBC</span> News/Wall Street Journal poll</a>, released on April 26, 2007, some 78% of Americans believe their country to be headed in the wrong direction. Only 22% think the Bush administration&#8217;s policies make sense, the lowest number on this question since October 1992, when George H. W. Bush was running for a second term &#8212; and lost. What people don&#8217;t agree on are the reasons for their doubts and, above all, what the remedy &#8212; or remedies &#8212; ought to be.</p>
<p>The range of opinions on this is immense. Even though large numbers of voters vaguely suspect that the failings of the political system itself led the country into its current crisis, most evidently expect the system to perform a course correction more or less automatically. As Adam Nagourney of the New York Times <a href="http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9C02E1DA163FF936A35757C0A9619C8B63">reported</a>, by the end of March 2007, at least 280,000 American citizens had already contributed some $113.6 million to the presidential campaigns of Hillary Rodham Clinton, Barack Obama, John Edwards, Mitt Romney, Rudolph Giuliani, or John McCain.</p>
<p>If these people actually believe a presidential election a year-and-a-half from now will significantly alter how the country is run, they have almost surely wasted their money. As Andrew Bacevich, author of The New American Militarism, <a href="http://www.thenation.com/doc/20070423/bacevich">puts it</a>: &#8220;None of the Democrats vying to replace President Bush is doing so with the promise of reviving the system of check and balances&#8230;. The aim of the party out of power is not to cut the presidency down to size but to seize it, not to reduce the prerogatives of the executive branch but to regain them.&#8221; </blockquote></p>
