<p><a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=578&ncid=578&e=1&u=/nm/20030621/ts_nm/iraq_dc">&#8216;Apocalypse Now&#8217; Music Fires Up U.S. Troops for Raid</a></p>
<blockquote>U.S. troops psyched up on a bizarre musical reprise from Vietnam war film &#8220;Apocalypse Now&#8221; before crashing into Iraqi homes to hunt gunmen on Saturday, as Shi&#8217;ite Muslims rallied against the U.S. occupation of Iraq.<br />
<br />
With the strains of Wagner&#8217;s &#8220;Ride of the Valkyries&#8221; still ringing in their ears and the clatter of helicopters overhead, soldiers rammed vehicles into metal gates and hundreds of troops raided houses in the western city of Ramadi after sunrise as part of a drive to quell a spate of attacks on U.S. forces. <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
There was nothing secretive about Saturday&#8217;s robust sweep through Ramadi, 100 km (60 miles) west of Baghdad, by soldiers of the First Battalion of the 124th Infantry Regiment who psyched themselves up at a base on a musical moment redolent of Francis Ford Coppola&#8217;s 1979 film about the Vietnam war. </blockquote>
