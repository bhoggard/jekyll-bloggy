<p>Last night we went to a great dance performance by Jeremy Wade.  Not only was his choreography and dancing amazing, so were his collaborators:</p>
<ul>
	<li>Jessica Hill as his dance partner in the first piece, <em>Glory</em></li>
	<li>Music by <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003988.html">Michael Mahalchick</a> and Loren Kiyoshi Dempster for <em>Glory</em></li>
	<li>Music by Mike Skinner (and Jeremy Wade) for <em>Fiction</em></li>
	<li>Lighting by Jonathan Belcher</li>
</ul>
<p>Jeremy Wade did the costumes as well.</p>
<p>His choreography uses dancers&#8217; faces in a way I have never seen before.  I also appreciated the mix of&#8230; how to put this&#8230; movements reminiscient of a child, or a person without full muscle control.  I can&#8217;t get his child-like gestures of joy out of my mind.  Interestingly, in the duet Jessica Hill&#8217;s movements were a mix of those plus more conventionally dance-like moves.  She was almost doing a pop and lock during parts of the early section.</p>
<p>There are two more nights &#8212; Friday (tonight) and Saturday &#8212; both at 7:30pm.  Go!  He is a definitely a distinctive voice in <span class="caps">NYC</span> dance.</p>
<p>Tickets information is <a href="http://dtw.org/dy_eventDetails.cfm?ID=105">here</a>.</p>
