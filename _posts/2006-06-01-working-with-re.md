<p>I often see arguments that New York needs to work with the <span class="caps">GOP</span>, since they control all three branches of government, and it&#8217;s the only way to get anything done for New York.  Bullshit.  The only sane thing to do is work to defeat every Republican we can.  There is no reason why New York State should have any Republicans representing us in Congress.  And once we&#8217;ve done that, we can launch challenges from the left for politicians like Hilary Clinton and Chuck Schumer who are pro-Iraq War and pro-<span class="caps">PATRIOT</span> Act.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.ny1.com/ny1/content/index.jsp?stid=1&amp;aid=59863">NY1</a> reports:<br />
<blockquote><br />
Local politicians are slamming the Department of Homeland Security Wednesday for its decision to slash New York CityÃ¢Â€Â™s counter-terrorism funding by $83 million this year, a nearly 40 percent cut from the previous year.</p>
<p>The Department of Homeland Security today announced its new national distribution plan, which divides a total of $740 million between 46 cities. <span class="caps">DHS</span> say the cuts will help spread funding to other communities facing threats.</p>
<p>The new funding formula shows the Big Apple will have to make do with $124 million in federal homeland security grants for the 2006 fiscal year, down from $207 million last year.</p>
<p>New York State is also taking a cut of just under $115 million this year, despite promises from Homeland Security Secretary Michael Chertoff earlier this year that his department would be distributing money based on risk.</p>
<p>&#8230;</p>
<p>While New York is facing cuts in funding, the <span class="caps">DHS</span> has decided to increase funding for cities including Omaha, Nebraska; Louisville, Kentucky; and Jacksonville, Florida.</blockquote></p>
<p>Funding was also cut for Washington, DC.  Let&#8217;s be realistic here.  If a terrorist wants to set off a dirty bomb, it&#8217;s going to happen in a dense city like New York or DC, not some place like Omaha with less than 500,000 people.</p>
<p>Who is the head of the House of Representatives&#8217; Homeland Security Committee?  Peter King, a Republican congressman from Long Island.  If this is what &#8220;working with Republicans&#8221; get us, I can&#8217;t imagine a better reason to work on throwing them all out.</p>
