<p>Tomorrow (Saturday the 9th) is the last day to see <a href="http://www.todseelie.com/&#39;s">Tod Seelie</a> show, titled <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/7517"><em>Slowdancing to Slayer</em></a>, at Cinders Gallery in Williamsburg &#8212; their first photo show.  They&#8217;ve had something come up and are not open today, so head on over tomorrow for some great photography at bargain prices.  The prints are approximately $200-600, framed.  There is something for everyone, from portraits of people along the Mississippi to shots of punk shows in abandoned warehouses.</p>
<p>In addition to really loving this show, I have another connection.  Everyone asks about the balsa wood squirrel sculpture in our living room, hanging on an old column.  It&#8217;s by Kim Schifino and James and I bought it a year ago at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/4899"><em>The Porch Show</em></a> at Cinders.  Tod is currently touring with the band she formed with Matt Johnson, <a href="http://www.mattandkimmusic.com/">Matt and Kim</a>, and <span class="caps">CSS</span>.   Check out his blog <a href="http://suckapants.com/">suckapants.com</a> for photos.  You can buy Matt and Kim CDs at Cinders.  That&#8217;s where I got mine.</p>
