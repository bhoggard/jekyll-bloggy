<p>Clinton was nearly impeached for lying about sex, but Bush appoints Elliott Abrams, <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/col/story/42297p-39766c.html">who lied to Congress in the Iran-Contra affair</a>.</p>
<p>The judge that says <a href="http://www.upi.com/print.cfm?StoryID=20021209-051307-3088r">we can&#8217;t see Cheney&#8217;s energy task force records</a> fought to release Clinton White House records <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A38773-2002Nov25.html">when he was a deputy of Kenneth Starr</a>.</p>
<p>Trent Lott says <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/12/10/opinion/10KRUG.html?pagewanted=print&position=top">we would have been better off if the segregationist Dixiecrats had won the presidency with Strom Thurmond</a>, but <a href="http://talkingpointsmemo.com/dec0202.html#1209021027pm">it takes days for the &#8216;liberal&#8217; media to notice</a>.</p>
<p>The U.S. <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&ncid=578&e=1&cid=578&u=/nm/20021210/ts_nm/iraq_dc">intercepts</a> the Iraq arms dossier intended for the U.N. to &#8220;make copies&#8221; and says it will only share it with certain members of the Security Council &#8212; not, so far, <a href="http://www.alertnet.org/thenews/newsdesk/L1080136">the weapons inspection team</a>.  Cynics might argue this is because it <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/wire/World/ap20021210_547.html">lists suppliers, including the U.S. and other Security Council members</a>.</p>
<p>What pill can I take to stop noticing things?  We&#8217;re not even pretending to be a real republic anymore.  One party state anyone?</p>
