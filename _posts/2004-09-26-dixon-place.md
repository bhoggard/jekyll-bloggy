Ellie Covan, founder of Dixon Place at her apartment (which is also the home of Dixon Place)</p>
<p><a href="http://www.dixonplace.org/">Dixon Place</a> is a great example of why so many of us moved to New York.  It is &#8220;an artistic laboratory with an audience,&#8221; a place where performing artists of all kinds try out work in progress before an audience.  From the very beginning, Dixon Place has also made a commitment to pay the performers who appear there.  For a space in <span class="caps">NYC</span> that hosts works in progress, that&#8217;s pretty rare.</p>
<p>It is all the creation of Ellie Covan, one of the saints of the art world.  I have been going to Dixon Place since it moved to the current (and past) loft on the Bowery.  <a href="http://jameswagner.com/">James</a> has been going since it was in Ellie&#8217;s apartment in the East Village.  During that time, people were told to start singing Happy Birthday if the police arrived, as it was a rather irregular situation.</p>
<p>The <em>New York Times</em> Real Estate section has an <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2004/09/26/realestate/26HABI.html">article</a> in its Habitats section on Ellie and Dixon Place.  It also talks about Dixon Place&#8217;s <a href="http://www.dixonplace.org/html/capital_campaign.html">new space on Chrystie Street</a>, which is currently being constructed.  They are still raising money for it, and I can think of few organizations more worthy of your money, if you have any to spare.  None of you who go out and buy $10 drinks every weekend can plead complete poverty!</p>
