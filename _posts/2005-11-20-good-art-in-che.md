<p>Earlier in the season, I wasn&#8217;t feeling so excited by the work I was seeing in Chelsea, except for a few exception like the always-reliable <a href="http://www.foxyproduction.com/">Foxy Production</a>.</p>
<p>Our visits in the last couple of weeks, though, have made me much happier.  I&#8217;ll put of some highlights tonight, and add a few more in the next day or so.</p>
<p><img alt="larry-mantello-marge.jpg" src="/mt/archives/larry-mantello-marge.jpg" width="400" height="600" /></p>
<p>Larry Mantello&#8217;s outrageous installations at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/1388">Kustera Tilton</a>.  We first saw his work in a great installation at Henry Urback in <a href="/2003/04/art.html">2003</a>.</p>

<p><img src="http://static.artcat.com/calendar/a57beab7611dc3361dc33e81e52adf7a05200edd.jpeg" alt="Julia Oschatz" /></p>
<p>Julia Oschatz, <em>Untitled (72-05)</em>, 2005, Oil, enamel, acrylic, spray paint on canvas, 21 5/8 Ã— 15 inches</p>
<p>Julia Oschatz&#8217;s New York debut show of videos, installation, and paintings at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/1486">Leslie Tonkonow</a>  is one of my favorites of the year.  Check out the <a href="http://www.tonkonow.com/oschatz.html">gallery web site</a> for more images, plus some video excerpts.</p>

<p>James has already written about the Josephine Meckseper show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/1420">Elizabeth Dee</a>, so go <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/005240.html">read him</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="mark-napier-empire.jpg" src="/mt/archives/mark-napier-empire.jpg" width="225" height="225" /></p>
<p>Mark Napier&#8217;s show <em>Empire</em>, at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/1253">bitforms</a>, manages to combine interactive computer-based art with a beautiful visual aesthetic that I have rarely seen in the field.  You know it&#8217;s a good tech-y art show when James is really into it.  Visit Mark&#8217;s <a href="http://www.potatoland.org/">web site</a> for more information on his work.</p>
