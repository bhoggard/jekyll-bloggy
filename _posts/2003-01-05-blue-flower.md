<blockquote>&#8230; in a river every moment passing new &#8230; I climbed the Eiffel Tower, and saw the rooftops from the angels&#8217; view.  Now things will never, will never be the same.  They will never, will never be the same.</blockquote>
<p>I saw <a href="http://www.weimarband.com/summary.html">Blue Flower</a> by the <a href="http://www.weimarband.com/">Weimarband</a> at <a href="http://www.here.org"><span class="caps">HERE</span></a> tonight.  It&#8217;s still a work in progress, but musically it&#8217;s very good &#8212; they describe themselves as Sturm n&#8217; Twang, or Kurt Weill meets Hank Williams &#8212; with strong musicians and talented, charismatic singers.  There are <a href="http://www.weimarband.com/sounds.html">samples</a> on the Weimbarband web site.</p>
<p>The visual design, by Ruth Bauer, uses beautiful slides of her own creation plus historic images of people such as <a href="/2002/09/doing-bad-ischl.html">Franz Josef</a> and <a href="http://hum.amu.edu.pl/~zbzw/ph/sci/msc.htm">Marie Curie</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://www.weimarband.com/background.html">historical context and references</a> range from the events leading to WW I, the <a href="http://www.barnsdle.demon.co.uk/hist/weilin.html">Weimar Republic</a>, a fictionalized menage of <a href="http://www.franz-marc-museum.de/">Franz Marc</a>, <a href="http://www.metmuseum.org/collections/view1.asp?dep=21&full=0&item=67%2E187%2E53a%2Dc">Max Beckmann</a>, <a href="http://www.grandstreet.com/gs/gs69/hoch/">Hannah Höch</a>, and Marie Curie, plus Dada.  Part of it takes place at the <a href="http://www.mital-u.ch/Dada/start.html">Cabaret Voltaire</a> &#8212; the last time Zurich was really interesting.</p>
<p>While watching the performance tonight, I was reminded of the <a href="/2003/01/this-explains-a.html">quote</a> I posted earlier.  I worry about the ability of artists to create works with historical resonance, or references, given the dumbing down of our culture and the nearly complete lack of historical or cultural knowledge.  No wonder people think movies are the highest art form now.  Most of them are easy on the eyes, don&#8217;t make you think too much, <a href="/2002/12/gangs-of-new-yo.html">ignore history</a>, and give you musical cues about how you&#8217;re supposed to be reacting.  I wonder if I&#8217;ll have to rely on European culture to keep such ideas alive for a bit longer, at least as long as I live.  That&#8217;s one more reason to work on my languages.</p>
