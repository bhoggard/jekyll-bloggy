<p>Inspired by Greg Allen&#8217;s <a href="http://www.greg.org/2003_04_01_archive.html#200098354">post</a> regarding Norway&#8217;s non-violent resistant to Nazi occupation, I present a piano piece by Harald SÃ¦verud titled &#8220;Ballad of revolt&#8221;: <a href="https://bloggy.com/mp3/andsnes-harald_saeverud-ballad_of_revolt.mp3">MP3</a> (3:19, 2.3MB).</p>
<p>The story from the composer regarding the work:</p>
<blockquote>I was not in a good mood, standing there freezing, getting more and more angry as I spotted the German barracks on the hillsides, disgustingly marring our lovely scenery.  My parents had not taught me to swear, but I had to make some kind of exclamation&#8230; <span class="caps">BAM</span> <span class="caps">BAM</span>&#8230; Like a shot the theme for <i>Ballad of Revolt</i> came to me.  And this was my shot during the war</blockquote>
<p>The work, written in 1943 is dedicated to &#8220;small and large Resistance fighters&#8221;, and it became a symbol for the Norwegian struggle during the war.</p>
