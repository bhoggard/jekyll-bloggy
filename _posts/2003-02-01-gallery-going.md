<p>We wandered around without a plan in Chelsea today after having lunch with friends.  Normally, <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> has a very planned itinerary, so it was nice to find things serendipitously.</p>
<p>Things I liked:<br />
<ul><li>Run, do not walk, to the $99 Bargain Store Show at <a href="http://www.cynthiabroan.com/">Cynthia Broan</a> (curated by <a href="http://www.cynthiabroan.com/frameset_thyzel.html">Tim Thyzel</a>)</li></p>
<li><a href="http://www.gblgallery.com/exhibitions/03_northridge.html">Matthew Northridge</a> at Gorney Bravin + Lee.  I felt less strongly about some of the work in the main room, but the collages in the project room at the back were exquisite.</li>
<li>Jan Dibbets at <a href="http://www.gladstonegallery.com/">Barbara Gladstone</a>.  I love the way the works read as beautiful abstracts at a distance, while the individual elements are wonderful photographs on their own.</li>
<li>&#8220;Air&#8221;, a group show, at <a href="http://www.jamescohan.com/">James Cohan</a>.  For me the standout work was a video by Hiraki Sawa titled <a href="http://www.jamescohan.com/index2.php?id=1&page=1&num_pages=2&work=111&image=927">Dwelling</a> &#8212; another still <a href="http://www.artnet.com/Magazine/reviews/manson/manson1-14-13.asp">here</a> &#8212; in which airplane models fly about an apartment.  In an era when flying machines conjure negative thoughts, it was soothing to watch a happy, magical work with flying planes.</li>
</ul>
