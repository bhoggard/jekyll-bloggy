<p>I just got back from standing in the rain, watching the firefighters march up Eighth Avenue from 14th Street to Madison Square Garden for the memorial service there.    When I awoke this morning, the first thing I heard through my apartment windows was the sound of bagpipes.</p>
<p>There were only a few people standing on the sidewalks watching.  The barricades that had been up since 5 last night seemed overkill.</p>
<p>They all seemed fascinated by the <span class="caps">NYSC</span> on the corner of 23rd and Eighth.  Maybe there was a class with pretty women happening &#8212; all of the ones I saw do that were men.  The departments from Florida and Texas were notable for the amount of Latinos/Latinas in their groups &#8212; a lot more than we have.</p>
<p>I saw men in kilts, bagpipe players having finished their march, carrying five cameras at once to take pictures for their friends that were still marching.</p>
<p>Most of them were Americans, but there were a lot of Canadians, and we saw one small group of Italians and one that was French.  I&#8217;m sure there were more countries, but I didn&#8217;t see the whole procession.  A policeman standing near us was telling a neighbor from my building about going to Ground Zero to try to help &#8212; &#8220;they were turning people away, there were so many.&#8221;</p>
<p>I could hear the ceremony starting a little after ten on the loudspeakers that were set up along the avenue.  I think Giuliani&#8217;s living with a gay friend in his apartment for a while has been good for him.  The first thing he said was that he wished that <a href="http://www.saintmychal.com/life01.htm">Father Mychal Judge</a> were there to speak first.  He also said that the people who died were trying to rescue people regardless of race, religion, or sexual orientation.</p>
