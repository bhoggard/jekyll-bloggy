<p>Today&#8217;s <em>Newsday</em> has an <a href="http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/arts/ny-ettop5476801nov27,0,4642885.story">article</a> on the artist <a href="http://www.joanlinder.com/">Joan Linder</a> by Ariella Budick and it&#8217;s one of the best things I&#8217;ve read about her work.  There is a short video too.  Here is an excerpt:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Turning the tables on generations of male artists who offered up the anatomies of women as fruits for delectation, Linder painted a series of &#8220;Men&#8221; who don&#8217;t seem aware that they&#8217;re hardly wearing any clothes. Corporate executives literally throw their weight around at the office, opening a file drawer or searching for a contact lens while bulbous bellies overhang their briefs and wobble over their stick-like legs.</p>
<p>&#8220;Nobody wants to look at this work,&#8221; Linder admits. &#8220;People don&#8217;t want to see naked men.&#8221;</p>
<p>The drawings were a response to Linder&#8217;s contemporaries Lisa Yuskavage and John Currin, who merge the classic nude with the Playboy kind. &#8220;I thought, &#8216;Screw that. I&#8217;m going to do these pictures of fat men and what power really looks like.&#8217;&#8221;</blockquote></p>
<p>We first met Joan in 2001 after buying a photo from her <a href="http://www.joanlinder.com/pages.php?content=gallery.php&navGallID=7">&#8220;Man About Town&#8221; series</a> at the <a href="http://www.winkleman.com/exhibition/view/1052">inaugural show</a> of Plus Ultra Gallery, titled &#8220;Skank.&#8221;  We also have a small drawing of an electrical outlet from more recent times.</p>
<p>The image above is a detail from her <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/5701">recent show</a> at Mixed Greens, via her website.</p>
