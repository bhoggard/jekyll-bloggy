<p>James and I ran into Kate Valk of <a href="http://www.thewoostergroup.org/">The Wooster Group</a> at the Isidro Blasco opening at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/3681"><span class="caps">DKCT</span> Contemporary</a> last Saturday.  We hadn&#8217;t realized that she does a radio show called &#8220;The Next Hour&#8221; on <a href="http://www.wbai.org/"><span class="caps">WBAI</span></a> with <a href="http://www.andrewandrewdotcom.com/">Andrew Andrew</a>.  They were accompanied by our friend <a href="http://www.ericdoeringer.com/">Eric Doeringer</a> and had spent the day look at Chelsea art shows.  The show is archived for easy listening, and it&#8217;s a lot of fun.  Go <a href="http://archive.wbai.org/">here</a> and scroll down to January 7, or just use <a href="http://archive.wbai.org/pls.php?mp3fil=9882">this link</a> to stream it.  The recording times are a a little off, so skip about 2 minutes and 50 seconds to get to the beginning.</p>
<p>You can see Kate, one of the most brilliant performers I have ever seen, in The Wooster Group&#8217;s &#8220;Hamlet&#8221; in <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> at <a href="http://www.artsatstanns.org/">St. Ann&#8217;s Warehouse</a> beginning February 27.  Buy your tickets now, as it will definitely sell out.</p>
