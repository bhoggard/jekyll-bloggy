<p>The Washington Post <a title="Drudging Up Personal Details (washingtonpost.com)" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A8158-2003Jul17.html">reports</a> that the White House was so unhappy about an <span class="caps">ABC</span> News story about bad morale in Iraq that they pointed out to Matt Drudge that the reporter who did the story is gay and Canadian.  Unless they&#8217;re trying to use jingoism and homophobia to further their fascist aims, what&#8217;s the point?</p>
<blockquote>Some folks in the White House were apparently hopping mad when <span class="caps">ABC</span> News correspondent Jeffrey Kofman did a story on Tuesday&#8217;s &#8220;World News Tonight&#8221; about the plummeting morale of U.S. soldiers stationed in Iraq.<br />
<br />
So angry, in fact, that the next day, a White House operative alerted cyber-gossip Matt Drudge to the fact that Kofman is not only openly gay, he&#8217;s Canadian.<br />
<br />
Yesterday Drudge told us he was unaware of the <span class="caps">ABC</span> story until &#8220;someone from the White House communications shop tipped me to it&#8221; along with a profile of Kofman in the gay-oriented magazine the Advocate.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
White House Press Secretary Scott McClellan &#8220;is having a rough first week,&#8221; Drudge said. &#8220;The White House press office is under new management and has become slightly more aggressive about contacting reporters. This story has certainly become talk radio fodder about the cultural wars-slash-liberal bias in the media.&#8221;<br />
<br />
A network insider was less sanguine about the White House tactic: &#8220;Playing hardball is one thing. But appealing to homophobia and jingoism is simply ugly.&#8221;</blockquote>
<p>Mr. Drudge is rather quiet about his own <a href="http://www.signorile.com/articles/nyp28.html">orientation</a> when the subject comes up.</p>
