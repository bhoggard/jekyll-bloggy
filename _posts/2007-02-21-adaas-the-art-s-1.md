<p>James and I visited press preview for <a href="http://www.artdealers.org/artshow/index.html">&#8220;The Art Show&#8221;</a> of the Art Dealers Association of America (<span class="caps">ADAA</span>) today.  Some general notes first.  Given our usual focus on underknown art, one might might expect a show billed primarily as showing &#8220;museum quality work&#8221; might not be our thing, but I had a great time.  I wonder if &#8220;the market&#8221; seems to be working better for work that&#8217;s not by <span class="caps">MFA</span> students and recent grads, as I saw some phenomenal pieces by established (and yes, dead) artists including a lot of amazing work from circa 1950 New York &#8212; Pollock, Avery, etc.  Overall I enjoyed the work I saw more than quite a few shows of 20th century art I&#8217;ve seen lately at museums in <span class="caps">NYC</span>.  Most surprising artist to see in several places?  Belgian artist Marcel Broodthaers.  There were quite a few Albers and Rothko paintings in various booths.  I also have to say that it is so nice to not be looked at as if one has two heads when asking to be sent a <span class="caps">JPEG</span>.  Even two years ago I would get that at plenty of art fair booths.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.crggallery.com/"><span class="caps">CRG</span> Gallery</a> had one of the best booths, with a show of Jim Hodges spiderweb chain sculptures plus an ink on paper work called <em>Wanted Poster 1</em> from 1991-1992.</p>
