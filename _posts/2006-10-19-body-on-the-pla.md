<p>James and I went to a couple of openings last night at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/8/3285">Apex</a> and <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/8/3341">KS Art</a> before seeing Adam Bock&#8217;s play <em>Thugs</em> at <a href="http://www.sohorep.org/">Soho Rep</a>.  Before I distract you too much, tickets to the play are only $15, and I recommend seeing it if you&#8217;re interested at all in theater, or if you ever worked as a temp in <span class="caps">NYC</span>.  Our friend <a href="/2003/01/theatre-is-aliv.html">Anne Kauffman</a> directed it.  It&#8217;s described on the website:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Mysterious things are happening on the 9th floor of a big law firm. What could a new temp have to fear? A new play about work, thunder, and people you don&#8217;t know.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>It&#8217;s quite like a music chamber piece in form.</p>
<p>On the way home around 9, the Eighth Avenue trains were running express.  They said it was due to some kind of &#8220;passenger emergency.&#8221;  We decided to take the A to 34th and walk back down to our apartment.  As we passed through the 14th Street station, we saw plenty of cops, some yellow tape, and a body bag on the platform plus some amount of blood.  When things like that happen, one always expects to come home and see all kinds of headlines, but it&#8217;s strange that it actually takes a while for what seem like pretty shocking events to show up in the media, whether online or on NY1.</p>
<p>Today, the <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/10-19-2006/news/story/463212p-389735c.html">New York Daily News</a> has the story.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>A man trying to board a crowded C train between cars was crushed to death last night when the train started moving and he became wedged against the platform, police said.</p>
</blockquote>
