<p>There is <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=586&e=4&u=/nm/20031009/wl_nm/nobel_peace_dc">some talk</a> that the Pope might win the Nobel Peace Prize.  Given the Church&#8217;s <a href="http://www.catholicsforchoice.org/new/pressrelease/100103EuroReport.htm">continuous attacks</a> on people trying to prevent <span class="caps">AIDS</span> or unwanted pregnancies, or its <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/sections/wnt/DailyNews/Ireland_abuse030126.html">enslavement</a> of young Irish women, I would think the Pope belongs in The Hague for crimes against humanity.  The latest outrage is the Church telling people in the developing world that <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/health/3176982.stm">condoms cannot prevent <span class="caps">AIDS</span></a>, so they should not use them.</p>
<blockquote>The Catholic Church has been accused of telling people in countries with high rates of <span class="caps">HIV</span> that condoms do not protect against the deadly virus.<br />
<br />
The claims are made in a Panorama programme called Sex and the Holy City to be screened on <span class="caps">BBC</span> One on Sunday.<br />
<br />
It says cardinals, bishops, priests and nuns in four continents are saying <span class="caps">HIV</span> can pass through tiny holes in condoms.<br />
<br />
The World Health Organization has condemned the comments and warned the Vatican it is putting lives at risk.<br />
<br />
The claims come just a day after a report revealed that a young person is now infected with <span class="caps">HIV</span> every 14 seconds. <br />
<br />
According to the United Nations Population Fund, around 6,000 people between the ages of 15 and 24 catch the virus every day.<br />
<br />
Half of all new infections are now in people under the age of 25 and most of these are young women living in the developing world.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
But according to Panorama, the Church is now telling people that condoms do not work.<br />
<br />
In an interview, one of the Vatican&#8217;s most senior cardinals Alfonso Lopez Trujillo suggested <span class="caps">HIV</span> could even pass through condoms.<br />
<br />
&#8220;The Aids virus is roughly 450 times smaller than the spermatozoon. The spermatozoon can easily pass through the &#8216;net&#8217; that is formed by the condom,&#8221; he says. <br />
<br />
 The cardinal, who is president of the Vatican&#8217;s Pontifical Council for the Family, suggests that governments should urge people not to use condoms.<br />
<br />
&#8220;These margins of uncertainty&#8230;should represent an obligation on the part of the health ministries and all these campaigns to act in the same way as they do with regard to cigarettes, which they state to be a danger.&#8221;<br />
<br />
The programme includes a Catholic nun advising her <span class="caps">HIV</span>-infected choir master not to use condoms with his wife because &#8220;the virus can pass through&#8221;.<br />
<br />
The Archbishop of Nairobi Raphael Ndingi Nzeki told Panaroma that condoms were helping to spread the virus.<br />
<br />
&#8220;Aids&#8230;has grown so fast because of the availability of condoms,&#8221; he said.<br />
<br />
In Kenya, one in five people are <span class="caps">HIV</span> positive. </blockquote>
