<p>I&#8217;ve seen 3 good shows with graphite drawings in Chelsea the last week.  Here is a short post with images and links before I head off to Savannah for the week.  Go see them all!</p>
<p><img alt="Eric_Beltz_Keep_the_Hammer_Busy_1336_86.jpg" src="/images/Eric_Beltz_Keep_the_Hammer_Busy_1336_86.jpg" width="488" height="600" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Eric Beltz, <em>Keep the Hammer Busy</em><br />
Graphite on paper<br />
11 &#215; 9 inches</p>
<p>Eric Beitz at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/8014">Morgan Lehman Gallery</a>.  Good old American weirdness.  A 2-person show with him and <a href="http://jameswagner.com/2007/06/sarah_peters_at.html">Sarah Peters</a> would be interesting.</p>

<p><img alt="rob-matthews-grandfather-ray.jpeg" src="/images/rob-matthews-grandfather-ray.jpeg" width="414" height="411" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Rob Matthews, <em>The Artist&#8217;s Grandfather (Ray)</em>, 2008<br />
graphite on paper<br />
7 inches diameter on 9&#215;9 inches paper</p>
<p><a href="http://www.matthewstheyounger.com/">Rob Matthews</a> at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/7671">Daniel Cooney Fine Art</a>.  Sweet small graphite drawings of family members holding objects that are important to them.  He was also in <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/5809">The Blogger Show</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="paolo_arao_daniel.jpg" src="/images/paolo_arao_daniel.jpg" width="400" height="530" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Paolo Arao, <em>Daniel</em>, 2008<br />
graphite on paper<br />
5.75 &#215; 4.375 inches</p>
<p><a href="http://www.paoloarao.com/">Paolo Arao</a> at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/8036">Jeff Bailey Gallery</a>.  A show with a sexy mix of big and intimate themes.  His <a href="http://www.baileygallery.com/exhibition_03.cfm?pg_art=6&amp;exh=574">Study for a Devotional Object</a> was probably my favorite work in the show, but it needs to be seen in person so I chose another image I liked for this post.</p>
