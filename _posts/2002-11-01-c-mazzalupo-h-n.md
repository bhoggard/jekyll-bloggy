<p>The parakeet is resting quietly in its cage.  Suggestions for what we should name it are welcome.  James&#8217;s take on the whole thing is <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/002931.html">here</a>.</p>
<p>We went to an opening at <a href="http://www.m3projects.org/">M3 Projects</a> in <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> for <a href="http://www.christinamazzalupo.com/">Christina Mazzalupo</a> and <a href="http://www.mixedgreens.com/mixedgreens/art_and_artists/artist_portfolio_template.jhtml?artistId=18">Harry Nolan</a>.  We have several works by Christina, and I&#8217;ve been watching Mr. Nolan since we first saw his work in a big Mixed Greens show in Williamsburg in 2001.  He was also in the first show at <a href="http://www.plusultragallery.com/index.html">Plus Ultra</a> (titled &#8220;Skank&#8221;), one of my favorite Williamsburg galleries.</p>
<p>If you go to the show, I highly recommend starting with Christina&#8217;s big wall of works on paper before you look at her paintings.  Those contain her ruminations on Atlantis, alchemy, and other myths &#8212; mostly from Internet sources &#8212; without which the paintings are a bit baffling.  Harry&#8217;s examinations of the imagery of war are quite powerful.  My favorite work is titled &#8220;Debriefing&#8221;, with images inspired by <a href="http://www.time.com/time/covers/1101020318/popup/">Operation Anaconda</a>.</p>
<p>My regular visits to <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> and Williamsburg make me feel that Manhattan is pricing itself out of being interesting.</p>
<p>After the opening, we went to dinner at one of my favorite restaurants, <a href="http://www.newyorkmetro.com/pages/details/8377.htm">Bistrot Margot</a>.  It&#8217;s the anti-Nolita restaurant &#8212; an oasis from the Mercedes <span class="caps">SUV</span>-driving trendoids that make that neighborhood so annoying.  As we walked back to the subway, we passed a former cyber cafe that I remember from when I worked in Soho in the late 90s.  It&#8217;s now empty, with a single payphone hanging on the wall.</p>
