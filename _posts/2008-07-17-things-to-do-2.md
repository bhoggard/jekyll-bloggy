<em>Tell Me</em>, 2006<br />
oil on canvas, 34 &#215; 36 inches</p>

<p>I haven&#8217;t been blogging about art because I find it difficult to look at art when it&#8217;s this hot outside.  The galleries I want to go to (as opposed to the behemoths with retail-quality a/c) can get pretty warm during these weather conditions.</p>
<p>Here are some recommended actions, regardless of the heat.  You should also check out what the other <a href="http://www.culturepundits.com/">Culture Pundits</a> bloggers are posting.</p>
<p>Visit Kendrick Mar&#8217;s <a href="http://www.kendrickmar.com/">website</a> to check out his art.  His artist statement says:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>My work is metaphorical self-portraiture that addresses childhood emotions and trauma. Issues surrounding memory, family dysfunction and being adopted manifest strongly in my work. Children&#8217;s books and television programs present a collective narrative in which grown-ups care for the best interests of their children. The pernicious disparity between this fiction and the brutal reality of childhood is the terrain I am most interested in exploring.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>I found him via the <a href="http://www.culturepundits.com/artist/list">Culture Pundits Artists</a> program.</p>
