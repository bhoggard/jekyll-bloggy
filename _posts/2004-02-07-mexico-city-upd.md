<p>I have made some minor edits to my Mexico City posts, to add names of places and artists that I hadn&#8217;t taken the time to figure out while we were there.</p>
<p>James has added captions to the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/mexico-city-2004">photo gallery</a> for the trip.</p>
<p>In the taxi on our way home from the airport on Wednesday night, James turned to me and said, &#8220;I guess we&#8217;re not on the &#8221;http://www.alternet.org/story.html?StoryID=14563&quot;&gt;no-fly</a> list!&quot;</p>
<p>I apologize for not being more interesting or analytical in my posts.  I think I did a better job when we were in Germany.  Several things were going on: We were both sick with cold/sinus things in Mexico City, and the 8,000 feet elevation and smog didn&#8217;t help.  The other was the exhaustion caused by sensory overload.  There is so much to see, hear, smell, and taste in a place like Mexico City.  That&#8217;s a good thing, but it meant I felt too worn out to write well after returning to the hotel each night.</p>
