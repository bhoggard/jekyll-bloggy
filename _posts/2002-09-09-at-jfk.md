<p>I&#8217;m writing notes in the business class lounge at <span class="caps">JFK</span> while we&#8217;re waiting for our flight to board.  Business class is much better than coach!  The lounge has free drinks &#8212; I&#8217;m drinking a very strong vodka tonic &#8212; and snacks.  It&#8217;s a weird crowd.  A lot of people are reading that  screeching conservative rag pretending to be a newspaper &#8212; the New York Post.  There is a TV loudly playing some <span class="caps">ESPN</span> show with highlights from previous <span class="caps">NFL</span> games, and there are cute German and Russian businessmen hooking up their laptops to the phones.  There is a certain amount of &#8220;privilege&#8221; in the attitude of the people in business class.  A lot of them have no qualms about talking as loudly as possible in their cell phones, even with strangers sitting right next to them.  These aren&#8217;t generally the people travelling on business &#8212; they&#8217;re people with enough miles or money to travel<br />
business class for pleasure trips.</p>
<p>Flyers seem to be dressing better.  Is it because summer is over?  Or are the tacky people more likely to be afraid to fly now?  I haven&#8217;t seen a single applique t-shirt.  Note to the Arkansans in the audience &#8212; I did see a Dillards suit bag!</p>
<p>The bookstore here in the terminal had a book about Flight 93 featured in the window.  Who would buy that to read on a plane?!</p>
