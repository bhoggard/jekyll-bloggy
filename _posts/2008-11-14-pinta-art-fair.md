<p>James and I attended the press preview and <span class="caps">VIP</span> reception tonight for <a href="http://www.pintaart.com/"><span class="caps">PINTA</span></a>, the modern / contemporary Latin American art fair..  Yes, they let press stay into the <span class="caps">VIP</span> period.</p>
<p>For someone like me who admires the modernism and geometric abstraction of Latin America in the 1950s and 1960s, there was a lot of great work from that period.  I&#8217;ve put up an image above of a wall from the booth of <a href="http://www.galeriaberenicearvani.com/">Galeria Berenice Arvani</a>, a São Paulo gallery.  Unfortunately their website does not seem to be loading tonight.</p>
<p>On the contemporary front, a new discovery tonight was the work of <a href="http://www.matiasduville.com/">Matías Duville</a>.  Here is a detail of the giant acrylic on particle board work in the booth of <a href="http://www.albertosendros.com/">Galeria Alberto Sendros</a>.  It&#8217;s related to <a href="http://www.matiasduville.com/impostor.htm">this piece</a> on the artist&#8217;s website.</p>

<p><img alt="matias-duville.jpg" src="/images/matias-duville.jpg" width="600" height="450" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Matías Duville (detail)</p>

<p>The artist creates works on unusual media and then distresses them in some way.  The booth across the aisle from Galeria Alberto Sendros was a Madrid gallery named <a href="http://www.distrito4.com/">Distrito Cu4tro</a> which had some works with ink on silk, where the artist had pulled the threads of the silk after drawing on the surface to distort his images.</p>
<p>Distrito Cu4tro also had some great work by Venezuelan artist <a href="http://www.distrito4.com/biografia.asp?Galeria=2&amp;Id=52">Alexander Apóstol</a> who now lives and works in Madrid.  In addition to large digital photographs of tall buildings in Venezuela with windows removed and graffiti added, there was a digital collage of personal ads from young Latin men (gay or otherwise) seeking American or European male &#8220;clients&#8221; to bring them to those richer continents.  There was also a video titled &#8220;AV. libertador&#8221; of transvestites talking to the camera claiming to be important Venezuelan artists such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gego">Gego</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armando_Rever%C3%B3n">Armando Reverón</a>.</p>
