<p>We saw the opening program of <a href="http://www.dtw.org/2003fall/central_station_03.htm">Central Station</a> &#8212; &#8220;a multi-week, multi-venue and multi-city program that will bring East/Central European work into the spotlight in many U.S. communities.&#8221;</p>
<p>The <a href="http://www.dtw.org/2003fall/shared_central_station_03.htm">evening</a> consisted of <i>La Sonnambula</i> by Galina Borissova (from Bulgaria), <i>Two</i> and <i>Stretching Thighs</i> by Márta Ladjánszki (Hungary), and <i>Serial Paradise</i> by Cosmin Manolescu.</p>
<div align="center"><img alt="galina_borissova_lg.jpg" src="/mt/archives/galina_borissova_lg.jpg" width="200" height="120" border="0" /><br />
<br />
Galina Borissova</div>
<p>The Borissova, danced by her as a solo, was an excerpt of a larger work.  It&#8217;s set to various sections of opera recordings, and mixes odd, fascinating movement, humor, and bits of sadness.  The entire work consists of solos by 14 women dancers, and I would love to see it.</p>
<div align="center"><img alt="cosmin_manolescu_lg.jpg" src="/mt/archives/cosmin_manolescu_lg.jpg" width="200" height="120" border="0" /><br />
<br />
Mircea Ghinea and Eduard Gabia in <i>Serial Paradise</i></div>
<p>The piece by Cosmin Manolescu consists of various excerpts from an hour-long work.  It includes some great dance making fun of machismo, the boy band phenomenon,</p>
<div align="center"><img alt="manolescu-boy-band.jpg" src="/mt/archives/manolescu-boy-band.jpg" width="320" height="240" border="0" /></div>
<p>and nationalism.  There is an amazing solo danced by Eduard Gabia, who wears earplugs to prevent hearing the Romanian folk music that plays as he dances.</p>
<p>Here is a <a href="http://revistarespiro.com/Issue7/teatru_ancarotescu.htm">page</a> with some more images from works by Manolescu.</p>
<p>The events take place at <span class="caps">DTW</span>, <a href="http://www.danspaceproject.org/">Danspace</a>, and <a href="http://www.ps122.org/">P.S.122</a>.  Go <a href="http://www.dtw.org/2003fall/central_station_03.htm">here</a> for more information.</p>
