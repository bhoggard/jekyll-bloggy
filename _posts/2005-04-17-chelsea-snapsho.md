<p>Shell casings on the floor &#8211; <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/571">Margeret Evangeline</a> at Stefan Stux.  Pick up a copy of the artist statement while you&#8217;re there. <p>Details of a scuplture and a wall piece &#8211; <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/591">Jim Drain</a> at Greene Naftali.  He is one of the founders of Forcefield, the first of the Providence, RI collectives to become famous.  Most people first saw their work at the 2002 Whitney Biennial, but the cognoscenti discovered them via Dean Daderko and his <a href="http://www.parlourprojects.org/">Parlour Projects</a> gallery.</p>

<p><img alt="bercowetz-bua-eye.jpg" src="/mt/archives/bercowetz-bua-eye.jpg" width="500" height="375" /></p>
<p>Jesse Bercowetz describes this as the &#8220;Uri Geller eye&#8221; from the sculpture he and Matt Bua did for a <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/606">group show</a> at Alona Kagan.  I couldn&#8217;t get a good photo of the live scorpion in the sculpture&#8217;s head.  It think their piece plus Jane Benson&#8217;s are the strongest of the show.  Their web site is <a href="http://www.overcoat.org/">here</a>.</p>
