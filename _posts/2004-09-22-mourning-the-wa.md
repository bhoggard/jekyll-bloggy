<p>A few days ago, I <a href="/2004/09/dead-gis-mother.html">wrote</a> about Sue Niederer, the mother of a young soldier killed in Iraq, who interrupted a speech by Laura Bush.  The New York Times has a <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2004/09/22/nyregion/22profile.html">feature</a> on her today.  It&#8217;s written by Chris Hedges, author of <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1586480499/tristanmedial-20">War Is a Force That Gives Us Meaning</a>  and who was <a href="http://www.alternet.org/story/15982">booed off the stage</a> when he tried to give an anti-war speech at a college graduation in May 2003.</p>
<blockquote>As Mrs. Bush was lauding her husband&#8217;s war on terror, Mrs. Niederer slipped on the shirt, which bore a photo of the lieutenant and the words &#8220;President Bush killed my son.&#8221; Standing at the back of the crowd, she interrupted Mrs. Bush, shouting that if the war was warranted, &#8220;Why don&#8217;t your children serve?&#8221;<br />
<br />
&#8220;She did not answer,&#8221; Mrs. Niederer said. &#8220;She looked stunned.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Suddenly, Mrs. Niederer recalled, she was surrounded by &#8220;men in dark suits with little earpieces&#8221; as well as angry Bush supporters. She was escorted from the hall, and as she tried to speak with reporters outside, she was arrested, handcuffed, taken to the Hamilton police station and charged with trespassing. She was released soon afterward, and prosecutors later dropped the charge. <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
&#8220;He had no training in bomb detection or in defusing bombs,&#8221; she said. &#8220;He did not have proper equipment. When I complained in public about the inadequate training and lack of equipment, the Army changed the story. They told me he was not trying to defuse a bomb. I still don&#8217;t know how he died. They won&#8217;t let me speak to or contact members of his platoon.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Asked about her accusations, Lt. Col. Paul Fitzpatrick, a spokesman for the 10th Mountain Division and Fort Drum, said in a statement yesterday that Lieutenant Dvorin &#8220;was well trained and fully integrated into his unit,&#8221; and that he &#8220;died leading his soldiers in combat.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Outside her office, Mrs. Niederer sat, swinging from anger to tears and back again, as low storm clouds cast a pall over the parking lot. &#8220;My goal is to bring the troops home as quickly as possible,&#8221; she said. &#8220;This was Seth&#8217;s wish. I can&#8217;t save my son, but I can save someone else&#8217;s son. Seth&#8217;s mission is mine.&#8221;</blockquote>
