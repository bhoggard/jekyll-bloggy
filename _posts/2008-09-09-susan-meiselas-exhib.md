<p>The second paragraph of Susan Meiselas&#8217;s bio on her <a href="http://www.susanmeiselas.com/content.php?sec=bc">website</a> states:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Meiselas joined Magnum Photos in 1976 and has worked as a freelance photographer since then. She is best known for her coverage of the insurrection in Nicaragua and her documentation of human rights issues in Latin America, which were published widely throughout the world. In 1981, Pantheon published her second monograph, <span class="caps">NICARAGUA</span>, <span class="caps">JUNE</span> 1978-<span class="caps">JULY</span> 1979.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In addition to being the author of the iconic photograph above, she is also known to many of us as the person who <a href="http://www.firstpulseprojects.net/joywar.html">sent her lawyer</a> after Joy Garnett accusing her of &#8220;pirating&#8221; the photo when Joy created the painting titled <em>Molotov</em> seen in detail above.</p>
<p>Given Meiselas&#8217;s progressive (other than on copyright issues) history, I was surprised when I saw this on the page for her <a href="http://www.icp.org/site/c.dnJGKJNsFqG/b.4391347/">upcoming exhibition</a> at the International Center of Photography:</p>
