<p>After seeing an <a href="http://www.wired.com/news/conflict/0,2100,58043,00.html">article</a> in Wired, I added <a href="http://www.back-to-iraq.com/">Back to Iraq 2.0</a> to my blog links.</p>
<blockquote> Sporting a cameraman&#8217;s vest and lugging a satellite phone, Christopher Allbritton may be no match for heavy artillery. But he&#8217;s apparently got enough guts to be the Web&#8217;s first independent war correspondent.<br />
<br />
Allbritton, a former New York Daily News reporter living in the East Village, plans to file stories directly to his weblog, Back to Iraq 2.0, next month as part of an independent news-gathering expedition to Iraq. <br />
<br />
 Allbritton says he wants to cover the humanitarian effects the likely U.S.-Iraq war will have on civilians in Iraqi Kurdistan, which is protected by a U.S.-imposed no-fly zone over northern Iraq.<br />
<br />
While &#8220;embedded&#8221; reporters with backing from major news outlets bump along on prearranged Hummer rides and report what they see in the mainstream media, Allbritton will hitchhike and bribe his way through an area that could become the most dangerous place in Iraq outside Baghdad. </blockquote>
