<p>We went to the <a href="http://www.divafair.com/general.html">DiVA Art Fair</a> today, and it was definitely the most pleasant experience of all of my art-going this weekend.  It&#8217;s at the Embassy Suites downtown on two floors, with a big atrium, so there is a nice feeling of openness and light as one wanders from room to room.  I think it&#8217;s also a result of the pace of viewing video works.  It&#8217;s harder to just run from space to space.  One spends a minute or two seeing if a work is interesting, and then staying longer if it seems to be.  Having plenty of chances to sit and look at work in dimmed lighting is a welcome change from the Armory and Scope experience.  I found it intereresting that the majority of the galleries are from foreign countries.  Also, an art fair featuring time-based work that gives out tickets that allow one to return on following days is a great idea.</p>
<p>My top picks, in somewhat random order:</p>
<ul>
	<li>All of the video work by <a href="http://yukikokawase.free.fr/MatthewLutzKinoy.htm">Matthew Lutz-Kinoy</a> at Paris-based Yukiko Kawase &#8211; quirky, funny, and brilliant work by a 20-year-old(!) artist</li>
</ul>
<ul>
	<li>Jill Miller&#8217;s <em>I am making art too</em> at <a href="http://annebarrault.free.fr/">Galerie Anne Barrault</a>.  The artist received permission (described as &#8220;enthusiastic&#8221; in the press release) from John Baldessari to create a new work based on his 1971 performance video titled <em>I am making art</em>.  The resulting video, mixing her performance with that of Baldessari, all set to Missy Elliot&#8217;s <em>Work It</em> is both funny and brilliant.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
	<li>Patrick Jolley&#8217;s <em>Here After</em> (2004) at Prato-based <a href="http://www.nicolafornello.com/">Nicola Fornello</a>.  Yes, that&#8217;s edgy video work from a gallery in a town outside of Florence.  Can you imagine such a thing in Westchester?  We last saw Jolley&#8217;s work when we saw <a href="http://www.brooklynart.com/artists/reynoldsjolley/default.htm"><em>Burn</em></a> in a group show at the Chelsea Art Museum.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
	<li><a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004716.html">Steffan Saffer</a>, Susanne Weirich, and Christoph Draeger at <a href="http://www.mullerdechiara.com/">Müller DeChiara</a></li>
</ul>
<ul>
	<li>Federico Solmi at Boreas &#8212; see <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004708.html">James</a> for more info on the artist</li>
</ul>
<ul>
	<li>Corinna Schnitt&#8217;s video <em>Living a Beautiful Life</em> at <a href="http://www.galeriefricke.de/">Galerie M+R Fricke</a>.  The artist interviewed adolescents about their idea of happiness and filmed this work about idealised lives at a luxury home in Beverly Hills.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
	<li>Robert Boyd&#8217;s <em>Xanadu</em> at <a href="http://www.lmakprojects.com/"><span class="caps">LMAK</span> Projects</a>.  We first saw it at White Box, and it gets better upon each viewing.</li>
</ul>
<ul>

</ul>
<ul>
	<li>Carolee Schneemann at <a href="http://www.elgawimmer.com/">Elga Wimmer</a></li>
</ul>
<ul>

</ul>
