<p>Not only does Ireland allow homos to march in their St. Patrick&#8217;s Day parades (unlike the one in <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/Print/0,3858,4625168,00.html"><span class="caps">NYC</span></a>), the Irish government is now warning priests that they are <a href="http://www.365gay.com/NewsContent/080203irishPriests.htm">subject to prosecution</a> if they distribute the Vatican&#8217;s anti-gay marriage document:</p>
<blockquote> (Dublin)  Priests and bishops are being warned by the Irish government that they face charges if they distribute the Vatican&#8217;s denouncement of gay marriage.<br />
<br />
The Irish Council for Civil Liberties (<span class="caps">ICCL</span>) said Friday that priests who quote sections of the document, hand it out, or send it to politicians or other citizens could be prosecuted under Ireland&#8217;s strict incitement to hatred legislation. <br />
<br />
The 12 page document released this week in seven languages describes gay marriage as &#8220;evil&#8221; and says &#8220;legal recognition of homosexual unions or placing them on the same level as marriage would mean not only the approval of deviant behavior.&#8221; It also says that &quot;Allowing children to be adopted by persons living in such unions would actually mean doing violence to these children. </blockquote>
<p>That reminds me of something else.  This document is coming from the Vatican&#8217;s &#8220;Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith&#8221;.  I think all articles referring to this office need to refer to its previous name: <a href="http://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congregation_for_the_Doctrine_of_the_Faith">The Roman Inquisition</a>.</p>
