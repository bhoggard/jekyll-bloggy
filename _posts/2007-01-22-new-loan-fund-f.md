<p><a href="http://www.newyorkbusiness.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=200770122011"><span class="caps">NYC</span> Investment Fund offers arts loans</a></p>
<blockquote>The New York City Investment Fund, an affiliate of the Partnership for New York City, said Monday that it has teamed up with the state Arts Council to launch a new $2 million loan program.<br />
<br />
The initiative &#8212; dubbed the Arts Entrepreneurial Loan Fund &#8212; aims to fund arts and nonprofit groups, including dance troupes, art, music and theater groups and other art-related programs, throughout the five boroughs.<br />
<br />
The loans, which will range from $25,000 to $500,000, will provide funding aimed at lowering the nonprofit&#8217;s overhead.<br />
<br />
&#8220;Arts groups frequently contribute to revitalization of the city&#8217;s most blighted neighborhoods, but this sector is threatened by rising costs and growing competition for scarce philanthropic dollars,&#8221; said Maria Gotsch, co-chief executive and co-president of the $100 million New York City Investment Fund.<br />
