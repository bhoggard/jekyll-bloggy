<p>I found it sad, knowing that people like Muhammad Ali are there, to read that people <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/07/05/sports/05cnd-hillary.html?ei=5088&amp;en=e4d7c34db8c1687d&amp;ex=1278216000&amp;partner=rssnyt&amp;emc=rss&amp;pagewanted=print">went wild over</a> Hillary Clinton&#8217;s &#8220;star power.&#8221;  I was more than sad &#8212; horrified was more like it &#8212; to learn that one of the other people in Singapore to lobby  for <span class="caps">NYC</span> to host the Olympics is Henry Kissinger.  I guess it&#8217;s one of the few places outside of the <span class="caps">USA</span> he can still visit without risk of arrest.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t want the Olympics here, and obviously no thinking person does either if they have that man representing us.  I agree with <a href="http://fromthefloor.blogspot.com/2005/05/match-made-in-not-heaven.html">Todd Gibson</a> on what hosting them would mean:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>a month of lockdown and police-state presence in 2012 that will make last summer&#8217;s Republican Convention look like a fire drill.</p>
</blockquote>
