<p>If you&#8217;re reading this right now in Los Angeles, you can head on over to Carrie Marill&#8217;s show opening tonight at <a href="http://www.sixspace.com/gallery/marill2007/pressrelease.php">sixspace</a>. Caryn Coleman of sixpace was cool and together enough to be IMing with me right before the opening, and sent me a link to some images.  Having just watched a <span class="caps">PBS</span> <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wnet/americanmasters/database/audubon_j_gallery4.html">show</a> about John James Audobon, I was particularly struck by these beautiful images of &#8220;gouache-on-paper paintings depicting threatened or endangered flora and fauna existing in an imaginary world.&#8221;</p>
<p>I was not surprised to see <a href="http://www.pbs.org/art21/artists/ford/index.html">Walton Ford</a> interviewed on the TV show about Audobon.</p>
