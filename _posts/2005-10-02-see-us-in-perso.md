<p>The lovely and talented <a href="http://www.janetphelps.com/">Janet Phelps</a> has asked us to be part of a panel on collecting at the <a href="http://www.aafnyc.com/">Affordable Art Fair</a> on October 29th.  Here is the info I have from her:</p>
<p>The panelists are as follows:</p>
<p>Jeremy Adams, Gallery Director, Cue Art Foundation<br />
Joshua Mack, New American Paintings<br />
Stacey Gershon, Curator, JP Morgan Chase Art Collection<br />
James Wagner and Barry Hoggard, collectors, write separate art-related blogs and together maintain an online visual arts calendar.<br />
Robert Goff or Cassie Rosenthal, Directors, Goff Rosenthal</p>
<p>We don&#8217;t know yet which of the Goff Rosenthal people will attend.</p>
<p>This panel of collectors, dealers, business men and women, artists, curators, advisors and critics will discuss collecting and how their careers and career choices relate to the way they collect, what they collect and how they collect. The panelists will try to put a human face to the often overwhelming prospect of collecting.  Moderated by Franklin Sirmans, Curator and Writer; with Jeremy Adams, Director of Cue Art Foundation; Joshua Mack of New American Painting; Stacey Gershon, Curator at JPMorgan Chase; James Wagner and Barry Hogard, Collectors, Activists, and Critics; and Robert Goff or Cassie Rosenthal, Co-Directors of Goff Rosenthal.</p>
<p>Details for the where and when:<br />
The Art of Collecting: 2:30 on Saturday October 29th at Pier 92. It will last about 1 1/2 to 2 hours tops.</p>
