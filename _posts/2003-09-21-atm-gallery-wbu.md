<p>On Friday we stopped by <a href="http://www.atmgallery.com/"><span class="caps">ATM</span> Gallery on Avenue B</a> for the opening of <a href="http://www.shoplifter.us/">Hrafnhildur Arnardóttir</a>.  There was a bit more buzz than one usually sees at an East Village opening these days &#8212; <a href="http://www.bjork.com/">Björk</a> was there, supporting her fellow Icelander.  She hung out in the gallery and on Avenue B for the entire time I was there, so she was probably there for most of the opening.  I love NY!  She can hang out like that without being accosted, which would be more likely to happen further uptown, or further west in Manhattan.</p>
<p>Then we hopped on the L to go to Williamsburg to check out the <a href="/2003/09/chris-caccamise.html">Third Friday</a> happenings.  One thing about the <a href="http://www.plusultragallery.com/">Plus Ultra</a> show &#8212; the cartoons by <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/redirect?tag=tristanmedial-20&path=search-handle-url/index%3Dbooks%26field-author%3DWill%2520Self">Will Self</a> were surprisingly good.</p>
<p>The highlights of the evening were to be found at  <a href="http://www.brooklynfireproof.com/">Brooklyn Fire Proof</a> and <a href="http://www.open-ground.org/">Open Ground</a>.  The former is morphing into a gallery space in addition to being artists studios.  They showed some great work, and had a good band with videos and people dancing in costumes.  They&#8217;re a little &#8220;at the edge&#8221; geographically, but that&#8217;s changing &#8212; witness the location of The Morning News <a href="http://www.themorningnews.org/party/">party</a>.  I can&#8217;t find anything about her via Google, but there were some nice works on paper by Stine Hedegaard Andersen, and I got a chance to meet <a href="http://www.brooklynfireproof.com/artists/ViewArtist.asp?ID=27">Steven Baines</a>, whose work I had seen recently at <a href="http://www.whitecolumns.org/wr.baines/gallery.html">White Columns</a>.</p>
<p>The other highlight was the <a href="http://www.open-ground.org/wiki/InSecurity">(In)Security</a> show at Open Ground.  Approriately for a collaborative gallery space, they have a <a href="http://c2.com/cgi/wiki">Wiki</a> for a web site!</p>
<p>One of the things from the show is available for download from the artist,  <a href="http://www.famousmime.com/">Rebecca Ross</a>&#8216;s web site.  It&#8217;s called &#8220;The Okay News&#8221; and is described thusly:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>The Okay News<br />
memory resident Mac <span class="caps">OSX</span> application</p>
<p>A re-formed newspaper that runs in the background of everyday computer use. Every twenty minutes OkayNews delivers a headline from that day&#8217;s New York Times to an operating system warning box with the single button &#8220;Okay&#8221;, which must be pressed to continue using the computer. </blockquote></p>
<p>I think the &#8220;Third Friday&#8221; event was a big success.  There were a lot of people at every gallery I visited, including neighborhood people that asked questions as if they didn&#8217;t normally go to galleries.  As we walked around, we encountered quite a few people who were asking for directions, or asked where to get a copy of the gallery map.</p>
