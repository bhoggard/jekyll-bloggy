<p>We had a good day in Chelsea on Saturday.  I told a gallerist that we had seen no duds that day, and she handed me a pink highlighter to &#8220;mark my calendar!&#8221;</p>
<p>The shows:</p>
<p>Richard Aldrich, Kamrooz Aram, Amy Granat and Van Hanos at <a href="http://www.5begallery.com/">Oliver Kamm 5BE</a>.  The installation in the front mixing film/sound work (using scratched film) by Granat, combined with collage/works on paper and sculpture by Aldrich, is one of my favorite rooms in months.  The combination of paintings and works on paper by Aram and Hanos in the &#8220;main&#8221; room is also wonderful.</p>
<p>Nancy de Holl and Kalup Linzy at <a href="http://www.taxterandspengemann.com/">Taxter &amp; Spengemann</a>.  de Holl does photos (printed in a way similar to iris prints) of still lifes that are so brilliantly lit and arranged that I was sure they had paint on top of the photos.  Linzy has several videos in the back that had us watching for a while, laughing much of the time.  I think he is one to watch.  We first saw his work in an Andrew Guenther-curated show at Capsule (see <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004533.html">James</a>).</p>
<p>Robert Gober at <a href="http://www.matthewmarks.com/">Matthew Marks</a>. As James said, I felt like I was in church, but this time I mean it in a good way.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.potrc.org/">Marjetica Potr&#269;</a> at <a href="http://www.maxprotetch.com/">Max Protetch</a>.  Her drawings (including wall drawings) commenting on the social aspects &#8212; for good and evil &#8212; of architecture, are moving and beautiful to look at.  Just ignore what the <em>NY Times</em> had to say.  I think they were disappointed that there are no sculptures in this show.</p>

<p><em>Stranger Town</em> at <a href="http://www.dinterfineart.com/">Dinter Fine Art</a>.  This Taylor McKimens-curated show of &#8220;eight successful artists who have each risen to prominence in areas outside the walls of the established art world&#8221; includes musicians, successful commercial graphic artists, and lots of good drawing plus some video.</p>
<p>Ben Jones at <a href="http://www.foxyproduction.com/">Foxy Production</a>.  Ben is a member of Paper Rad, and this is his first solo show.  I think the solo format has provided him with a chance to show the formalist/conceptual aspects of his work in a way that&#8217;s rather difficult when doing the collaborative shows.  I look forward to the future solo shows by other members at Foxy.</p>

<p>Lastly, I loved the Mary Heilman show at <a href="http://www.303gallery.com/">303 Gallery</a>, but didn&#8217;t like the &#8220;no photos allowed!&#8221; policy or the fact they don&#8217;t send out e-mail announcements.  They have decent images on their web site of individual works, but sometimes one wants to show the interesting placement of two images.  Check out the image of <em>Heaven</em> on the site.  That&#8217;s the one everyone sees and says, &#8220;Is that one of her paintings?&#8221;</p>
