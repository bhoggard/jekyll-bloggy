<p>On Friday night we saw <a href="http://www.thecivilians.org/shows.html#gonemissing">&#8220;Gone Missing&#8221;</a> by The Civilians.  James provided a <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003447.html">preview</a> in July inspired by an article in the NY Times about Steve Cosson, the artistic director.</p>
<p>&#8220;Gone Missing&#8221; is a theater work, with original songs by Michael Friedman, about things lost and found.  It&#8217;s very clever, and manages to go from silly songs and stories to very profound ones about loss and memory.  I think the way it is wrapped up by the end is pretty brilliant, all the more so from being a collaborative project rather than a work by a single smart playwright.  To give you a sense of what the songs are like, here are MP3s of two of the songs from the show.  I don&#8217;t think the CD is as good as they are live, in case you&#8217;re wondering.</p>
<ul><li><a href="https://bloggy.com/mp3/The_Civilians-Lost_Horizon.mp3">Lost Horizon</a> &#8211; sung by Trey Lyford, a kind of mini-epic</li>
<li><a href="https://bloggy.com/mp3/The_Civilians-Etch_A_Sketch.mp3">Etch A Sketch</a> &#8211; a somewhat silly song sung by the brilliant Jennifer Morris.  I have talked with her about it, and she admits she is more of an actress than a singer, so she is lucky to be working with a composer who can write for her.  Live, she is such a charismatic performer you don&#8217;t think about whether she is a great singer, and she pulls it off.  Actually, listening again to the recording &#8212; she sounds great on the CD.  How can you go wrong with a song that begins, &#8220;Once I was a Lockean tabula rasa&#8221;?</li>
</ul>
<p>I would like to add one more <a href="https://bloggy.com/mp3/The_Civilians-La_Canaille.mp3">MP3</a> &#8211; of the French song &#8220;La Canaille&#8221; (English translation and arrangement by Michael Friedman) from their show <a href="http://www.thecivilians.org/shows.html#pariscommune">&#8220;Paris Commune&#8221;</a>.   I talked about it <a href="/2003/03/paris-commune.html">here</a>.  This performance is by a lovely young woman named Quincy Tyler Bernstine.  I sat next to her at Joe&#8217;s Pub a few months ago and blurted out &#8220;I love you!&#8221; when I recognized her.</p>
<p>There is a nice <a href="http://www.thebrooklynrail.org/theater/oct03/disobedience.html">article</a> in the latest Brooklyn Rail about them and &#8220;Gone Missing.&#8221;</p>
<p>I want to point out to those reading this that I&#8217;m normally not a fan of musicals, so I rarely recommend musical theater.  The Civilians produce very out of the ordinary works.</p>
<p>The show runs through November 2 at the <a href="http://www.smarttix.com/index.cfm?showcode=GON&eventtype=Show">Belt Theater</a> on West 37th Street.</p>
