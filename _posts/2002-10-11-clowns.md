<p>This one&#8217;s for <a href="http://glennalicious.blogspot.com/">Glenn</a>: an <a href="http://www.robertbermangallery.com/Pages/clowns/clowns_main_003.html">exhibit</a> of amateur paintings of clowns from the collection of Robert Berman and <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Keaton,+Diane">Diane Keaton</a>.</p>
<p>Speaking of Ms. Keaton, I recently watched <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Title?0079522">Manhattan</a> for the first time since I moved to <span class="caps">NYC</span> in 1989.  It&#8217;s such a wonderful appreciation of New York.  Now that I&#8217;ve lived here for years, I recognize so much, and I know where they are when they&#8217;re in that gallery building on West Broadway where Castelli, Sonnabend, etc. were located.  I would not have laughed as much at the &#8220;oh, you liked that?&#8221; conversation when Diane Keaton first appears.  I don&#8217;t think I knew who <a href="http://directory.google.com/Top/Arts/Photography/Photographers/Masters/Arbus,_Diane/?il=1">Diane Arbus</a> even was when I first saw it.  I still remember laughing at the angst over pronouncing &#8220;Van Gogh&#8221; when I first saw it in college.</p>
