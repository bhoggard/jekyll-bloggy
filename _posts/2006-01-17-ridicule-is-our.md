<p>Voltaire was 250 years ahead of me.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m currently reading a new biography of Voltaire, titled <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1582346305/tristanmedial-20"><em>Voltaire Almighty</em></a>, by Roger Pearson.  In the discussion of his writing <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0393960587/tristanmedial-20"><em>Candide</em></a>,  he says something that I have said about dealing with our appalling government.  Quoting from <em>Voltaire Almighty</em>, p. 261:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>The French &#8212; and not only the French <del>- were much more likely to condemn the horrors of war and religious intolerance if these were made to seem absurd.  Don&#8217;t rant and rail: just show people what&#8217;s dumb.  Horrors and injustices are always someone else&#8217;s problem, but none of us wants to look foolish.  As he told Damilaville, a fellow</del><em>philosophe</em>, ten years later: &#8220;I have only ever addressed one prayer to God, and it is very short: &#8216;My God, please make all our enemies ridiculous.&#8217;  God has granted my wish.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
<p>I wish we had the option of engaging in electoral politics to fix the mess we&#8217;re in, but I don&#8217;t see that happening.</p>
<p>We live in a country where a senator named Rockefeller <a href="http://www.dailykos.com/storyonly/2005/12/21/143035/30">kept quiet</a> when he learned of secret, warrantless wiretaps of American citizens.  If he can&#8217;t oppose this President, who can?</p>
<p>We live in a country where the Senate continues its normal business as we learn of how this administration lied about Iraq, about Abu Ghraib, and about wiretaps without a warrant.</p>
<p>We live in an era when the Democratic party has &#8220;leaders&#8221; such as Hillary Clinton who thinks the solution to Iraq is more troops, or Joe Lieberman who hasn&#8217;t announced how he&#8217;ll vote on Alito.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not lifting a finger to help the Democrats take back Congress if a &#8220;victory&#8221; means those people, who voted for the Patriot Act and the Iraq War, are in charge.</p>
<p>If pro-torture Alberto Gonzales as Attorney General, or a <a href="http://www.savethecourt.org/site/c.mwK0JbNTJrF/b.849267/k.CC39/Home.htm">Supreme Court Justice Alito</a> aren&#8217;t a good enough reason to filibuster, then the Democrats are almost as guilty as the <span class="caps">GOP</span>.  They have failed us.</p>
<p>Ridicule them all.</p>
