<p>I went to see <a href="http://www.andyschest.com/">Andy</a>&#8216;s show <a href="http://www.andyschest.com/pottymouth/current.html">Potty Mouth</a> last night (or is it early this morning).  As we waited outside to be let in, there was an adorable little dark-haired kid playing on the sidewalk.  When some air-conditioner condensation &#8212; I hope that&#8217;s what it was &#8212; started pouring off the awning above our heads, he jumped into it and got all wet.  He then proceeded to try to embrace all of the gay guys in line.  A future performance artist is born!</p>
<p>It was <span class="caps">GREAT</span>.  I had seen part of it &#8220;in process&#8221; at <a href="http://www.dixonplace.org/">Dixon Place</a>, but that was months ago, and I think it has really come together into a fabulous raunchy, moving, hilarious piece of one-man theater.  It runs through October 25.  Go!</p>
<p>I am still so jet-lagged.  I got my second wind around 2am, and went with some of my fellow bloggers/audience members to <a href="http://newyork.citysearch.com/profile/33069911/">Lolita</a>.  I liked the vibe of the place &#8212; low key (and cute) bartenders, and a crowd that was about half straight and half gay.  Not enough places pull that off successfully in <span class="caps">NYC</span>, even on the Lower East Side.   There were gorgeous photographs of the abandoned buildings at Ellis Island in the back.  The even had <a href="http://www.brooklynbrewery.com/beer_weisse.html">Brooklyn Weisse</a> on tap!</p>
<p>Do I have to list all of the bloggers that were there and link to them here?  I&#8217;m tired.  The one person I hadn&#8217;t met before, at least not in &#8220;the real world&#8221;, was <a href="http://everythingbut.org/" title="he has a list of attendees">Mark</a>.  At one point, when Andy talked about gay men with cats, we both groaned simultaneously &#8212; getting a reaction from our performer.</p>
<p>For those that missed it, e.g. <a href="http://glennalicious.blogspot.com/">Glenn</a> and <a href="http://www.ultrasparky.org/">Sparky</a>, <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> was sick and didn&#8217;t make it either.  We might go see it on October 18 at 10pm if anyone wants to join us.</p>
