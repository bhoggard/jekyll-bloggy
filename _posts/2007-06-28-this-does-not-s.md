<p><a href="http://www.amny.com/news/local/crime/am-copclass0628,0,4519805.story">Smaller <span class="caps">NYPD</span> class raises fears</a>, <em>AM New York</em> (emphasis mine below)</p>
<blockquote>Researchers and city officials Wednesday wondered if an increasingly stretched police force can continue to keep crime rates down on the same day that the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> fell far short in its goal of 2,800 recruits at cadet graduation.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
While 1,097 cadets graduated Wednesday, the department is 1,828 short of the number of officers it is allowed to hire this year, an <span class="caps">NYPD</span> spokesman said. The force has steadily declined as veteran officers retire or leave the city to work in higher-paying areas.<br />
<br />
Critics have charged that the city is unable to attract enough recruits because <span class="caps">NYPD</span> salary levels are not competitive with those in the suburbs and New Jersey. <strong>Police academy recruits start at $25,100, a rate that&#8217;s lower than newly hired sanitation workers, Central Park gardeners and plumbing inspectors. </strong>Top pay maxes out at $59,588.</blockquote>
