<p>James and I ventured up to midtown on Tuesday to see several shows.  The highlights:</p>
<p><img alt="pablo-bronstein-theatre.jpg" src="/mt/archives/pablo-bronstein-theatre.jpg" width="450" height="401" /></p>
<p>Pablo Bronstein, <em>Theatre</em>, 2005, ink, gouache/paper, 16&quot; by 18&quot; [image from gallery website]</p>
<p><em>I Love My Scene: Scene 1_, curated by Jose Freire of Team Gallery at <a href="http://www.maryboonegallery.com/exhibitions/2005-2006/scene1/index.html">Mary Boone</a>.  I&#8217;m happy to see the gallery pulling in some interesting outside curators to do shows.  It&#8217;s an intriguing mix including photography (Weegee and Cecil Beaton), sculpture (Keith Sonnier, Lothar Hempel, and Banks Violette), and drawing by Pablo Bronstein.  I hadn&#8217;t heard of him before, but his work appears to be drawings for fantastic stage sets.  Google tells me that <a href="http://www.saatchi-gallery.co.uk/artists/pablo">Saatchi</a></em>bronstein.htm owns some works by him.</p>

<p><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/103551594/"><img src="https://static.flickr.com/40/103551594_8bf0aa44b9.jpg" width="500" height="375" alt="William Kentridge at Marian Goodman" /></a></p>
<p>William Kentridge&#8217;s drawings and designs for a production of Mozart&#8217;s <em>Magic Flute</em> at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/3/1764">Marian Goodman</a> were, pardon the expression, enchanting.</p>

<p><a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/103551749/"><img src="https://static.flickr.com/28/103551749_0f72d34d3a.jpg" width="500" height="375" alt="Eija-Liisa Ahtila at Marian Goodman" /></a></p>
<p>Eija-Liisa Ahtila&#8217;s 4-screen work <em>The Hour of Prayer</em> was a nice discovery in the back at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/3/1950">Marian Goodman</a> after visiting the Kentridge show.  There are more images of both Kentridge and Ahtila&#8217;s work in my <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/sets/72057594049484418/"><span class="caps">NYC</span> Art 2006 set</a> on Flickr.</p>

<p><img src="http://www.artcal.net/static/artcal-images/0/700.jpeg" alt="Tim Davis White Tiger" /></p>
<p>Tim Davis, <em>White Tiger</em>, 2005, c-print, 35&quot; × 44&quot; [image from gallery website]</p>
<p>I love everything Tim Davis does, and this show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/3/1833">Greenberg Van Doren</a> is no exception.  In some ways it is like Roe Ethridge&#8217;s last show at Andrew Kreps &#8212; it feels like a set of images the artist just likes.  If you&#8217;re a photographer like one of those, that&#8217;s good enough for me.</p>
