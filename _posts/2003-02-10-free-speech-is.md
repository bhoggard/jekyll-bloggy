<p>A federal judge <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/ny-demo0210,0,661385.story?coll=nyc%2Dtopheadlines%2Dleft">has denied</a> a permit allowing the <a href="http://www.unitedforpeace.org/">anti-war march</a> on February 15 to march to the U.N., saying that heightened security concerns posed by up to 100,000 protesters would threaten the public safety and security of the U.N.  Instead a <em>stationary rally</em> will be allowed five blocks away.  The judge says it is constitutional to ban all marching in Mahattan.</p>
<p>NY Newsday <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/ny-nyplaz103124339feb10,0,5554038.column?coll=nyc%2Dtopheadlines%2Dleft">also reports</a> that the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> has approved <em>no</em> permits for demonstrations since the fall of 2002.</p>
<p>Apparently things like the St. Patrick&#8217;s Day Parade don&#8217;t have the same kinds of security concerns that political speech does.</p>

If you&#8217;re going to be there, send me an email if you don&#8217;t have already have my cell phone number, so we can meet up.</p>
