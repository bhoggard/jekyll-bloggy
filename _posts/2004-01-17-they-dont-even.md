<p>The Bush regime doesn&#8217;t even have to pretend to care about appearances any more.  Americans don&#8217;t seem to care.</p>
<p>From the <a href="http://www.latimes.com/news/nationworld/nation/la-na-ducks17jan17,1,7777211.story?coll=la-home-headlines">LA Times</a>:</p>
<blockquote>Vice President Dick Cheney and Supreme Court Justice Antonin Scalia spent part of last week duck hunting together at a private camp in southern Louisiana just three weeks after the court agreed to take up the vice president&#8217;s appeal in lawsuits over his handling of the administration&#8217;s energy task force.<br />
<br />
While Scalia and Cheney are avid hunters and longtime friends, several experts in legal ethics questioned the timing of their trip and said it raised doubts about Scalia&#8217;s ability to judge the case impartially.<br />
<br />
But Scalia rejected that concern Friday, saying, &#8220;I do not think my impartiality could reasonably be questioned.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Federal law says &#8220;any justice or judge shall disqualify himself in any proceeding in which his impartiality might be questioned.&#8221; For nearly three years, Cheney has been fighting demands that he reveal whether he met with energy industry officials, including Kenneth L. Lay when he was chairman of Enron, while he was formulating the president&#8217;s energy policy. </blockquote>
