<p>I don&#8217;t feel so hot, so posting is limited at the moment.  Here is an <a href="http://www.brooklynrail.org/theater/feb04/gibson.html">interview</a> with Melissa James Gibson, the playwright who wrote <i>[sic]</i> (which we loved) and now <i>Suitcase</i>, both produced at <a href="http://www.sohorep.org/">Soho Rep</a>.  We saw it last week and really enjoyed it, as did the rather actorly (Nina Hellman of The Civilans, David Greenspan, etc.) audience.  Apparently, I should have written about this earlier, as tickets just went from $15 to $35.  Still, it&#8217;s probably worth going, and certainly is more worth your time than any Broadway crap.</p>
<p>There is an amazing, weird, disjointed quality to the dialogue in her plays.  I had thought it was the way they are directed, but now that I&#8217;ve read the interview and seen an excerpt of the latest play, I realize that&#8217;s the way they&#8217;re written.</p>
