<p>I love <a href="http://www.gavinbrown.biz/artists/view/elizabeth-peyton">Elizabeth Peyton</a> and <a href="http://jameswagner.com/2008/08/mary_heilmann_at_wac.html">Mary Heilmann</a> as much as anyone, but don&#8217;t those seem rather un-new-ish choices for big shows at an institution called <a href="http://www.newmuseum.org/">The New Museum</a>?  Perhaps some arrangement could be made to send those to <a href="http://www.moma.org/">MoMA</a> in place of their upcoming Miró and Van Gogh shows?</p>
<p>Meanwhile, a 20-year survey of the work of <a href="http://www.e-flux.com/shows/view/5142">Lyle Ashton Harris</a> is only going to appear in Scottsdale, AZ, and Buffalo, NY.  Is there really no room at any New York area institution for such an exhibition?</p>
<p>And why does Lyle&#8217;s <a href="http://www.lyleashtonharris.com/">website</a> just have a flash slide show and no other information on the artist?</p>
