<p><a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/378">Claudia Weber</a> at Momenta.  Quoting from the press release:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Claudia Weber&#8217;s work presented at Momenta began with a photograph that the artist took at a construction site. The photograph, optically stark and ostensibly formal, records the transformation that took place when a classical balustrade received an unceremonious backdrop of a sheet of particle-board. Riffing off of this image, the artist re-contextualizes two other cultural forms from vastly different sources: a pre-fabricated bookcase and a heavily reproduced painting by the German Romantic Caspar David Friedrich.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>She uses simple materials like Styrofoam, aluminum foil, wood, and wire to create these sculptural installations.  We talked with a friend along for the ride about the use of such materials in sculpture.  As we walked around a rapidly changing neighborhood like Williamsburg, we saw bits of these same materials scattered about, but to create a moving work of art out of them seems more of a leap than what is accomplished using a medium like paint or marble.  I&#8217;m not arguing that this makes her more of an artist, but I think people are more prepared to view a painting as &#8220;art&#8221; than they are an installation of what appears to be torn up construction detritus.  I loved it.</p>
<p>At <a href="http://www.31grand.com/">31 Grand</a>, my favorite work was actually in the back &#8212; some drawings by Carol &#8220;Riot&#8221; Kane, and a couple of collaged soldiers by <a href="http://www.michaelcambre.com/">Michael Cambre</a>.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/348">Le Petit Prince</a> at AG Gallery.  This gallery, in the back of a cool store, has had several great shows, and given that the store hours are later than most galleries, makes a great stop at the end of the day, before or after dinner at Relish.  My favorite works in the show are the paintings of Christopher Reiger, and the &#8220;Action Painting&#8221; video by Ryouga Katsuma, which is one of my favorite videos of the year so far.  His paintings and drawings are good too.  There is a &#8220;Valentine&#8217;s Eve Afternoon Tea Party&#8221; at the gallery with sweets and tea on Sunday, February 13th from 3-5pm.</p>
<p>Eric Hollender&#8217;s works on paper at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/323">Dam, Stuhltrager</a>.  He uses silkscreens as if they were brushes or stamps to create compelling images of the competitive pigeon flocks of Williamsburg.</p>
<p>The wall drawings by <a href="http://www.open-ground.org/cgi-bin/wiki.rb/BrynnaTucker.html">Brynna K. Tucker</a> in the <a href="http://www.open-ground.org/cgi-bin/wiki.rb/ConfluenceExhibition.html">Confluence</a> show at Open Ground were my favorite works of that show.</p>
<p>There was a lot of good work in the <a href="http://www.blackandwhiteartgallery.com/buyitnow.html">Buy It Now</a> show at <a href="http://www.blackandwhiteartgallery.com/">Black and White</a>, but the show is over now.  There are some images on the web site, including the press release page.  I liked all of the work, but the video by Danielle LaPlante and the Photoshopped images of Saraw Sweeney really stood out.</p>
