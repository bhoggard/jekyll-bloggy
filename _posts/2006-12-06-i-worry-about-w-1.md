<p>The more I see of older art works, the more I worry that very few things are truly new.  The latest incident?  When I saw John Smith&#8217;s <em>Girl Chewing Gum</em> (1976) in Artists Space&#8217;s <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/7/3551"><em>In the Poem About Love You Don&#8217;t Write The Word Love</em></a>.  One watches a street scene and listens to a voice appearing to direct the action on the screen.  It doesn&#8217;t hurt that he sounds a bit like a Monty Python narrator.</p>
<p>I had never heard of this piece when I first saw Noah Klersfeld&#8217;s work <a href="http://www.noahklersfeld.com/PROJECTS/Payroll.htm"><em>Pay Roll</em></a>.  Todd Gibson describes it <a href="http://fromthefloor.blogspot.com/2004/11/backpack-give-me-neck-kink.html">here</a>.    Maybe it doesn&#8217;t really matter, since I knew nothing of John Smith&#8217;s work, and probably Todd and Mr. Klersfeld didn&#8217;t either.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.luxonline.org.uk/artists/john_smith/the_girl_chewing_gum.html">luxonline</a> has a page describing the work and a short video excerpt.</p>
