<p>For those in the DC area, or those that feel like visiting, <a href="http://www.joeovelman.com/home">Joe Ovelman</a> has a show at <a href="http://www.connercontemporary.com/">Conner Contemporary</a> opening March 4 and running through March 26.</p>
<p>From the press release:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Within gay culture the slang term snow queen typically refers to a black man who only dates white men. However, for Joe Ovelman the term is used more literally. His recent photographic series depicts the artist, dressed in his grandmother&#8217;s white stag coat and glamour drag, in wild abandon on a snowy night in the notorious &#8216;rambles&#8217; of New York&#8217;s Central Park. The images possess both the candidness found in Lisette Model&#8217;s socialites and the campy absurdity of John Waters.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>If you are interested in the details of the piece, I was told by Joe that it was shot in a single night by himself with a tripod.  Amazing.</p>
<p>We are big, big fans of Joe&#8217;s work, having purchased the first piece he ever sold, and staying friends with him ever since.  We own a couple of pieces from the <em>Snow Queen</em> series, including the one above.  Ours is from an edition that is of larger dimension that this one.</p>
<p>For those readers that have visited our apartment, Joe did the <em>Marine Corps Uniform- c1970</em> work in our entry hallway.  Ours is the study for the <a href="http://www.joeovelman.com/work/gallery/116">final version</a>.</p>
<p>I am impressed with Conner.  Last year they showed a series, <em><a href="http://www.joeovelman.com/work/gallery/108">17 Strangers</a></em>, that was never shown in a New York Gallery.</p>
<p><strong>Updated</strong></p>
