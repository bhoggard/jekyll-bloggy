<p>I&#8217;m too discouraged to post on political topics too often, but here are two things I want you to see.</p>
<p>Target may like to pretend it&#8217;s the hip alternative to Wal-Mart and K-Mart, but they <a href="http://americablog.blogspot.com/2005/11/target-digs-itself-deeper-hole-in.html">have decided</a> that their pharmacists are allowed to decide whether to fill prescriptions for emergency contraceptive pills (such as Plan B) based on their <strong>religious beliefs</strong>.  That&#8217;s crap.  You&#8217;re either a pharmacy or you&#8217;re not.  Leave that stuff to the churches who are already abusing their tax-exempt status to interfere in our lives.  As I said to James, apparently religious freedom is the only right left.</p>
<p>Via <a href="http://edwardwinkleman.blogspot.com/2005/11/call-your-senators.html">Ed Winkleman</a>, I learned of an <a href="http://obsidianwings.blogs.com/obsidian_wings/2005/11/closing_stateme.html">excellent series</a> on the group blog Obsidian Wings about the debate over habeas corpus and torture currently being conducted in the Senate.  Well, not really conducted, since they&#8217;re trying to ram this stuff through with minimal debate.  The idea that the Senate is talking about secret trials and repealing habeas corpus for any group at all (Magna Carta, anyone?) is appalling.</p>
<p>Remember, after World War II, a number of German and Japanese officials and military officers were executed for the things many Republicans, including Dick Cheney, are advocating.  Check out this <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2005/11/13/AR2005111301061.html">op-ed</a> from the Washington Post for more on the topic.</p>
<p>In a perfect world, these people would be hauled before The Hague.  We&#8217;ll see.</p>
