<p>Now, why would anyone expect something sexy to happen in an opera about Don Juan?  From <a href="http://www.msnbc.com/news/910921.asp?0cv=CB20"><span class="caps">MSNBC</span></a>, the story of a production of Don Giovanni that had to be &#8220;edited&#8221;.  It&#8217;s not enough for people to home-school their kids &#8212; they have to arrange for censorship of any arts productions they might see too.</p>
<blockquote>Opera Colorado directors have scrapped a racy scene from a production of &#8220;Don Giovanni&#8221; that prompted complaints from parents of home-schooled students who watched a dress rehearsal. The scene featuring a woman in a one-piece bustier, fishnet stockings, garter belt and high heels cavorting with a sometimes shirtless Don Juan Giovanni will be replaced with a food fight in some performances.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
&#8220;It was kind of hard to miss what they were doing. It was pretty blatant,&#8221; said Candice Cirbo, who attended the rehearsal with the group and her sixth-grade daughter.<br />
<br />
Opera spokesman Rex Fuller said earlier this week that the graphic action in the opera had been ordered toned down several days beforehand by the opera president and general director, Peter Russell.</blockquote>
