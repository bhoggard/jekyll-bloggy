<p>I forgot to mention a couple of other items in my earlier <a href="/2003/10/moved-by-art-in.html">post</a> on our trip to Chinatown and the Lower East Side to see art.  There is a cool show of almost photo-realistic oil on plastic paintings by Dan Colen at <a href="/2002/05/overheard-on-th.html">Rivington Arms</a> &#8212; the gallery that disdains the idea of a website.  I once asked whether they were going to get one, and they said, &#8220;that&#8217;s not really the audience we&#8217;re aiming for.&#8221;  This comes from people who were chatting with trucker-hatted visitors who were explaining that one simply cannot live comfortably in <span class="caps">NYC</span> with an apartment worth less than $1 million.</p>
<p>The other thing: there was a street fair on lower Crosby that was a real one &#8212; not one of those awful fried dough and sausage cart things that pass for most street fairs.  There were quite a few artists who also do work with clothing there.  I bought a scarf made by <a href="/2003/05/intermittent-po-1.html">Emily Noelle Lambert</a>.</p>
