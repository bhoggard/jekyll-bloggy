<p>Derek Jarman&#8217;s manuscripts are featured in an exhibit titled <a href="http://www.bl.uk/whatson/exhibitions/gardens/homepage.html">The Writer in the Garden</a> at the British Library.  I read about it in <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/arts/news/story/0,11711,1341392,00.html">The Guardian</a>.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Some of his late manuscripts and notebooks &#8211; covers beautifully personalised with gold or black impasto, full of poems, jottings and memories &#8211; will be revealed this week at an exhibition at the British Library in London.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Jarman kept 16 volumes of diaries recounting the making of his garden at Prospect Cottage, on the Kent coastline, where he moved in 1988. Here he created a curious Eden in the most hostile, salt-caked, windswept environment imaginable, a strange garden full of twisted metal and driftwood, in the shadow of the Dungeness nuclear power station.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Much of the material from the notebooks was incorporated into his published diaries, Modern Nature, but the exquisite objects themselves, two of which the British Library has borrowed from Jarman&#8217;s estate, have never before been seen in public.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Speaking of visual/film artists with a talent for the written word, last night we attended an event at <span class="caps">PPOW</span> featuring writers and artists inspired by the works (written and otherwise) of <a href="http://www.ppowgallery.com/exhibitions/2004wojnarowicz/index.html">David Wojnarowicz</a>.  The main reason we attended was to see <a href="http://www.mattwolf.info/&#39;s">Matt Wolf</a> slide show on Wojnarowicz&#8217;s &#8220;magic box&#8221; in his archives at <span class="caps">NYU</span>.  It&#8217;s amazing to realize that Matt is only 22.  We have already <a href="/2003/12/insurance-for-n.html">seen</a> several works by him, and even &#8220;invested&#8221; a small amount into his <a href="http://www.mattwolf.info/love/index.html">latest film project</a>.  It&#8217;s titled <em>I Feel Love</em> &#8212; Matt titles his latest films after Communards/Bronski Beat songs &#8212; and has Andrew Cunanan as its subject.</p>
