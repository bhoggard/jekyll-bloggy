<p>So just go and read the latest <a href="http://sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/g/a/2002/07/10/notes071002.DTL&nl=fix">Mark Morford column</a>.<br />
<blockquote>Of course we&#8217;re at war. Just look at all those flags stuck in all those manicured lawns, the ominous United We Stand billboards, the all-new 2003 Ford Excursion now with room for 13 and a full 10mpg Highway/7mpg City, all the cheap plastic stars-and-stripes kitsch at the Hallmark store, Made in Malaysia.</p>
<p>&#8230;</p>
<p>Of course we&#8217;re at war. Witness all the angry puffed-up deflections, every reproach of the president and every suspicious glance in the direction of his corporatized administration instantly retorted with a nice &#8220;how dare you don&#8217;t you know we&#8217;re at war&#8221; or maybe &#8220;the president has a great deal on his very compact little mind right now and he can&#8217;t be bothered with the details of, you know, rampant favoritism and hypocrisy.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8230;</p>
<p>And it&#8217;s becoming increasingly difficult to find anyone but the truest I-believe-everything-Ari-Fleischer-says jingoists who actually believes this &#8220;war&#8221; has become anything but a grand excuse, a marvelously leveragable plaything which the Bush cadre can point to as their very own personal holy shroud, some sort of sacrosanct shield to protect them from criticism and claims of blatant impropriety and selling the nation&#8217;s soul for pennies on the barrel. </blockquote></p>
