	<li>Marty Eisenberg</li>
	<li>Nicole Eisenman, artist</li>
	<li>Barbara Hunt, executive director of <a href="http://www.artistsspace.org/">Artists Space</a> (remember when they got in trouble over David Wojnarowicz?)</li>

	<li><a href="http://www.nicoleklagsbrun.com/">Nicole Klagsbrun</a>, gallerist &#8211; hosting an event on the 26th with Peter Hort</li>

<p>My list of names came from an email Simon sent to James, which was also forwarded to me by several people who thought I might be interested in this particular mix of arts and politics.  There are additional people mentioned in a <a href="http://www.peterhort.com/media/PR/20040304.PR.Art.Community.pdf" title="PDF">press release</a> on Peter Hort&#8217;s web site.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s hard to fit all of this into a single post, but I&#8217;m going to try.</p>
<p>Item 1:  A vote for Peter Hort is a vote for the Republican Party.  That means you are supporting the following agenda:</p>
<ul>
	<li>anti-gay</li>
	<li>anti-choice</li>
	<li>pro-Iraq War, which diverted resources away from hunting the perpetrators of 9/11 and has wasted so many lives and billions of dollars</li>
	<li>anti-immigrant</li>
	<li>anti-cities</li>
	<li>anti-culture</li>
</ul>
<p>Item 2: A vote for Peter Hort is a vote to strengthen the power of the right-wing House leadership.  The de facto leader of the House is Tom DeLay (R-TX), the Majority Leader.  Were Peter Hort to defeat Jerrold Nadler, it would increase the GOP&#8217;s margin in the House.  Here are some links on Tom DeLay:</p>
<ul>
	<li><a href="http://www.buzzflash.com/editorial/04/10/edi04072.html">Why no American should vote for Tom DeLay</a></li>
	<li><a href="http://www.takingontomdelay.com/">Taking on Tom DeLay weblog</a></li>
	<li><a href="http://www.thecarpetbaggerreport.com/archives/000025.html">Discussion of a Paul Krugman column on DeLay</a></li>
	<li><a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/ac2/wp-dyn?pagename=article&amp;node=&amp;contentId=A18077-2002Apr19&amp;notFound=true">DeLay on &#8216;only Christianity has the answers&#8217;</a></li>
	<li><a href="http://atrios.blogspot.com/2004/02/us-and-them.html">DeLay on homosexuals as un-American</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Another member of the House leadership is Speaker Dennis Hastert.  Yes, the one who said New Yorkers were <a href="/2004/08/hastert-says-ne.html">too greedy after 9/11</a>.</p>
<p>I can think of two explanations of why people in the art world would support Peter Hort.</p>
<p>The first one is that they are being naive.  They buy into the idea that politics can be about individual politicians, and not their parties.  I wish that were the case, but to support someone running on the Republican and Conservative Party lines for Congress against a Democratic incumbent is a vote to empower Tom DeLay and his ilk.  I wish there were moderates in positions of power in the <span class="caps">GOP</span>, but there are none.</p>
<p>It doesn&#8217;t matter if Mr. Hort professes decent positions in person. I am told by several people that I should talk to him personally, and that he is pro-choice and pro-gay marriage.  It doesn&#8217;t matter.  A vote for him is a vote for the <span class="caps">GOP</span> agenda.</p>
<p>The second explanation is the cynical one.  Mr. Hort&#8217;s parents, <a href="http://www.artnet.com/magazine/people/laster/laster3-29-04.asp">Susan and Michael Hort</a>, are among the most important collectors in New York right now.  Peter Hort runs a foundation named after his late sister, the Rema Hort Mann Foundation, which <a href="http://www.remahortmannfoundation.org/Art_Grants/Recipeints/recipeints.html">gives grants to artists</a>.</p>
<p>When important collectors ask people in the art world to support their son&#8217;s candidacy, many of them feel they have no choice but to do so.  To do otherwise would be bad for business or bad for one&#8217;s artistic career.  Maybe most of them realize that he is unlikely to win, so &#8220;no harm done&#8221; is their rationale.  Besides, in general they are privileged enough to feel that the <span class="caps">GOP</span> agenda won&#8217;t directly cause them any harm, while opposing Peter Hort&#8217;s campaign may do quite visible harm to a career or business.</p>
<p>I find it inconceivable that any person who cares about culture or the state of our nation and planet would vote for a member of the party of George W. Bush.</p>
<p>This is my last post on this subject unless something really big comes up.  It&#8217;s giving me an ulcer.<br />
