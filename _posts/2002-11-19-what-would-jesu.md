<p>Yes, that&#8217;s an actual <a href="http://www.whatwouldjesusdrive.org/">quote</a> from a new media campaign <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/19/business/media/19ADCO.html?ex=1038373200&en=2a609d81d572a50a&ei=5062&partner=GOOGLE">linking conservation with godliness</a>.</p>
<blockquote> &#8220;We are under a commandment to be faithful stewards of God&#8217;s creation,&#8221; said Paul Gorman, executive director of the National Religious Partnership for the Environment, an umbrella organization of Christian and Jewish groups. &#8220;This is a crisis in God&#8217;s creation at the hands of God&#8217;s children.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Leaders of many groups within the partnership have signed a letter to the Big Three&#8217;s chief executives asking for improvements in fuel economy. They say they have a biblical mandate to be good stewards of God&#8217;s creation and a responsibility to the poor who are especially harmed by pollution. And they decry supporting &#8220;autocratic, corrupt and violent&#8221; governments that produce oil.</blockquote>
<p>I think the more likely outcome, if there a lot of such ads, is that it will lower the power of religion, but not oil.  This country manages to combine <a href="http://www.atheists.org/flash.line/church16.htm">one of the highest rates of believers in God</a> in the rich world with a depressing lack of concern for anyone less fortunate.</p>
