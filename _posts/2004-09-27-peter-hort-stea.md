<p>I have been meaning to write about this subject for several weeks, but today&#8217;s <em>Newsday</em> <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/news/local/manhattan/nyc-nynad273986631sep27,0,2215654.story?coll=nyc-moreny-headlines">article</a> on Peter Hort reminded me to do it.  Peter Hort is running for Congress against <a href="http://www.house.gov/nadler/">Jerrold Nadler</a>, the incumbent.  Nadler is one of the most liberal members of Congress, and I rarely bother to contact him on any issue, because I agree with his position on nearly everything.  The only disagreement is that I think he is a bit too ready to give Israel&#8217;s government the benefit of the doubt when it comes to its behavior vis-a-vis the Occupied Territories.</p>
<p>Peter Hort is running as a stealth Republican.  His campaign literature doesn&#8217;t mention it, and barely appears on his web site unless you read fine print like the mention of his appearance on <span class="caps">WNYC</span>.  He pretends to be an &#8220;independent.&#8221;  New Yorkers need to realize what happens when they vote for a Republican.  I don&#8217;t care if he marches in the annual gay pride parade.  A vote for a Republican in the House is a vote for the House leadership, unless the candidate is promising to work to defeat the current leadership.  That leadership includes:</p>
<ul>
	<li><a href="/2004/08/hastert-says-ne.html">Dennis Hastert</a>, Speaker of the House</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.zmag.org/content/showarticle.cfm?SectionID=43&amp;ItemID=4703">Tom</a> <a href="http://nomoremister.blogspot.com/2002_11_10_nomoremister_archive.html#84547699">DeLay</a>, Majority Leader</li>
</ul>
<p>Mr. Hort&#8217;s <a href="/2004/04/the-horts-colle.html">parents</a> are contemporary art collectors, whose taste I respect, and who support very edgy artists, including ones that make sexually explicit art.  How their son can be a member of a party that thinks those people should be in jail, if not worse, is beyond me.  I have written to Mr. Hort&#8217;s campaign to ask his position on the current <span class="caps">GOP</span> House leadership team.</p>
