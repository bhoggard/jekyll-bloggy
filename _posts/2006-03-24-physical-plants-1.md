In a time when clowns are tortured and circuses banned, a renegade troupe enacts the story of a girl who longs for their outlawed life.</blockquote>

<p>I don&#8217;t know if there are <a href="http://www.smarttix.com/show.aspx?showCode=NOT2">tickets left</a>, but there are two more performances (tonight and Saturday) of &#8220;Not Clown&#8221; at <a href="http://www.sohorep.org/">Soho Rep</a>.  It will be the best $15 you have spent on theater lately.</p>
<p>I have to say, this isn&#8217;t the play for people expecting a Broadway show.  It&#8217;s more in the spirit of the good East Village / Lower East Side work I saw in the late 80s and early 90s.  It&#8217;s disturbing and strange.  I will admit that, at times, I found it annoying and repellent, and wondered what I was doing there.  By the end, I realized the dramatic reasons for those sections, and felt it was one of the best things I&#8217;ve seen in the theater in a long time.  Those of you who know us probably aren&#8217;t surprised to read that we like art that annoys, disturbs, and then pulls it all off briliiantly.</p>
<p>The company presenting it, <a href="http://www.physicalplant.org/">Physical Plant</a> is based in Austin, Texas.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t have time to write more right now, but wanted to get this posted so maybe some more people can go see it.</p>
