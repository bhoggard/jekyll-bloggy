<p>Hmm&#8230;  Should this be filed under <a href="/?cat=2">culture</a> or <a href="/?cat=6">queer</a>?  It&#8217;s one of those high/low art kind of things.</p>
<p>Last night we saw <a href="http://www.gaypimp.com/">Jonny McGovern</a>, Gay Pimp Daddy/International Gay Teen Pop Superstar, at <a href="http://www.ps122.org/">PS122</a>.  We took James&#8217;s nephew Peter &#8211; the gay psychriatrist nephew &#8211; and his friend John.  It was great fun, with Jonny and his backup dancers (including one <a href="http://www.abercrombie.com/">Abercrombie and Fitch</a> model), plus two fabulous draq queens.  I think my favorite song was &#8220;Hey, Little Raver Boi&#8221;.  The choreography, by Jonny, Kimmarie Lynch, and Christopher Campell, was hilarious &#8212; trashy queer boy band dancing.  The costumes for &#8220;Raver Boi&#8221; included light sticks, visors, and bell bottoms of course.</p>
<p>It lasts until May 12.  Worth a visit I think.  The crowd was an interesting mix, including straight boys and their girlfriends.  The girls really screamed for the songs about Jonny seducing straight boys.  This <a href="http://www.villagevoice.com/issues/0132/nutter.php">post-straight</a> world is very interesting.</p>
<p>We had dinner afterward at my favorite East Village restaurant, <a href="http://newyork.citysearch.com/profile/11313422/">Raga</a> &#8212; Indian fusion, not really Indian, one block east of the glitter of the 6th Street Little India.</p>

<blockquote>Camber attacks with buzzing guitars desperately hung over a rock solid backbeat.</blockquote><br />
I just love hung over guitars.</p>
