<p>After a court ruled that the Pledge of Allegiance containing the phrase &#8220;under God&#8221; (added in 1954) <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A49515-2002Jun26.html">is unconstitutional</a>, our elected leaders all rushed to proclaim their support for God:</p>
<blockquote> House members gathered on the front steps of the Capitol to recite the Pledge of Allegiance en masse. The Senate unanimously approved a resolution sponsored by its Democratic and Republican leaders that expressed support for the reference to God in the pledge, and instructed the Senate&#8217;s legal counsel to intervene in the case. The vote was 99 to 0, with Sen. Jesse Helms (R-N.C.) absent.</blockquote>
<p>Unanimous.</p>
<p>When the phrase &#8220;Under God&#8221; was added in 1954, Eisenhower said the change was being made &#8220;to recognize a Supreme Being&#8221; and advance religion at a time &#8220;when the government was publicly inveighing against atheistic communism&#8221;.</p>
