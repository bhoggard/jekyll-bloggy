<p>Let&#8217;s just call it the <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/news/opinion/nyc-vppad273559572nov26.story">Bush junta</a>.  Arguing before an appeals court that the government should be able to hold José Padilla indefinitely in a military brig without acccess to a lawyer, Bush&#8217;s lawyers said that the entire United States is a battlefield and thus operates under &#8220;military justice.&#8221;</p>
<blockquote>On Sept. 11, 2001, &#8220;al-Qaida made the battlefield the United States and the evidence indicates that they&#8217;re trying to make it the battlefield again,&#8221; said Deputy Solicitor General Paul Clement. And if it&#8217;s a &#8220;battlefield&#8221; arrest, Bush can detain anyone alleged to be in league with terrorists, including citizens, for as long as it takes to gather intelligence and deter future attacks. &#8220;This is the way it&#8217;s been done for 200 years in military justice,&#8221; Clement said.</blockquote>
