<p>Given the recent Scooter Libby trial, now seems like a good time to mention a short theater work that James and I saw last month as part of Target Margin&#8217;s <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/006010.html">season</a> of plays inspired by classical Greek works.  The theme of the play was scapegoats, and the title was <a href="http://targetmargin.org/GREEKLABS.html">&#8220;Taking One for the Team&#8221;</a>.  It managed to blend the stories of Iphigenia, Scooter Libby, plus others into a short sharp piece.  It was created by the sound artist Michael Kraskin in collaboration with the cast.  I loved the final speech of Iphegenia, inspired by a text of Euripides.  In it she talks of how her sacrifice, and that of other Greeks, is necessary to show that the Greeks are civilized, and their enemies are barbarians.</p>
<p>Thanks to that production, I discovered the podcast he has been doing with David Terry since late 2005, titled <a href="http://www.catalogueofships.com/">Catalogue of Ships</a>, with the title <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalogue_of_ships">inspired</a> by  Homer&#8217;s &#8220;Iliad&#8221;.  Thanks to the power of Odeo, I&#8217;m embedding a few of my favorite episodes so far.  I&#8217;ve been listening in order, and I&#8217;m only through the first year so far.  If you like <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/006018.html">Robert Ashley</a> or <a href="http://www.mikelrouse.com/">Mikel Rouse</a>, I think you&#8217;ll like these.</p>
<p>I chose Episode Three for its really beautiful sound design.</p>
