<p>I took a two-page printed list of the books from my <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/redirect?tag=tristanmedial-20&amp;path=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2Fgp%2Fregistry%2Fregistry.html%2Fref%3Dwlem-si-ht_gotowl%3F%255Fencoding%3DUTF8%26id%3D2MIBD7TBYEVI8">Amazon wish list</a> with me to my local library branch, since I start jury duty on Thursday.  I&#8217;m a pretty fast typist, so I checked a bunch in a couple of minutes, but soon a little old lady tapped on my shoulder.  &#8220;Are you going to go through that whole list?!&#8221; she asked.  I told her I was almost finished, pointing to the item on the page I was then checking.  She asked, &#8220;Is that the end, or are you going to flip it over and do some more?&#8221;  I assured her it was really at the end.</p>
<p>My jury duty is at 60 Centre Street.  I welcome any nearby attractions (ha!) or restaurant recommendations, particularly Vietnamese.  This <a href="http://www.chowhound.com/boards/manhat20/messages/26571.html">post</a> from Chowhound pretty much sums up the way I feel about those court buildings.</p>
<p>Maybe I&#8217;ll get a chance to sneer at the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004566.html">Kerik Complex</a> while I&#8217;m there.</p>
