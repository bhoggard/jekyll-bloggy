<p>I dropped by <a href="http://www.artcat.com/exhibits/10332">Cheryl McGinnis Gallery</a> (at West 38th and 8th Avenue) last week to see Susan Hamburger&#8217;s show with the delicious title <em>Moral Hazard</em>. The Economist has a <a href="http://www.economist.com/RESEARCH/ECONOMICS/alphabetic.cfm?letter=M#moralhazard">definition</a> but the meaning in this sense is primarily that powerful individuals and institutions take more risks when they know their failures will be bailed out by another party &#8212; often the taxpayers.</p>

<p><img alt="susan-hamburger-low-road.jpg" src="/2009/12/06/images/susan-hamburger-low-road.jpg" width="600" height="450" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Detail of <em>The Low Road</em> with Mark Foley, Ted Haggard, Larry Craig, Vito Fosella, and John Ensign.</p>

<p>The exquisitely-crafted works, using acrylic on paper and collage, contain aspects of <a href="http://www.metmuseum.org/toah/hd/ropt/hd_ropt.htm">Roman wall painting</a> but according to the artist&#8217;s <a href="http://www.susanhamburger.net/pages.php?content=gallery.php&amp;navGallID=16">web site</a> are inspired by self-made <em>ancien régime</em> wallpaper manufacturer <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_R%C3%A9veillon">Jean-Baptiste Réveillon</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="susan-hamburger-Ponzi.jpg" src="/2009/12/06/images/susan-hamburger-Ponzi.jpg" width="450" height="600" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Detail from <em>The Con and The Nine</em> with Charles Ponzi</p>

<p>Susan uses her beautiful technique to reflect on the financial and sexual rogues of our current era, ranging from <span class="caps">GOP</span> Senator <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2009/10/07/gop-in-bind-over-scandal_n_312561.html">John Ensign</a> to Wall Street bankers.</p>

<p><img alt="susan-hamburger-the-nine.jpg" src="/2009/12/06/images/susan-hamburger-the-nine.jpg" width="600" height="450" class="mt-image-none" /></p>
<p>Detail from <em>The Nine</em> with John Mack (Morgan Stanley), John Thain (Merrill Lynch) and Henry Paulson (Former Secretary of the Treasury)</p>

<p>The show is up through December 18.  Highly recommended as a response to the beautiful objects vs. serious issues <a href="http://jameswagner.com/2009/11/powhida_on_numu.html#comment-205708">&#8220;dichotomy&#8221;</a> in the art world.  Susan gives us both.</p>
