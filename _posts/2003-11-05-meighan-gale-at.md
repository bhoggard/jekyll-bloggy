<p>Did I scare my readers?  Perhaps you feared no more art posts?  Barry had gone off the deep end?</p>
<p>Fear no more.  Here are a couple of art recommendations.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.blackandwhiteartgallery.com/gale/index.htm">Meighan Gale</a> at <a href="http://www.blackandwhiteartgallery.com/">Black &amp; White Gallery</a> in Williamsburg:</p>
<p><img alt="meighan-gale-1.jpg" src="/mt/archives/meighan-gale-1.jpg" width="487" height="377" border="0" /></p>
<p>Meighan, who had work in the inaugural show of this gallery, now has a solo show.  We have known her for a number of years, and have a few works by her.  In person, she always feels like a dancer &#8212; the way she moves, a certain kind of poise.  I think the photographs in the show give a sense of that.  I&#8217;ve never known her to do public performances &#8212; the photographs serve as documentation for performances that we never see in person.  The color works in the show are high-quality inkjet prints, which give a very polished, elegant quality to her work, which was often rougher and more hand-made in the past, from the quality of the prints to the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003513.html">stitched thread</a> that appears in many of the works.  I don&#8217;t prefer one style over the other, but it is cool to see an artist work both ways and make interesting art in the process.</p>
<p>__</p>
<p>My other recommendation is David Shapiro at <a href="http://www.jackthepelicanpresents.com/shapiro_pr.html">Jack The Pelican Presents</a>.  It&#8217;s next door to Black &amp; White on Driggs, so it&#8217;s easy to stop by and see both of these.  I have seen a few snarky &#8220;been there, done that&#8221; comments on the show, generally from people who sound like they haven&#8217;t bothered to see it &#8212; cough <a href="http://www.gawker.com/archives/009591.php">gawker</a> cough.  I think the show is great.  I&#8217;m not sure how to explain it, but it works.  Don&#8217;t miss the back room where his plants, cared for with loving attention, live in pots printed with the scary ingredient lists of some of the items in the front.</p>
