<p>Macro/Micro.  Maybe it&#8217;s my economics degree causing me to use such words, but that was in my thoughts after I left <a href="http://www.sixtysevengallery.com/artists/eggebrecht/eggebrecht.html&#39;s">Echo Eggebrecht</a> smart painting show at <a href="http://www.sixtysevengallery.com/">Sixtyseven Gallery</a>.  It&#8217;s the first show in their new space on 27th Street since moving from Williamsburg.</p>
<p>The paintings range in size from 18&#8243; &#215; 18&#8243; to 36&#8243; &#215; 48&#8243;, but I remembered them as quite large.  I don&#8217;t think they reproduce that well in the images on the gallery web site, as there is a high level of detail in them.  I had the gallery give me a CD of high quality images so I could provide some details.</p>
<p>The first one is <em>Snake in the Grass</em> (2004) , acrylic on panel, 24&#8243; &#215; 36.&#8243;</p>

<p>You can&#8217;t easily see the interesting details, like the quality of the grass around the garden hose and the amazing needle-point-like fabrics on the clothes drying rack.  Even with detail shots I have trouble getting my point across, but here goes.</p>
