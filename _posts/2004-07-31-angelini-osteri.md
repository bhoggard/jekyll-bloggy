<p>Wow!  <a href="http://www.angeliniosteria.com/">This</a> was one of my favorite creative Italian dining experiences ever, including our <a href="http://www.hoggard.org/travel/">trips to Italy</a>.  It was recommended by our friend David F.</p>
<p>We started with a glasses of prosecco, then two appetizers: one of oven roasted polipo (octopus) with roasted cherry tomatoes and arugula, and one of house-cured anchovies with beets and artichokes.  For our main course, James had bomboletti all&#8217;amatriciana (short rigatoni with tomato, onions, red pepper and <a href="http://www.italiancookingandliving.com/food/glossary/g.html">guanciale</a>), and I had homemade ravioli stuffed with <a href="http://www.italiancookingandliving.com/food/ingredients/burrata.html">burrata</a> and heirloom tomatoes with a pesto sauce.</p>
<p>We had a bottle of <a href="http://www.feudi.it/">Feudi di San Gregorio</a> Fiano di Avellino with the meal.</p>
<p>The espresso was perfect too.</p>
