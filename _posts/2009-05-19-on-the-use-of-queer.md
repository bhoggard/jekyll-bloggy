<p>I love this part of the <em>New York Times</em> <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/13/books/13garn.html">review</a> of Reynold Price&#8217;s latest book.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Mr. Price applies the word &#8220;queer&#8221; and dislikes &#8220;gay&#8221; because he thinks the latter hurt homosexual men during the early years of the <span class="caps">AIDS</span> crisis. It fit the assumption of bigots, he writes, that &#8220;homosexuals were giddy irresponsibles, negligible creatures.&#8221; Mr. Price quotes a friend who says: &#8220;Please don’t call me gay. If you need an adjective, call me morose.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
