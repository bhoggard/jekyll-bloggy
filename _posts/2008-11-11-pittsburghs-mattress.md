<blockquote><p><strong><a href="http://www.streetwithaview.com/">Street With A View</a></strong> introduces fiction, both subtle and spectacular, into the doppelganger world of Google Street View.</p><p>On May 3rd 2008, artists <a href="http://www.robinhewlett.com/">Robin Hewlett</a> and <a href="http://www.bkinsley.com/">Ben Kinsley</a><br />
invited the Google Inc. Street View team and residents of Pittsburgh’s<br />
Northside to collaborate on a series of tableaux along Sampsonia Way.<br />
Neighbors, and other participants from around the city, staged scenes<br />
ranging from a parade and a marathon, to a garage band practice, a<br />
seventeenth century sword fight, a heroic rescue and much more&#8230;&nbsp; </p><p>Street<br />
View technicians captured 360-degree photographs of the street with the<br />
scenes in action and integrated the images into the Street View mapping<br />
platform. This first-ever artistic intervention in Google Street View<br />
made its debut on the web in November of 2008.</p>
An incredible
<p>cast of real-life characters contributed their time, energy and talents<br />
to creating pseudo-street life on Sampsonia Way. Please check out the <a href="http://www.streetwithaview.com/scenes.html">scene breakdown,</a> the <a href="http://www.streetwithaview.com/participants.html">participant page</a> and the  <a href="http://www.streetwithaview.com/video.html">video documentation</a> to learn more about the artists, groups and participants that made <strong>Street With A View</strong> possible.<br /></blockquote></p>
