<p>If I&#8217;m going to live in a third world banana republic, things should be cheaper, the cities should be more colorful, the food should be better and more spicy, and my fellow citizens should be more attractive.</p>
<p>From today&#8217;s Washington Post we <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A9708-2003Nov6.html">learn</a>:</p>
<blockquote>The Bush White House, irritated by pesky questions from congressional Democrats about how the administration is using taxpayer money, has developed an efficient solution: It will not entertain any more questions from opposition lawmakers.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
The director of the White House Office of Administration, Timothy A. Campen, sent an e-mail titled &#8220;congressional questions&#8221; to majority and minority staff on the House and Senate Appropriations panels. Expressing &#8220;the need to add a bit of structure to the Q&amp;A process,&#8221; he wrote: &#8220;Given the increase in the number and types of requests we are beginning to receive from the House and Senate, and in deference to the various committee chairmen and our desire to better coordinate these requests, I am asking that all requests for information and materials be coordinated through the committee chairmen and be put in writing from the committee.&#8221;<br />
<br />
He said this would limit &#8220;duplicate requests&#8221; and help answer questions &#8220;in a timely fashion.&#8221;<br />
<br />
It would also do another thing: prevent Democrats from getting questions answered without the blessing of the <span class="caps">GOP</span> committee chairmen.<br />
<br />
&#8220;It&#8217;s saying we&#8217;re not going to allow the opposition party to ask questions about the way we use tax money,&#8221; said R. Scott Lilly, Democratic staff director for the House committee. &#8220;As far as I know, this is without modern precedent.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Norman Ornstein, a congressional specialist at the American Enterprise Institute, agreed. &#8220;I have not heard of anything like that happening before,&#8221; he said. &#8220;This is obviously an excuse to avoid providing information about some of the things the Democrats are asking for.&#8221; </blockquote>
<p>A Democratic Senator cannot ask questions unless a Republican Senator approves the question.</p>
