<p>Right now I can hear the &#8220;gay pride&#8221; fireworks outside.  It&#8217;s odd to hear something that sounds like we&#8217;re being bombed as I listen to a <a href="http://www.kusc.org/pro_Record_Shelf.shtml">program</a> on <span class="caps">KUSC</span> featuring <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/redirect?link_code=ur2&amp;camp=1789&amp;tag=tristanmedial-20&amp;creative=9325&amp;path=classical-artist-search/Oswald%20Kabasta">recordings</a> of symphonies conducted by <a href="http://classicalcdreview.com/kabasta.htm">Oswald Kabasta</a>.  Go read that last link.  Kabasta was a pro-Nazi conductor originally from Austria, and performed in Munich until performances were suspended due to heavy Allied bombing in 1944.  After his de-Nazification hearing, and his demotion to the status of a &#8220;common laborer,&#8221; he committed suicide in February 1946.</p>
<p>The recordings we heard tonight were extraordinary.  It&#8217;s sad to think such a musical genius was so terribly wrong in his political beliefs.</p>
<p>All hail the power of the internet!  I had never heard his story until I listened to this music today from a radio station on the other side of the country.</p>
