<p>I&#8217;m not sure if &#8220;admire&#8221; is the right word, but I&#8217;m impressed with what the Bush regime can get away with.  They&#8217;ve appointed a man who <a href="http://www.google.com/url?sa=X&oi=news&start=0&num=3&q=http://www.newsday.com/news/local/longisland/politics/ny-livit283023372nov28,0,7088374.column%3Fcoll%3Dny-lipolitics-print">orchestrated secret bombing</a> during the Viet Nam War, and <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/pinochet/Story/0,11993,735920,00.html">can&#8217;t travel freely overseas</a> at the risk of <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/views01/0603-05.htm">being arrested or subpoenaed</a> for his role in helping Pinochet&#8217;s Chile kill its opponents, even in the U.S.</p>
<p>Interestingly, the NY Times hasn&#8217;t mentioned Chile once in connection with Kissinger in yesterday&#8217;s <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/28/politics/28HENR.html">article</a> on his past or today&#8217;s <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/29/opinion/29FRI1.html">editorial</a>, which argues that he&#8217;s not a very good choice.</p>
