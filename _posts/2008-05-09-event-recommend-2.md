<p>James and I have been busy attending music and theater performances.  Here are some recommendations:</p>
<ul>
	<li>The New Georges (see <a href="http://www.google.com/custom?domains=bloggy.com&amp;q=new+georges&amp;sa=Google+Search&amp;sitesearch=bloggy.com&amp;client=pub-5677245260484261&amp;forid=1&amp;channel=9421574984&amp;ie=ISO-8859-1&amp;oe=ISO-8859-1&amp;flav=0000&amp;sig=RfmOPftUM_wl5Mzb&amp;cof=GALT%3A%230066CC%3BGL%3A1%3BDIV%3A%23999999%3BVLC%3A336633%3BAH%3Acenter%3BBGC%3AFFFFFF%3BLBGC%3AFF9900%3BALC%3A0066CC%3BLC%3A0066CC%3BT%3A000000%3BGFNT%3A666666%3BGIMP%3A666666%3BFORID%3A1%3B&amp;hl=en">earlier posts</a>) present <a href="http://www.newgeorges.org/ce.html">&#8220;Stretch&#8221;</a>, described as a fantasia about the final days of Rose Mary Woods, Richard Nixon&#8217;s loyal secretary.  You even get a live score for violin, trumpet, bass and <span class="caps">IBM</span> Selectric typewriter.  Use code <span class="caps">BLAST</span> <a href="http://www.smarttix.com/show.aspx?showCode=STR25">here</a> for $16 tickets through May 14.</li>
	<li>The <a href="http://www.elevator.org/">Elevator Repair Service</a>, one of the greatest theater companies I know, are presenting &#8220;The Sound And The Fury&#8221; at New York Theater Workshop.  Brave the evil Telecharge&#8217;s &#8220;Broadway Offers&#8221; site <a href="http://www.broadwayoffers.com/go.aspx?MD=2001&amp;MC=SDFTC56">here</a> to get $20 tickets for Memorial Day Weekend.  Anything starring Susie Sokol cannot be missed.</li>
	<li>There are still tickets left for <a href="http://www.gothamchamberopera.org/upcoming.html">Gotham Chamber Opera</a>&#8216;s &#8220;Ariadne Unhinged&#8221; this weekend, with music of Monteverdi, Haydn, and Schoenberg, production and choreography by <a href="http://www.armitagegonedance.org/">Karole Armitage</a>, and design by the artist <a href="http://www.gagosian.com/artists/vera-lutter">Vera Lutter</a>.  We&#8217;re going tonight.</li>
</ul>
<p>On the subject of Off and Off-Off Broadway, check out <a href="http://playgoer.blogspot.com/2008/05/ish-sets-oob-back-30-years.html">The Playgoer</a> on Christopher Isherwood&#8217;s stupid attack on Off-Off, as if it&#8217;s some kind of community theater.   See, my visual arts readers?  It&#8217;s not only the visual arts that get this kind of random stupid articles from <em>The New York Times</em>.  Do not miss the comments, especially that from sbs about the difficult relationship between Equity creative theater companies doing the best work in <span class="caps">NYC</span> including Elevator Repair Service and Target Margin.</p>
