<p>I&#8217;m in the middle of a fascinating book, titled <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0375421068/tristanmedial-20">Parallels and Paradoxes</a>.  It consists of a series of conversations between Daniel Barenboim and Edward W. Said on music, culture, and politics.  A sample, from Barenboim:</p>
<blockquote>I still think the greatest sense of isolation and removal from anything, for me &#8212; but this is very subjective, and I do not claim this to be objective &#8212; is in late Beethoven.  If you look at the <i>Grosse Fugue</i>, you look at passages of the <i>Missa Solemnis</i>, and you look at the Diabelli Variations or the last three piano sonatas, this is total isolation and removal from the world, much more so than Schoenberg.</blockquote>
