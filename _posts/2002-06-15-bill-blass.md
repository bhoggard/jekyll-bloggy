<p>Nice <a href="http://www.planetout.com/pno/news/article.html?2002/06/14/3">obit</a> of Bill Blass from PlanetOut.  I never met him, but I have a friend who was close to him, and another who worked for him for years.<br />
<blockquote>In New York, Blass is also remembered as a generous and influential supporter of <span class="caps">AIDS</span> treatment services since the late 1980s.</p>
<p>&#8220;He was a major donor to Gay Men&#8217;s Health Crisis at a time when prominent people were silent about <span class="caps">AIDS</span>,&#8221; said Ronald Johnson, associate executive director at <span class="caps">GMHC</span>, a New York-based agency that serves people with <span class="caps">AIDS</span>. &#8220;His visibility was important in raising consciousness about <span class="caps">HIV</span>/<span class="caps">AIDS</span>.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8230;</p>
<p>Jonathan Jacobs, executive director of the <span class="caps">AIDS</span> Care Center at New York Presbyterian Hospital, remembered that Blass first visited when the center was a &#8220;threadbare clinic&#8221; in 1988, and he became a major fund-raiser thereafter.</p>
<p>&#8220;He understood the spirit of what we were trying to do,&#8221; Jacobs told the Gay.com/PlanetOut.com Network. He also praised Blass&#8217; attention to detail, noting that Blass quietly made sure there were beautiful flower arrangements in the center&#8217;s waiting area each week for the past 14 years.</p>
<p>&#8220;We never had to ask him,&#8221; Jacobs said. &#8220;He was always there for us.&#8221;</blockquote></p>
