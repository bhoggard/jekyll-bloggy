<p>For a couple of minutes, I had a post up about Harriet Miers, the new Supreme Court nominee, being a board member of Exodus Ministries.  According to <a href="http://www.chrisgeidner.com/blog/archive/004827.html">Law Dork</a>, via <a href="http://www.wonkette.com/politics/white-house/index.php#mierss-shameful-secret-128664">Wonkette</a>,  this is not the &#8220;Freedom from homosexuality through the healing power of Jesus Christ&#8221; organization, but a Dallas based-organization which &#8220;seeks to encourage ex-offenders, to reunite them with their families, and to empower them to become self-sustaining, productive, Christ-centered members of society.&#8221;  No word yet on what they do with non-Christian ex-cons.</p>
<p>Still, this is one of the least qualified people I have seen in a while being nominated.  The employment section of the Washington Post <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2005/10/03/AR2005100300252.html">story</a> is pretty underwhelming:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Experience: 2004-present, White House counsel; 2003-2004, White House deputy chief of staff for policy; 2001-2003, White House staff secretary; 1995-2001, chairwoman, Texas Lottery Commission; 1972-2000, private law practice; 1992, president, Dallas Bar Association; 1989-1991, member, Dallas City Council; 1985, president, Dallas Bar Association.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>According to <a href="http://atrios.blogspot.com/2005_10_02_atrios_archive.html#112834385992438085">Atrios</a>, even some right-wingers thing she&#8217;s a joke.</p>
<p>James heard about the nomination today and said, &#8220;She can&#8217;t possibly get approved.&#8221;  My response was, &#8220;Justice Thomas.  There is nothing you can&#8217;t get on the Supreme Court these days.&#8221;</p>
