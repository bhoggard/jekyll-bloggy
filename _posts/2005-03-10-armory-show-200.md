<p>An impressionistic account of our first <a href="http://www.thearmoryshow.com/">Armory Show</a> day follows.  My emphasis is on artists I hadn&#8217;t seen before, with occasional lapses if amused by someone whose work I already know.</p>
<p>I agree with James that, so far, I have found the Armory more interesting than last year.  There is a bigger variety of non-blue chip artists.  Last year there were a lot of works by Gerhard Richter, for example.  I didn&#8217;t see one so far.</p>
<p>The best thing about having a press pass (other than getting into the press preview and not having to pay to come back):  <a href="http://www.choiresicha.com/&#39;s">Choire Sicha</a> face when he saw us at the press conference.</p>

<p>Susan Turcot is a French Canadian artist with something in common with our friend <a href="http://www.firstpulseprojects.com/joy.html">Joy Garnett</a>.  She creates works inspired by images in the media, but not directly from such images.</p>

<p>One of my favorite discoveries was the work of <a href="http://www.galeriebarbaraweiss.de/dateien/exhibitions.php?intern_nr=16">Andreas Siekmann</a> from the Berlin-based <a href="http://www.galeriebarbaraweiss.de/">Galerie Barbara Weiss</a>, a mix of excellent drawing and political content.  He had a show at the gallery titled <em>The Exclusive. On the Politics of the Excluded Fourth</em>.  The fourth refers to a fourth branch of government, in addition to executive, legislative, and judicial.  &#8220;Exclusive&#8221; is a play on words.  To us it means an elite of some kind, but in his case he means those excluded from regular political access, such as the very poor and immigrants.</p>
