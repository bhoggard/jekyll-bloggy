<p>Lisa at <a href="http://www.ruminatethis.com/2002_12_15_ruminatethis_archive.html#90067012">Ruminate This</a> writes about TomPaine.com&#8217;s <a href="http://www.tompaine.com/op_ads/opad.cfm/ID/6945">offer of $10,000</a> for information about who inserted the provision into the Homeland Security Bill protecting Eli Lilly from lawsuits:</p>
<blockquote>In November, as Congress finalized the legislation authorizing a new Department of Homeland Security, two paragraphs suddenly appeared in the bill giving drug maker Eli Lilly &amp; Company something it desired: a shield from lawsuits by parents who claim the company&#8217;s vaccines caused their children&#8217;s autism.<br />
<br />
The provision diverts those suits from state courts to a federal &#8216;vaccine court&#8217; where damages are capped at $250,000 &#8211; small compensation for a child&#8217;s lifetime of medical care. And because any damages awarded by the vaccine court are paid by U.S. taxpayers, manufacturers are relieved of liability.</blockquote>
<p>We have a very broken democracy when provisions like this become law and no person has to take credit or blame for it.  Bills don&#8217;t write themselves.</p>
