<p>Like a lot of busy tech types, I use <span class="caps">RSS</span> feeds to keep up with a lot of sites.  What&#8217;s <span class="caps">RSS</span> you ask?  <a href="http://www.feedburner.com/fb/a/aboutrss">FeedBurner</a> has a good explanation.  Basically they are feeds published by a website that can be read by an external website or program.  I use feeds to keep up with blogs and news sites so that I see when they have new items without having to actually visit the 100 different websites I&#8217;m tracking.  My favorite applications for tracking <span class="caps">RSS</span> feeds are</p>
<ul>
	<li><a href="http://www.bloglines.com/">BlogLines</a> (website)</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.google.com/reader">Google Reader</a> (website)</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.newsgator.com/NGOLProduct.aspx?ProdId=NetNewsWire&amp;ProdView=lite">NetNewsWire Lite</a> (<span class="caps">OSX</span>)</li>
</ul>
<p>My webhosting service for artists and galleries, <a href="http://www.artcat.com/">ArtCat</a>, automatically generates <span class="caps">RSS</span> feeds for my clients so that it&#8217;s easy for me to see when galleries have news or have added exhibitions, or when artists have new works and news items.  Until ArtCat, I had never seen a gallery other than <a href="http://www.sixspace.com/">sixspace</a> with a feed.  It appears that sites running the <a href="http://www.exhibit-e.com/">exhibit-E</a> software now are capable of generating <span class="caps">RSS</span> feeds if they wish.  I first noticed this when visiting the <a href="http://www.gagosian.com/">Gagosian</a> site to look at information on the Warhol shows.</p>
<p>I would like to think that ArtCat and <a href="http://www.artcal.net/">ArtCal</a> (which has <a href="http://www.artcal.net/about/feeds.tt">several feeds</a>) helped start this trend, but I doubt anyone&#8217;s likely to say so out loud.</p>
<p>While we&#8217;re on the subject of websites, what&#8217;s the deal with big name galleries that don&#8217;t have websites?  I know they want to keep things &#8220;personal&#8221; for their collectors, but it really interferes with the ability of the less fortunate to get information on their artists and exhibitions.  <a href="http://www.marianneboeskygallery.com/">Marianne Boesky Gallery</a> has a new building, but no website at the moment?  <a href="http://www.cohanandleslie.com/">Cohan and Leslie</a> has taken down their old site and now there is just a placeholder.  It&#8217;s amazing to me that galleries don&#8217;t work on a site at a temporary <span class="caps">URL</span> while keeping their old site up.  It&#8217;s not that hard.  When galleries move their sites to ArtCat, they work on a temporary site until they have brought enough information over to launch.  We don&#8217;t relaunch with a blank site that says &#8220;coming soon.&#8221;</p>
