<p>There is a <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&ncid=514&e=1&cid=514&u=/ap/20021215/ap_on_go_co/lott">news story</a> that Sen. Don Nickles of Oklahoma (no. 2 in the Senate) wants a new vote on the <span class="caps">GOP</span> Majority Leader.  Look at this quote:</p>
<blockquote>&#8220;I am concerned that Senator Lott has been weakened to the point that may jeopardize his ability to enact our agenda and speak to all Americans,&#8221; Nickles said in a statement. &#8220;There are several outstanding senators who are more than capable of effective leadership, and I hope we have an opportunity to choose.&#8221;<br />
<br />
In an <span class="caps">ABC</span> interview, he added: &#8220;Can he be effective? Can he campaign in places like Chicago? I don&#8217;t want to squander our ability to get things done. We only have a short window this year.&#8221; </blockquote>
<p>What&#8217;s with the Chicago bit?  I guess he thinks the <span class="caps">GOP</span> has the blacks in Oklahoma and Mississippi under control, but they&#8217;re not as docile in some of those Yankee cities?</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>Remember people, Nickles isn&#8217;t exactly a nice alternative.  He was <a href="http://gaytoday.badpuppy.com/garchive/events/062298ev.htm">one of the main opponents</a> of Hormel as ambassador to Luxembourg:</p>
<blockquote>He said that President&#8217;s Clinton&#8217;s appointment of philanthropist James Hormel, who is openly gay, to an ambassador&#8217;s post in Luxembourg should be denied because Hormel promotes &#8220;immoral behavior.&#8221;<br />
<br />
The Bible, according to the Oklahoma Senator&#8217;s calculations, teaches that homosexual behavior is &#8220;a sin&#8221; and &#8220;if one promotes that behavior&#133;that person shouldn&#8217;t be a representative of this country.&#8221; </blockquote>
