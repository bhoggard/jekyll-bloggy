<p>After making a brunch stop to get our invitations to <a href="http://www.thecivilians.org/benefit_2002.html">The Civilians benefit</a> on December 9, we headed downtown for two of <a href="http://www.lmcc.net"><span class="caps">LMCC</span></a>&#8217;s current projects.</p>
<p>The first, called <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/EventsandExhibitions/Exhibitions/Looking_In/LookingIn_main.html">Looking In</a>, consists of quite a few site-specific installations on the ground floor of a new luxury rental building at <a href="http://www.50murray.com/">50 Murray Street</a>.  We mainly went to visit with <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/EventsandExhibitions/Exhibitions/Looking_In/LookingIn_performances.html">Nancy Hwang</a>.  For the current project (through November 9), you can make a reservation by <script type="text/javascript">send_email('nancyhwang','mac.com', 'email')</script> or telephone (917-887-9892) to spend time with her for a nice chat, with free tea or coffee and pastries from <a href="http://www.splendora.com/NewYork/Savories/HauteCakes/178.htm">Ceci-Cela</a>.    Drop-ins are also welcome if she&#8217;s not already busy with someone.  One of these events was how I first met Nancy.  Whether you can schedule a visit with her or not, the art is worth seeing, and you can go anytime, since it&#8217;s all  in windows and other spaces on the ground floor of the building.</p>
<p>Afterward, we went to see <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/Residencies/NewViews_WFC/NV_WFC_Exhibit/NV_WFC_exhibit_main.html">New Views: World Financial Center</a>, the successor to their <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/EventsandExhibitions/WTC_Retrospective/WTC_Retro_main.html"><span class="caps">WTC</span> studio residency</a>.  I went mainly to see <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/Residencies/NewViews_WFC/NV_WFC_Exhibit/NV_WFC_exhibit_instal.html#goldman">Charles Goldman</a>&#8216;s work, but the whole things is really great.  I used to work in the <span class="caps">WTC</span>, and later in the <span class="caps">WFC</span>, and it&#8217;s a very refreshing change to see good art in those hallways.  So much of the time public art exhibits are very dissapointing.  My other favorite works in the show were the videos by <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/Residencies/NewViews_WFC/NV_WFC_Exhibit/NV_WFC_exhibit_instal.html#lindman">Pia Lindman</a>, but all of the work was of really high quality.</p>
