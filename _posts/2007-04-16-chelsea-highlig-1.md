<p>My MacBook is still in the shop, so here is a quick post of my favorite things from last Saturday.  The first two are now &#8220;top picks&#8221; on <a href="http://www.artcal.net/">ArtCal</a>.</p>
<p><img alt="christopher_johnson_gallery.jpg" src="/mt/archives/christopher_johnson_gallery.jpg" width="530" height="398" /></p>
<p>Christopher Lowry Johnson at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4301">Winkleman Gallery</a>.  Hard to show via JPEGs, these are beautiful subtle paintings.  We&#8217;ve been fans for a long time, having bought a painting by Christopher from the very first show at Plus Ultra.  This is the final week, so run over there!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="http://www.artcal.net/static/artcal-images/3/3086.jpeg" alt="Donna Huanca" /></p>
<p>Donna Huanca, <em>Che Guevara&#8217;s Family</em>, 2006, fabric, 82 Ã— 57.5 inches</p>
<p>Donna Huanca at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4506">Susan Inglett Gallery</a>.  We first saw her work at the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/005745.html">&#8220;Manic and Wasted&#8221;</a> show curated by at LMCC&#8217;s Swing Space.  I will probably add some photos to my <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/">Flickr </a>feed once I have my MacBook back, as there are some great sculptures/installations too.  The show also includes a sound installation.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="http://www.artcal.net/static/artcal-images/2/2884.jpeg" alt="Julie Evans" /></p>
<p>Julie Evans, <em>Perch</em>, 2006</p>
<p>Julie Evans at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4291">Julie Saul Gallery</a>.  Gorgeous paintings influenced by Indian miniature painting.  I met her via Edward Winkleman, who has a big <a href="http://edwardwinkleman.blogspot.com/2007/03/julie-evans-julie-saul-gallery.html">post</a> on her work.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="http://www.artcal.net/static/artcal-images/2/2860.jpeg" alt="Lisa Sanditz" /></p>
<p>Lisa Sanditz, <em>Pearl Farm II</em>, 2007, acrylic on canvas, 70 X 90 inches</p>
<p>&#8220;Endangered Wasteland&#8221; at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4271"><span class="caps">CRG</span> Gallery</a>.  Lots of big paintings hovering between abstraction and landscapes.  I especially liked the paintings of Lisa Sanditz.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="http://www.artcal.net/static/artcal-images/3/3011.jpeg" alt="Jae Hi Ahn" /></p>
<p>Jae Hi Ahn at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4369">Phoenix Gallery</a>.  Jae Hi is pushing the limits of what one can do with found plastic objects.  We have one lovely example hanging on wall here at the Hoggard/Wagner Collection.</p>
