<p>From the <em>New York Times</em> <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/07/world/americas/07granado.html">obituary</a> of Alberto Granado, who accompanied Che Guevara on his motorcycle journey around South America.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Before Che left Cuba in 1965 to pursue revolutions abroad, he left several books with inscriptions for close friends. They included one about the sugar industry for Mr. Granado.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>The inscription was prescient.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;My dreams shall know no bounds, at least until bullets decide otherwise,&#8221; wrote Che, who was captured and killed in Bolivia in 1967. &#8220;I&#8217;ll be expecting you, sedentary gypsy, when the smell of gunpowder subsides. A hug for all of you. Che.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
