<p>We visited three worthy groups shows in Chelsea at their openings last Thursday.  Go see them &#8212; not all art is mindless in the summer!</p>
<p><a href="http://www.capsulegallery.com/">Capsule</a> has a big group show titled <i>And one for Grandma</i>.  It was about 100 degrees inside at the opening, but we saw enough to notice several things, including a wall painting by [can&#8217;t remember, nothing on the site], the photograph on the invitation by Christine Callahan, and some great drawings by Andrew Guenther.  I like his work, especially the fact that there is a big variety of work ranging from sculpture to drawings to installations.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.florencelynchgallery.com/">Florence Lynch</a> has a show, <i>minimalpop</i>, curated by Petra Bungert of <span class="caps">CCNOA</span> (Center for Contemporary Non-Objective Art) Brussels.  It includes sculpture by John Beech, whom we finally met at the opening.  We have one of his <a href="http://www.charlottejackson.com/artists/john_beech.htm">rotating paintings</a>.</p>
<p>My favorite work in the show titled <a href="http://www.5begallery.com/content.php?mode=gallery&section_id=32"><i>The day after I destroyed the women, I wished I had not destroyed them</i></a> at Oliver Kamm 5BE (curated by Lital Mehr) doesn&#8217;t photograph well, so I won&#8217;t put it up.  It&#8217;s <i>The Birds and The Bees</i> by Aaron Wexler.  It&#8217;s an amazing painting-like work created from cut paper mounted on wood.  Go by and see it, and ask to see his other works in the back.  Other work includes sculpture and installation by Agata Oleksiak (with dancers wearing some at the opening) and paintings by Tom Meacham.  Tom is also in the current group show at <a href="http://www.nicoleklagsbrun.com/">Nicole Klagsbrun</a>.</p>
<p>Also, don&#8217;t forget that White Box has a new show (and opening) beginning each Wednesday, 6-8pm, through September 1.</p>
