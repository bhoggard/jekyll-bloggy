<p>My work projects are keeping me too busy to write much, but I wanted to mention two shows I saw today in Chinatown that are very much worth seeing.</p>
<p>The first is Phil Collins (the brilliant young English artist I last saw at <a href="/2003/05/intermittent-po-1.html">Apex Art</a>, not the musician) at <a href="http://www.maccarone.net/">Maccarone</a> on Canal Street in Chinatown.  Their web site is barely there, so check out their <a href="http://www.efluxmail.com/archive/displayshow.php?file=message_1064493506.txt">e-flux</a> announcement.  They now have all three floors of their building, formerly occupied mostly by Kunst Electronics.  The first floor has large photographs of the Britney Spears poster that appeared in the subway stations in late 2001, only to be immediately defaced, plus a video of the <span class="caps">BBC</span> arriving to photograph a family of Kosovar refugees in Kosovo &#8212; including having a 15-year-old boy take off his shirt to show his bullet scars.</p>
<p>The second floor has photographs of his lover, his circle of friends, and people in Palestine, Belfast, New York, Belgrade, and various places in England.  The third floor consists of photos from a project called &#8220;real society&#8221;, where he placed an add in San Sebastian, Spain to ask people to come be photographed naked in a luxury hotel suite.</p>
<p>It is a very powerful show.</p>
<p>The other show is a solo exhibit of (mostly) abstract paintings by <a href="http://www.canadanewyork.com/artbin/wallacewhitney/whit_2003/whit_2003.html">Wallace Whitney</a> at <a href="http://www.canadanewyork.com/">Canada</a> on Christie Street.  I don&#8217;t think the images on the web site do it justice, so you&#8217;ll just have to go.  Think a young <a href="http://www.artnet.com/ag/fineartthumbnails.asp?aid=6243">Louise Fishman</a>.</p>
