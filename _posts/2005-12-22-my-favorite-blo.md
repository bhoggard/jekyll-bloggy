<p>[Disclaimer: this post is a bit of a mess and I keep revising it to try to say what I really think.]</p>
<p>It&#8217;s Steve and Jen&#8217;s <a href="http://stevegilliard.blogspot.com/">news blog</a>, especially since Steve and Jen disagree on the strike and are both writing about it.</p>
<p>I started reading this blog again after <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/tommoody/?34299">Tom Moody</a> wrote about the strike and linked to it.</p>
<p>I will say I think the union is managing its PR and strategy rather badly.  We should be reminded constantly of how incompetent/devious the <span class="caps">MTA</span> executives are, including their ability to &#8220;find&#8221; an extra $1 billion in the budget once in a while, or the fact that in a time of supposed terrorist threats, they are closing token booths and forcing us to rely on <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/columnists/ny-nysub244482699oct24,0,2377770.column?coll=ny-rightrail-columnist">turnstile exits</a> that will cause a huge number of deaths in an emergency.  See more by <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/columnists/ny-nysub124423092sep12,0,7302150.column?coll=ny-rightrail-columnist">Ray Sanchez</a> on <span class="caps">MTA</span> security.  Now would be an excellent time for Bloomberg to start pushing to have the <span class="caps">MTA</span> become a city-controlled agency, rather than a state one, but he&#8217;s busy acting more like a billionaire than a mayor at the moment.</p>
<p>I will say I&#8217;m troubled by the actual strike.  I don&#8217;t think it had gotten to the point where a strike was necessary.  I don&#8217;t know what leverage a union has in this age, but a strike by transit workers hurts so many working people in the city.</p>
