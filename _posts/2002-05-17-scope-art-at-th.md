<p>On Sunday we went to brunch &#8211; which one was the mother? &#8211; at <a href="http://newyork.citysearch.com/profile/11456014/">The Park</a> with our friend Brownie, followed by a visit to <a href="http://www.scope-art.com/">Scope Art</a> at the <a href="http://www.gershwinhotel.com/">Gershwin Hotel</a>.  No celebrities were spotted at The Park.  The food wasn&#8217;t bad, and is surprisingly cheap at brunch/lunch time.  If service is going to be that bad, shouldn&#8217;t all of the waiters look like models?  (They don&#8217;t.)</p>
<p>Scope Art was great fun!  Sort of like the Gramercy Art Fair at the beginning &#8212; nicely low key.  There were a number of interesting out of town galleries &#8212; without websites to link!  I was pleased by the amount of drawing and painting on display.  It&#8217;s not dead!</p>
<p>There&#8217;s a very interesting gallery in Cleveland of all places called Shaheen showing people ranging from <a href="http://www.artnet.com/ag/fineartdetail.asp?aid=5900&wid=53954&page=1&group=&max_tn_page=">Roe Ethridge</a> to <a href="http://www.artnet.com/Magazine/reviews/brennan/brennan11-20-10.asp">Blake Rayne</a>.</p>
<p>Others things of note: Stuart Hawkins from <a href="http://priskajuschkafineart.net/">Priska Juschka</a> and a show called &#8220;Amenities&#8221; curated by <a href="http://www.vmga.org/exhibiting/galleries/firehouse.html">Pascal Spengemann</a> &#8212; an entire room of works by artists replacing or working with the objects in the room &#8212; a painting in the Gideon Bible (Ben Potter), toilet paper of handmade paper from utility bills (Missy Bly), and casts of vacuumings from the hotel (Corin Hewitt).</p>
