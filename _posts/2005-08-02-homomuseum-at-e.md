<p>You still have a change to see <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/13/713">Homomuseum</a> at Exit Art, as the show has been extended until August 19.  Each of the artists in the show chose a hero, and their statements are mounted next to their work.  I suspect you won&#8217;t be surprised to learn that Jack Smith is the hero of ak burns.  Rune Olsen chose the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bonobo">Bonobo monkeys</a>, famous for their polysexuality, as a reminder that those who use the word &#8220;unnatural&#8221; to describe homosexuals are denying the way nature really operates.  His sculpture is of two female Bonobos having sex as a younger male looks on.</p>
<p>I didn&#8217;t put up an image, but I also loved the black and white photos of the piers by Alvin Baltrop.  The photographer died in 2004, but there is a foundation web site with <a href="http://www.baltrop.org/black%20and%20white%20photographs%20piers.htm">images</a> from the series.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re into Jack Smith, and who isn&#8217;t these days, also check out the show Jonathan Berger curated at Grimm Rosenfeld, titled <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/974">Founders Day</a>.</p>
