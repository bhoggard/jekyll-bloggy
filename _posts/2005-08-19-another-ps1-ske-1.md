<p>Come on people!  We only have two images in the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/gny2005-sketch">gallery</a> of PS1 Greater New York artist sketches so far, now that I just added <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/tommoody/?32835">Tom Moody&#8217;s entry</a>.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s get some more up there!  You can email images or links to James or me.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s a rather slow art week, but we&#8217;re going to an <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/1017">opening</a> tonight in the East Village of a 2-person show that includes our friend <a href="http://www.derickmelander.com/">Derick Melander</a>.  He curated an awesome <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004180.html">group show</a> in <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> last summer, which is how we met him.</p>

<p><img alt="derick melander wedge" src="/mt/archives/wedge375.jpg" width="329" height="375" /></p>
<p>Derick Melander, <em>Wedge</em></p>
<p>His description:</p>
<blockquote>One of my new works is called <em>Wedge</em>.   It is made from ordinary folded clothing that cascades from the wall to the floor.  The clothing is arranged so that the actual brands embroidered on the clothing tags are displayed one below the next.  As you read the tags, they spell out the following &#8220;Brand Poem&#8221;:<br />
<br />
No Boundaries | 2 | Ecko | 2 | Discus | s<br />
<br />
So&#8230; | U | And I | Underscore | A Line | Barely There<br />
<br />
U | Breakaway | Access<br />
<br />
And I | Report | Zero Exposure</blockquote>
