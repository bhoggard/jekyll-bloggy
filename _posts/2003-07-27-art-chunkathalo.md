<p>Yesterday we went to Williamsburg to see a couple of galleries, but we spent most of the time at the <a href="http://www.bikesummer.org/2003/calendar/detail.php?CalendarID=29">Chunkathalon</a>, organized by <a href="http://www.dclxvi.org/chunk/">C.H.U.N.K 666</a>.  Don&#8217;t miss the photos from <a href="http://www.dclxvi.org/chunk/operations/chunkathalon2002/index.html">the 2002 one</a>.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ll talk about the gallery stuff first.  <a href="http://www.jackthepelicanpresents.com/">Jack the Pelican</a> has a group show called <a href="http://www.jackthepelicanpresents.com/newlawn_pr.html">New Lawn</a> that was my favorite show I&#8217;ve seen there since the opening solo show of <a href="http://www.jackthepelicanpresents.com/bercowetz_pr.html">Jesse Bercowitz</a>.  The best items in the show: the two videos by Susan Ingraham titled &#8220;Iguana&#8221; and &#8220;GOescarGO&#8221;, both with awesome costume and makeup design by the artist and soundtracks inspired by/lifted from French new-wave films.</p>
<p>The other item of note is the <a href="http://www.k-stop.com/innerviews/index.asp">Innerviews</a> project by Karen Schoellkopf in the group show at <a href="http://www.momentaart.org/">Momenta</a>.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>Back to the Chunkathalon &#8212; an event best described as <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Title?0082622">Knightriders</a> meets that short-lived TV show &#8220;Double Rush&#8221;, plus a bit of <a href="http://www.sca.org/">Society for Creative Anachronism</a>.  I have to say though, that the <span class="caps">SCA</span> people at my university where never in <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/chunk2003/IMG_2300">this kind</a> of shape.  My full gallery of photos is <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/chunk2003">here</a>.</p>
<p><a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003446.html">James</a> spent more time on his write-up, photos, and captions, so go check out his entry.</p>
<p>Also, see <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/tommoody/?22936">Tom Moody</a>&#8217;s report.  We finally met each other after he recognized me in the crowd.  I was even wearing sunglasses at the time.</p>
<p><a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/chunk2003/IMG_2286"><img border="0" src="/albums/chunk2003/IMG_2286.thumb.jpg" /></a>&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/chunk2003/IMG_2351"><img border="0" src="/albums/chunk2003/IMG_2351.thumb.jpg" /></a></p>
<p>The Master of Ceremonies, who started out in a wrestling mask, was dressed very <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/chunkathalon/PICT1256">glam</a>.  I have one more (adult) story about something I overheard a young man named <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/chunk2003/IMG_2315">Zach</a> tell his friends.  Those who don&#8217;t wish to read it should stop reading <span class="caps">NOW</span>.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>Zach seemed to be from the West Coast group of bikers &#8212; Portland/Seattle.  He was telling a story about the competition between the bike messengers and the car drivers at one of the companies where he worked, which would occasionally evolve into actual brawls.   One of the &#8220;humiliations&#8221; of a car messenger he bragged about was <a href="http://www.bostonphoenix.com/archive/movies/98/08/06/JOHN_WATERS.html">teabagging</a> someone, plus some &#8220;serious cock and mouth action&#8221;.</p>
