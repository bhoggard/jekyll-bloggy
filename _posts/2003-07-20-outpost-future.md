<p>Last Sunday we went to <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> to see <a href="http://www.dumbo-newyork.com/index.cfm/bay/content.view/catid/182/cpid/621/eventview/1.htm">Outpost</a> at <a href="http://www.smackmellon.org/">Smack Mellon</a> and <a href="http://www.dumboartscenter.org/gallery/future.html">Future Species</a> at <span class="caps">DUMBO</span> Arts Center (<span class="caps">DAC</span>).  The press release for &#8220;Outpost&#8221; is from a real estate site, since Smack Mellon hasn&#8217;t updated their web site in a month.  While you&#8217;re on that site, feel free to buy me one of the penthouses at 30 Main Street as a present.</p>
<p>The <span class="caps">DAC</span> show was a bit disappointing.  The best works in the show, and not just because <a href="/2003/01/new-art-acquisi.html">we own one of them</a>, are the <a href="http://www.dumboartscenter.org/gallery/futureimages/callinan.html">Matthew Callinan</a> sculptures.  They&#8217;re quite magical, hanging in a space with large windows in <span class="caps">DUMBO</span>, and everyone walking into the gallery talked about them and smiled.  I also liked the twisted toy sculptures of <a href="http://www.dumboartscenter.org/gallery/futureimages/krepfle.html">David Krepfle</a>.</p>
<p>The &#8220;Outpost&#8221; show at Smack Mellon, curated by Ada Chisholm, was excellent.  I&#8217;m very sorry I missed <a href="http://www.beigerecords.com/cory/">Cory Arcangel</a>&#8216;s power-point-presentation-with-Van-Halen-guitar-solo at the <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/tommoody/?22613">opening</a>.  His work in the show consists of &#8220;videos&#8221; on <span class="caps">DVD</span> created by tricking QuickTime to interpret memory blocks in his computer as video data.  They&#8217;re quite beautiful.  Greg Simsic, who is also the author of some design and computer books, had a great installation of 11 video monitors stacked on several folding tables.  They showed various activities in his studio &#8212; picking up objects, things getting soaked, hands dipping into paint, etc.  Chad Silver had a funny video in which he looks around his apartment for animals and characters hidden in the patterns of the wall textures, clothing piles, and other places.</p>
