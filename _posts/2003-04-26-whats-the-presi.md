<p>Here is an amazing exchange from yesterday&#8217;s White House <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/news/releases/2003/04/20030425-4.html#8">press briefing</a>.  Does anyone know who the reporter is that asked the question?</p>
<blockquote>Q And if I can just shift gears very briefly, what&#8217;s the President&#8217;s beliefs about homosexuality?<br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: You know, that&#8217;s a question that&#8217;s been put to the President, and if you go back and you look at it, the President has said that, first of all, he doesn&#8217;t ask that question about people. He judges people about who they are, their individual soul. That&#8217;s not a matter the President concerns himself with. He judges people for how they act and how they relate, and that&#8217;s his focus on that.<br />
<br />
Q How they act sexually? Because I asked sexually &#8212;<br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: How they act as a person. The same way &#8212;<br />
<br />
Q But the police in Texas asked how they act sexually.<br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: The same way you would say that about how anybody &#8212; what&#8217;s his reaction to this person or that person &#8212; say, are they a nice person, what kind of person are they? It has nothing to do with their sexuality.<br />
<br />
Q So does he believe that they ought to be free to be themselves, without interference from police?<br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: The President has always said that when it comes to legal matters, that it&#8217;s a question of different groups, homosexual groups, gay groups should not have special rights or special privileges.<br />
<br />
Q Is it a special privilege to be able to love the person you love the way you want to love them, without interference from the police?<br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: If you&#8217;re asking about a matter that is a legal matter that is pending before the Supreme Court, that&#8217;s a matter for the court to rule on, and we&#8217;ll find out what the court says in the specific case in mind.<br />
<br />
Q So he has no position on that?<br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: It&#8217;s a matter that&#8217;s pending before the court, in regard to your last question. </blockquote>
