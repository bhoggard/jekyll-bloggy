<p>I posted a version of this in the comments on a friend&#8217;s web log:</p>
<p>I think the thing people need to realize is that the people that end up at Harvey Milk are, as the administrators put it, ones &#8220;with a history.&#8221; These are generally kids that have reached the point of being physically hurt, kicked out of their homes, etc. These aren&#8217;t kids with a lot of options. In a perfect world we wouldn&#8217;t need such schools, but to say that it is the duty of these vulnerable kids to change the world while those of us who are more privileged wait for a more pure solution is offensive. I am reminded of the idea that it is not the job of the oppressed to educate their oppressors.</p>
<p>Go read <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003451.html">James</a>&#8216;s post on the subject.  I think it&#8217;s one of the best things he has ever written for his weblog.</p>
<p>For some statistics on gay youth and education, check out this <a href="http://www.pflag.org/education/schools.html"><span class="caps">PFLAG</span> page</a>.</p>
