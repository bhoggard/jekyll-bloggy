<p>Over the last few days, I&#8217;ve &#8220;done some art&#8221;, plus seen one movie that wasn&#8217;t quite art, but was entertaining:  I went to a preview screening of <a href="http://www.tlareleasing.com/psyourcatisdead/templates/home.cfm">P.S. Your Cat is Dead</a> from actor/co-writer/director/80s hunk <a href="http://www.steveguttenberg.com/">Steve Guttenberg</a>.  It&#8217;s amusing, but I would probably wait to see it on video if I hadn&#8217;t seen it already.  The screening was organized by <a href="http://www.newfestival.org/">The New Festival</a>, so it was a big gay crowd there to see him in person.  The highlight of the evening was getting to talk to David Drake, whom we hadn&#8217;t seen since attending his <a href="/2002/11/theatre-recomme.html">Son of Drakula</a> at Dance Theater Workshop.  People like David give me hope for homo culture and art &#8212; he&#8217;s a pretty face, but he&#8217;s a smart guy who is much more than a pretty face.  Another one of those is <a href="http://www.thesexfilmproject.com/">John Cameron Mitchell</a>.</p>
<p>OK.  Enough celebrity worship &#8212; on to other things.</p>
<p>Several nights ago I saw a cool evening of music at Merkin.  The first half was a collaboration of &#8220;live animation&#8221; by Pierre Hébert and music by <a href="http://detritus.net/ostertag/">Bob Ostertag</a> called <a href="http://detritus.net/ostertag/garbagelive.html">Between Science and Garbage</a>.  Hébert used an iBook plus web cam and a lot of objects and drawings to gradually assemble an animated film while we watched, using everything from drawings on paper to apples and Coke cans.  They were selling Ostertag&#8217;s CDs in the lobby, so I bought <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B00000IJOD/tristanmedial-20">PantyChrist</a>, which I always knew about but had never heard.  It&#8217;s wonderful &#8212; a collaboration between him and <a href="/2002/09/kiki-and-herb-a-1.html">Justin Bond</a>.  How could you ask for more?  I&#8217;ll put up some MP3s later.</p>
<p>On Friday we went to an opening for <a href="http://www.stacygreene.com/">Stacy Greene</a> at <a href="http://www.plusultragallery.com/2003/stacygreene/pressrelease.html">Plus Ultra</a> in Williamsburg, then hopped back on the L to see puppet wizard <a href="http://www.ps122.org/html/calendar.html#zaloom">Paul Zaloom</a> at P.S. 122 featuring political puppetry and a gay Punch and Judy show, renamed &#8220;Punch and Jimmy&#8221;.</p>
<p>Stacy&#8217;s new work, as she has told me, is a new direction for an artist who already has worked in several media.  We first encountered her at Plus Ultra&#8217;s inaugural show, titled &#8220;Skank&#8221; where we picked up a copy of her <a href="http://www.stacygreene.com/rorschachmain.html">Rorschach Striptease</a> <span class="caps">DVD</span>.  There are 3 photographs of abandoned drive-in movie theaters, then the rest of the works consists of pieces assembled from photgraphs on individual panels to form a sort of collage.  My favorite piece in the show is called &#8220;Los Zapatos de Lorraine&#8221;, in which the title comes from a pair of chintz-patterned shoes belonging to her aunt Lorraine in one of the panels.</p>
<p>Yesterday we went to a few Chelsea galleries.  My favorite things I saw were <a href="http://www.devorahsperber.com/">Devorah Sperber</a> at <a href="http://www.carengoldenfineart.com/">Caren Golden</a>, Michael Wetzel at <a href="http://www.clementine-gallery.com/wetzel.html">Clementine</a>, and Kevin Landers at <a href="http://www.elizabethdeegallery.com/artists/kevin_landers/index.html">Elizabeth Dee</a>.</p>
<p>Today we&#8217;re headed out to Williamsburg to see some shows.</p>
