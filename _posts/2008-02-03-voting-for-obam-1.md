<p>This Tuesday when I vote in the Democratic primary, I&#8217;ll be voting for Barack Obama since my only choice is either Hillary Clinton or him.   I would have voted for Dennis Kucinich if he were still in the race. I will admit that I have some concerns about Obama&#8217;s tendency to <a href="http://www.americablog.com/2008/01/obama-and-gays-again.html">consort with anti-gay bigots</a>.</p>
<p>My main reasons for voting against Hillary Clinton:</p>
<ul>
	<li>She voted for the Iraq War.  I think she did it cynically.  If she could be fooled by the Bush administration&#8217;s &#8220;intelligence&#8221; she is too stupid to be President, but I think she was actually just doing it because she thought it was somehow good politics.  Never mind that the overwhelming majority of her constituents (including a million plus demonstrators in the streets of <span class="caps">NYC</span>) thought she should vote no.</li>
	<li>She voted for the <span class="caps">PATRIOT</span> Act.</li>
	<li>She supports the Defense of Marriage Act, again cynically arguing that it was good politics to ward off a constitutional amendment.  Really?!  Bring it on.  I would like to have seen the anti-gay right try to get that passed in 3/4 of the states in this country.  I would welcome that discussion of whether this is really what the <span class="caps">USA</span> wants for its constitution.</li>
	<li>She says that gay marriages / civil unions should be left to the states.  Given that federal law covers Social Security, inheritance, and taxes, that is a deeply flawed position.  I have not seen her argue that other anti-discrimination laws, or abortion rights, should be decided at state level.  Did you know that covering your married spouse&#8217;s health insurance through your employer is not taxable, but that it <a href="http://www.glennalicious.org/archives/2008/01/the_really_angr.html">counts as income</a> if it&#8217;s via a domestic partnership?  Tell that to your states&#8217; rights friends.</li>
</ul>
