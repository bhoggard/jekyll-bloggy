<p>Ha&#8217;aretz has a good <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/pages/ShArt.jhtml?itemNo=270513">column</a> by an Israeli on the deaths of civilians at the hands of the Israeli Army.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ll quote the end:</p>
<blockquote>Israel&#8217;s efforts at obfuscation are intended above all to keep our conscience clean. This is false posturing that can no longer be countenanced. When the <span class="caps">IDF</span> demolished a mosque on the &#8220;Philadelphi&#8221; route on the outskirts of Rafah and immediately claimed that the building had been abandoned, no one asked why the mosque had been abandoned. Here is the real sequence of events: first the Gaza Strip is occupied, then settlements are established in it, then guarded roads are built to protect the settlers. In the next stage, after the Palestinians begin to rebel violently against the occupation, we begin killing them until they are forced to abandon the mosque and indeed the entire area. Finally the &#8220;abandoned&#8221; mosque is demolished. But to us it seems that only the Palestinians destroy holy places, such as Joseph&#8217;s Tomb. Our conscience is pure and unblemished, always.</blockquote>
