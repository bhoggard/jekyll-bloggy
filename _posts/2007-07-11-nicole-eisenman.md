<p>Nicole Eisenman, <em>Little Boy Walking Big Dog</em>, 2007, Oil paint and mixed media on wood, 17 &#215; 18.5 inches</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>I loved this sculpture/painting we saw at Smith-Stewart&#8217;s show titled <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/5012"><i>She was born to be my unicorn</i>.</a>  The curator <a href="http://www.teenageunicorn.com/">Amy Kellner</a> describes it as the flipside of the Bob Nickas &#8220;cute boy energy&#8221; show at Team in 2003, <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003714.html"><i>my people were fair and had cum in their hair (but now they&#8217;re content to spray stars from your boughs)</i></a>.  I also really liked the papillon ink and watercolor piece by J Penry, who is in a 3-person show opening Friday at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/5064">V&amp;A</a> on the Lower East Side.</p>
<p>Sorry for the lack of posting.  It&#8217;s been too hot to think, and I&#8217;m working on some new things for <a href="http://www.artcat.com/">ArtCat</a> and <a href="http://www.artcal.net/">ArtCal</a>.</p>
