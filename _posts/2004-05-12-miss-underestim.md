<p>I didn&#8217;t realized there was actually a book that uses that horrible neologism &#8220;misunderestimated&#8221; as a title.  When I mentioned it to <a href="http://jameswagner.com/">James</a>, he said, &#8220;that sounds like a drag queen!&#8221;</p>
<p>There  really is a book, titled <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0060723831/tristanmedial-20"><i>Misunderestimated: The President Battles Terrorism, John Kerry, and the Bush Haters</i></a> by a &#8220;reporter&#8221; for the Rev. Moon-owned Washingon Times.</p>
<p>Hear that sound? It&#8217;s the sound of all of our IQs plummeting.</p>
<p>More on the man who cannot speak can be found <a href="http://slate.msn.com/id/2100064/">here</a>.  Also an <a href="http://observer.guardian.co.uk/international/story/0,6903,1195931,00.html">article</a> from The Observer gives us a choice quote from Karen Hughes:</p>
<blockquote>&#8220;I remember one time he had mispronounced one of his words,&#8221; she said, looming over the podium. &#8220;It was not the time he said &#8216;subliminable&#8217; or &#8216;strategery.&#8217; This time, it was &#8216;misunderestimate.&#8217; He said it three times, so I kinda had to point it out to him.&#8221; She paused to let the friendly laughter wash over her. &#8220;The same morning, he had called the terrorists &#8216;folks.&#8217; And it fell to me to say, &#8216;You know, Mr. President, uh, these are trained killers, I&#8217;m not sure you really want to be calling them &#8216;folks.&#8217;&#8221;</blockquote>
