<p>I went to the opening of <a href="http://dks.thing.net/TODAYSMAN.html">Today&#8217;s Man</a> at <a href="http://www.johnconnellypresents.com/">John Connelly Presents</a>.  Due to the fact that the <span class="caps">MTA</span> decided to shut down the L-train from Eighth Avenue to Lorimer Street(!) the crowd took a while to assemble, which meant those of us arriving near 6 could actually see the work, a rather unusual occurrence at his <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/nyc-2002-12/aac">openings</a>.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s a very good show, with about 50 works by male artists on male subjects.  As <a href="http://jameswagner.com/">James</a> pointed out, there isn&#8217;t a lot of nudity, as you might expect at such a show.  The link above on Douglas Kelley&#8217;s web site lists all of the artists, so I&#8217;ll only mention the works that struck me:</p>
<ul>
<li>A dark oil painting of a boy on a windowsill by <a href="/2003/06/art-rain-art-ra.html">Tim Lokiec</a></li>
<li>A drawing by <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003376.html">Paul P.</a></li>
<li>A collage by <a href="http://www.damelioterras.com/artists_art.asp?art_code=58">Tony Feher</a> that included a sexy ass shot of someone, a coffee cup lid, an cigarette butt, and a prescription label &#8212; hey, he and James have the same doctor!</li>
<li>A beautiful drawing (on vellum?) by Assume Vivid Astro Focus/Eli Sudbrack</li>
<li>A drawing of a boy in a rowboat about to be attacked by a tentacled monster by Hernan Bas</li>
<li>A collage by <a href="/2003/01/artsapoppin.html">Christian Holstad</a></li>
<li>A delicate drawing by Nick Mauss</li>
<li>A painting by <a href="http://www.clementine-gallery.com/wetzel.html">Michael Wetzel</a> &#8212; I really like his work the more I see it</li>
</ul>
<p>Speaking of Paul P, check out the nice <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/07/18/arts/design/18GALL.html?pagewanted=print&position=">review</a> he got from Holland Cotter.  Knowing that Paul loves Whistler, getting compared to Whistler and Caravaggio&#8217;s &#8220;punk angels&#8221; is a good thing.  He was down from Toronto for the opening.</p>
<p>On the subject of reviews, in a week when even the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/003030.html">troglodyte</a> Michael Kimmelman reviews this sort of work (see the Holland Cotter link above), it shocked me to see New York Data Probe&#8217;s <a href="http://nydataprobe.blogspot.com/2003_07_01_nydataprobe_archive.html#105856081791401718">dismissive comments</a> on the John Connelly show.  There is a fine line between amusing  <a href="http://www.gawker.com/">Gawker</a>-esque snarkiness and being a philistine.  I think <span class="caps">NYDP</span> has crossed that line.  Quote:</p>
<blockquote>John Connelly Presents presents its second opening in two weeks, for the show &#8220;Today&#8217;s Man,&#8221; with 49 artists, not many of them artists by career. <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
Lucky for me that this is basically a rerun of the last opening, because I can&#8217;t be there&#8212;I&#8217;m going to the Hamptons.</blockquote>
<p>What does &#8220;not many of them artists by career&#8221; mean?  I&#8217;ve seen most of them in galleries whose taste I respect.  Are they not &#8220;by career&#8221; unless they&#8217;re making a good living at it like Ross Bleckner or Annie Leibovitz?  Ugh.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s also poor journalism to say &#8220;a rerun of the last opening&#8221; when there isn&#8217;t that much overlap.  I assume he is referring to the work that John is <a href="/2003/07/a-couple-of-pho.html">showing</a> at D&#8217;Amelio Terras, and not the last show in his space with <a href="/2003/06/fresh-tracks-at.html">Dearraindrop</a>.</p>
<p>What&#8217;s the point of having an art blog if your writing is even more sloppy than the print journalists?  I&#8217;m no <a href="http://modernartnotes.blogspot.com/">Tyler Greene</a> or <a href="http://www.digitalmediatree.com/tommoody/">Tom Moody</a>, but I would be embarassed to write such a thing without some <a href="http://www.marcnorth.com/archives/general/000350.html">air quotes</a>.</p>
