<p>While talking about the <a href="http://www.dixonplace.org/calday.html#90001">Dixon Place Queer Hot Festival</a>, I mentioned that <a href="http://www.queenmother.tv/nycgirl/zoraya/zora.html">Armen Ra</a> was one of the performers.  (See <a href="http://www.villagevoice.com/issues/0225/musto.php">Musto</a> on her.)  She performs on the <a href="http://theremin.info/">theremin</a>, which is a pretty fascinating instrument invented in 1919.</p>
<p>While contemplating that such an amazing piece of musical technology was invented so long ago, I started to think about the conundrum that is America.  What has made us great is the ability to ignore history and forge ahead, more than any culture in history.  However, I think this &#8220;ahistorical&#8221; attitude is also our biggest weakness.  We&#8217;re like children, thinking that each thing we discover is the first time anyone ever thought of it.  If we had any idea what other cultures have accomplished, from the <a href="http://ancient-rome.tripod.com/">Romans</a>, to post-war Europe, which created great welfare states capable of providing basic healthcare for everyone (in countries devastated by war), we could learn from those achievements and build on them.</p>
