<p>The hotel only has dial-up in the rooms, so posting won&#8217;t happen too often, or be very link-ish.</p>
<p>On Saturday we went to some of the Chung King galleries.  Highlights:</p>
<p>Brad Borevitz&#8217;s video titled <i>PornoCopia: 10 Machines for Fucking Your Eyes Out and 1 Romantic Interlude</i>, which uses wacky image manipulation, like screens which break into geometric &#8220;shards&#8221; with the original source material being hard-core gay porn.  Good soundtrack too of various mixed music.  At <a href="http://www.4-fgallery.com/">4-F gallery</a>.</p>
<p>Terence Koh at <a href="http://www.peresprojects.com/">Peres Projects</a>.  You may know of his previous &#8220;incarnation&#8221;, asianpunkboy.</p>
<p>Rosson Crow, Andrew Guenther, and LoVid at <a href="http://www.thehappylion.com/index.php?exhibitions=040710_040814">The Happy Lion</a>.</p>
<p>We also went to the minimalism show at <span class="caps">MOCA</span>, which was very good.  We spent so much time there we didn&#8217;t make it to <a href="http://www.sixspace.com/">sixspace</a>, but we did have drinks with Caryn, Sean, and their friend Mike later at the beautiful Millenium Biltmore.</p>
<p>After drinks we had dinner at <a href="http://www.standardhotel.com/">The Standard Downtown</a>&#8216;s &#8220;coffee shop.&#8221;  I was surprised by how great the food was, given the over-the-top scene.  They have a Calder mobile and a Jenny Holzer scrolling <span class="caps">LED</span> text piece in the lobby, along with a pool table and plenty of bouncers.  Our waitress was like Suzanne Sommers&#8217;s character on Three&#8217;s Company, but maybe 10-15 years later.  We loved her.  At one point, when she started to pour more wine for us, we told her, &#8220;That&#8217;s OK.  We&#8217;ll pour it.&#8221;  Usually, people are offended when we do that.  This time, the response was, &#8220;Thank you!  I appreciate that!&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>Updated:  Oops, left off one great show: <i>Tapestry From An Asteroid</i> at <a href="http://www.davidkordanskygallery.com/">David Kordansky</a>, with some of the artists we know from <span class="caps">NYC</span> galleries like Foxy Production, Daniel Reich, and John Connelly Presents.  I especially liked the sculpture by Sterling Ruby.</p>
