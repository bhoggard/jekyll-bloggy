<p>At the entrance of the <a href="http://chelseaartmuseum.org/">Chelsea Art Museum</a></p>

<p>One would hope that a pairing of a show of &#8220;celebrity art&#8221; curated by Baird Jones, and one of Goya&#8217;s <a href="http://www.wesleyan.edu/dac/coll/grps/goya/goya_intro.html"><em>Los Caprichos</em></a> along with contemporary art, curated by the brilliant <a href="http://www.elgawimmer.com/">Elga Wimmer</a>, is ironic.  I showed up at the museum wearing a t-shirt, khakis, and my favorite <a href="http://adbusters.org/metas/corpo/blackspotsneaker/orderv1.html">sneakers</a>. I knew I would be headed to <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/818&#39;s">Monya Rowe</a> opening later, which I expected to be warmish, given the 20+ artists in the show for a moderately-sized space.  Someone at the museum entrance looked at me, and said, &#8220;You&#8217;re here for the Goya?&#8221;  The scary guys with <a href="http://www.blackberry.com/">Blackberries</a>, briefcases, and bleached-blonde bimbos in tow where pointed to the other show.</p>
