<p>Due to our previous <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/006857.html">coverage</a> of &#8220;photography not allowed&#8221;  policies, <a href="http://ionarts.blogspot.com/">blogger</a> and artist <a href="http://www.markbarryportfolio.com/">Mark Barry</a> forwarded an email he just received from 303 Gallery regarding 2 images on Flickr from his <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/markart/sets/72057594079826604/">set</a> from the 2006 Armory Show.  The photos were taken during the press preview.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>From: Simon Greenberg<br />
Date: May 7, 2008 10:06:33 AM <span class="caps">EDT</span><br />
To: mark@markbarryportfolio.com<br />
Subject: Maureen Gallace image &#8211; flickr</p>
<p>hello mark -</p>
<p>this is simon at 303 gallery. i noticed you had an image of Maureen<br />
Gallace&#8217;s work up on your flickr page &#8211; please be aware that 303 Gallery<br />
owns the copyright to the work and all public display of images, including<br />
web content. if you could kindly remove this image from your page, it would<br />
be most appreciated.</p>
<p>best<br />
simon</blockquote></p>
<p>This is one of the more infuriating things I have seen from an art gallery lately.  Do the gallery&#8217;s artists know that they&#8217;re spending this kind of time trolling the web and harassing bloggers?  It hardly seems like a good use of resources.</p>
