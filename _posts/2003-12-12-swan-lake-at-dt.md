<p>There is one more night of <a href="http://www.dtw.org/2003fall/swan_lake_03.htm">Swan Lake</a> at Dance Theatre Workshop &#8212; Saturday the 13th at 7pm.  We saw it tonight, and I <b>highly</b> recommend it.  It&#8217;s a dance theatre work from the <a href="http://www.vonkrahl.ee/en/">Von Krahl Theatre</a> involving Estonian and Russian artists and plays off of the fact that totalitarian regimes have found the &#8220;high art&#8221; form of ballet a useful art form for their purposes.  The music (by Sergei Zagny and recorded by <a href="http://www.dtw.org/2003fall/swan_lake_03.htm"><span class="caps">NYYD</span></a>), dancing, and design are all brilliant.  It&#8217;s part of the <a href="/2003/12/dancetheatre-re.html">Central Station</a> series which features work from Central and Eastern Europe.</p>
<p>P.S.  Not only were we there tonight, so was Mikhail Baryshnikov.</p>
