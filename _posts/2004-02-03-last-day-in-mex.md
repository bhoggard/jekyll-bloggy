<p>On our last day we explored more of the downtown area.  We went to the <a href="http://www.frommers.com/destinations/moreattract.cfm?a_id=24282">Palacio Nacional</a> (National Palace), on the Zócalo, to see the great murals of Diego Rivera, the rooms where Benito Juarez lived, and the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/mexico-city-2004/IMG_2658">parliament room</a> (I think that&#8217;s the right name).  There are some images of the Rivera murals <a href="http://www.interamericaninstitute.org/images1.htm">here</a>.  Oops, I just realized I never talked about the <a href="http://www.mexicocity.com.mx/bellasar.html">Palacio de Bellas Artes</a> (Palace of Fine Arts).  It is filled with great murals by Rivera, Siqueiros, and Orozco.  It has halls for performances, like Lincoln Center, but it is amazing to see that the murals are all very left wing, with images of Marx, and attacks on capitalists.  I think Lincoln Center could use a few of those.</p>
<p>We also saw the <a href="http://www.mexicocity.com.mx/cated_i.html">Cathedral</a> (including women selling &#8220;relics&#8221;, bits of a saint&#8217;s skulll from a table set up inside, and the <a href="http://www.mexicocity.com.mx/history1.html#Templo%20Mayor">Templo Mayor</a>.  In the same area, and definitely worth a visit, is the <a href="http://www.mexicocity.com.mx/santdom.html">Plaza Santo Domingo</a>, surrounded by very old <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/mexico-city-2004/IMG_2693">buildings</a>.  It is also the location of an <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/mexico-city-2004/IMG_2688">arcade</a> with &#8220;scribes&#8221;, all equipped with typewriters to help people compose letters, or fill out official forms.  I was surprised to see they were all (except one) using electric ones now.  The last time I had read an article on the area, they talked about old manual ones.</p>
<p>We had a great lunch at Casa de las Sirenas, Guatemala 61, just behind the Cathedral.   It is in 17th century building.  James has some <a href="http://jameswagner.com/gallery/mexico-city-2004?page=2">photos</a> of it.</p>
