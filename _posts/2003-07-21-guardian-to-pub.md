<p>I was reminded that I hadn&#8217;t posted anything about this when I saw the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/07/21/business/media/21GUAR.html">article</a> in the NY Times today.  The <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/">Guardian</a>, a great leftish British newspaper, is considering an American weekly magazine.  I would buy several gift subscriptions right away?  Not only is their political and world events coverage great, they are one of the smartest voices covering <a href="http://books.guardian.co.uk/">books</a> and the rest of the <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/arts/">arts</a>.  For an example, see this <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/arts/fridayreview/story/0,12102,975837,00.html">article </a> on Strauss&#8217;s opera Die Schweigsame Frau (The Silent Woman):</p>
<blockquote>The librettist was Jewish. The composer was head of the Reich Music Chamber. But Stefan Zweig and Richard Strauss still managed to beat the Nazis and get their comedy sea opera on stage. </blockquote> 
<p>New York Magazine had an article by Michael Wolff titled <a title="En Guardian!" href="http://www.newyorkmetro.com/nymetro/news/media/columns/medialife/n_8938/">En Guardian!</a> on this a couple of weeks ago.</p>
