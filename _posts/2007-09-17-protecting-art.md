<p>I see that at least two galleries, both of whom have a significant number of artists with street art or illustration backgrounds, think taking photos in their galleries is a bad thing.  What are they afraid of?  Do they think any of us is going to go home and upload  images to <a href="http://www.cafepress.com/">CafePress</a> to sell t-shirts?</p>
<p>James and I encountered a <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/006647.html">rather hostile reception</a> at Capla Kesting after he took some photos of <a href="http://www.goldmineshithouse.com/about/profile_travis.htm">Travis Lindquist</a>&#8216;s exhibition, and were told to never return.  Didn&#8217;t they found the <a href="http://www.fountainexhibit.com/">Fountain Art Fair</a>, and invite us to attend as press?</p>
<p>Jonathan LeVine Gallery &#8212; who shows <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/4967">Shepard Fairey</a> for heaven&#8217;s sake! &#8212; now has a policy of no photos too.  I learned that from <a href="http://heartasarena.blogspot.com/2007/09/ok.html">Heart as Arena</a>.</p>
