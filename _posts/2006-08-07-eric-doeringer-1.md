<p>James is quoted in an <a href="http://www.variety.com/index.asp?layout=weekend&amp;content=jump&amp;jump=article&amp;articleID=VR1117947945&amp;category=2046">article</a> in <em>Variety Weekend</em> about <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004745.html">Eric Doeringer</a>.  I have to say it&#8217;s better written than most press I&#8217;ve read about Eric&#8217;s work.  Some choice quotes:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;I love the humor in the work,&#8221; says longtime collector James Wagner, who, with partner Barry Hoggard, has a large collection of contemporary pieces with political and humorous themes. &#8220;We don&#8217;t buy Eric&#8217;s work instead of other art. We buy it because we enjoy the conceptual strategy behind it and the politics that seem to address issues of branding and star systems.&#8221;</p>
<p>That idea finds even greater clarity in Doeringer&#8217;s &#8220;Cremaster Fanatic,&#8221; a faux fan site devoted to Matthew Barney, the art-world superstar best known for his inscrutable, epic-length films.</p>
<p>&#8220;Barney epitomizes the notion of an artist as a celebrity,&#8221; says Doeringer. &#8220;And there&#8217;s this whole notion that the art world is supposed to be intellectual and above something like rabid fandom.&#8221;</p>
<p>Ironic? Sure. That&#8217;s why Doeringer tends to invoke Andy Kauffman rather than Andy Warhol in describing his influences. &#8220;With the best of [Kauffman],&#8221; he says, &#8220;you were never really sure if it was made up or the real thing.&#8221;</blockquote></p>
