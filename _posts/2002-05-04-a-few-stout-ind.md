<p>Last night we saw John Guare&#8217;s <u>A Few Stout Individuals</u> at <a href="http://www.signaturetheatre.org/">Signature Theatre Company</a>.  While not a perfect play (the ending seems to peter out), it&#8217;s a very clever rumination on history, and has one of the best casts I&#8217;ve even seen in a play.  I think he was trying to reach for more surrealism than he was able to pull off &#8212; maybe he&#8217;s afraid of confusing the kind of audience he has now.  I didn&#8217;t find it particularly unusual as a play, but the generally 60ish crowd around me all talked about how &#8220;weird&#8221; it was.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s a huge cast (13 actors) and is very well directed.  The standouts included <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Holliday,+Polly">Polly Holliday</a>, <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Moffat,+Donald">Donald Moffat</a>, and <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Sadler,+William">William Sadler</a> as a perfect Samuel Clemens.  I felt like Mark Twain had returned from the dead.  The woman who played the great diva Adelina Patti (Cheryl Evans) was a real opera singer &#8212; I saw her in Akhnaten a couple of years ago in <a href="http://www.blo.org/">Boston</a>.  <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Yaegashi,+James">James Yaegashi</a> was quite dashing as The Emperor of Japan.</p>
<p>I had never seen Donald Moffat on stage &#8212; he has a great presence as a physical actor and made a believable U.S. Grant at the end of his life.</p>
<p>The title comes from a quote by <a href="http://www.rwe.org/">Ralph Waldo Emerson</a>: &#8220;all history resolves itself very easily into the biography of a few stout and earnest persons&#8221;.</p>
<p>You can get cheaper tickets right now while it&#8217;s in previews.  For my impoverished actor/artist friends, I recommend calling to see if you can usher/volunteer to see it.  I think it&#8217;s worth seeing just for the cast.</p>
<p>One more thing:  The actress playing the Empress of Japan, <a href="http://www.lajollaplayhouse.com/history/1996/boy_photos.htm">Michi Barall</a>, kept looking at James and me as if she knew or recognized us; we were in the front row.  Hmm&#8230; other than in plays, do I know her?</p>
