<p>James and I don&#8217;t only follow the purely visual arts.  We attend a lot of theater, dance, and other performance.  The next few weeks have a lot of things of interest. <p><strong>Ensemble Pi: The Rest is Silence</strong></p>
<p>Saturday March 1st, 2008 at 8pm<br />
Tickets at the door $15.</p>
<p>Venue:<br />
The Great Hall at Cooper Union<br />
7 East 7th Street at Third Avenue<br />
<a href="http://maps.google.com/maps?q=7+east+7th+street&ie=UTF8&oe=utf-8&client=firefox-a&ll=40.728901,-73.990066&spn=0.010309,0.024526&z=16">map</a></p>
<p>Program:</p>
<ul>
	<li>William Kentridge, Philip Miller: Two Shorts from Nine Projections featuring a live performance of original score for string quartet, trumpet and piano (2003)</li>
	<li>Frederic Rzewski: Johnny Has Gone for a Soldier, for piano (2003) U.S. premiere</li>
	<li>John Harbison: Abu Ghraib, for cello and piano (2006) N.Y. premiere</li>
	<li>Kristin Norderval: Far From Home, for two voices and computer-generated sound (2007)</li>
	<li>Dmitri Shostakovich: Piano trio No 2 in E minor, opus 67 (1944)</li>
</ul>
<p>Guest Speaker: Naomi Wolf, author: <em>The End of America</em></p>
