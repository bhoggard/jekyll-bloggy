<p>I&#8217;ve been reading the comments over on Edward Winkleman&#8217;s <a href="http://edwardwinkleman.blogspot.com/2006/09/galleriesplease-note.html">blog post</a> about galleries and diversity, particularly regarding gender.</p>
<p>James and I don&#8217;t really think about whether the shows we see, or the art we buy, are by women or men generally.  Sometimes we realize we have written about only women for a bit on our blogs, and certainly our collection has many women in it.  Our records aren&#8217;t organized enough for us to tell you percentages.</p>
<p>When it came time to curate our first shows at Dam, Stuhltrager, we wanted to make sure we didn&#8217;t have an all straight white male show.  That doesn&#8217;t describe the people we know and live around, so it didn&#8217;t really make sense to have a group of artists like that.</p>
<p>I will say we struggled with the idea of thinking about diversity without choosing artists by &#8220;category,&#8221; or expecting a specific person to be some kind of representative of a group.  I think setting out with some kind of quota system wouldn&#8217;t really have been in the spirit of the way we explore the art world and find new things.</p>
<p>Come to the next opening on October 13th, and check out the works.  We have a title now:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/3175"><em>Dangling Between The Real Thing And The Sign In The Window</em></a></p>
<p>The title is inspired by this Morton Feldman <a href="http://ublib.buffalo.edu/libraries/units/music/spcoll/feldman/mfslee326.html">lecture</a>.</p>
<p>The lists of artists is: Jaishri Abichandani, Ina Diane Archer, Peter Corrie, Susan C. Dessel, Nicolas Garait, Joy Garnett, and Jacques Louis Vidal.</p>
<p>Susan C. Dessel&#8217;s <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/2969">installation</a> will still be in the backyard sculpture garden during the run of the &#8220;Dangling&#8221; show.</p>
