<p>NY Times columnist Maureen Dowd <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/08/28/opinion/28DOWD.html">announces</a>, &#8220;I&#8217;m with Dick! Let&#8217;s Make War.&#8221; She admits she was dubious at first, but says that Cheney&#8217;s vision &#8212; as the vice-president put it, &#8220;a government that is democratic and pluralistic, a nation where the human rights of every ethnic and religious group are recognized&#8221; &#8212; convinced her. &#8220;I&#8217;m on board,&#8221; she says. &#8220;Let&#8217;s declare war on Saudi Arabia!&#8221;</p>
<p>My favorite part:<br />
<blockquote> The Saudis would probably use surrogates to fight anyway. They pay poor workers from other countries to do their menial labor. And they paid the Americans to fight the Iraqis in 1991. The joke among the American forces then was: &quot;What&#8217;s the Saudi national anthem? &#8216;Onward, Christian Soldiers.&#8217; &quot;</blockquote></p>
