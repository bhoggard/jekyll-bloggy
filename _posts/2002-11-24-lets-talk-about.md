<p>On Friday we had a studio visit with <a href="http://www.creative-capital.org/artists/visual/holland_fred/holland_fred.html">Fred Holland</a>, an artist with the <a href="http://www.ps1.org/cut/ca.html">P.S.1 Studio Program</a>.  We had first seen his work, and met him, at the <a href="http://www.momentaart.org/pas_pro/holland.html">Momenta</a> benefit.  In his honor we had the curried onion fritters at <a href="http://www.lezincnyc.com/">Le Zinc</a> afterward, spotting <a href="http://www.artretran.com/newsite/EXHIBITIONS/Sep2000/ProjectSpace2/projectSpace2.html">Michael Dvorkin</a> having lunch.</p>
<p>This afternoon we were planning to see the &#8220;Time to Hope&#8221; exhibit at <a href="http://www.stjohndivine.org/">St. John the Divine</a> &#8212; which includes works by Goya and El Greco &#8212; but the wait was too long, so we just spent some time looking at the cathedral instead, and walked north to see a less than exciting show at <a href="http://www.elproyecto.com/">The Project</a>.  We then headed back downtown to see a show I highly recommend: &#8220;Regarding Gloria&#8221; at <a href="http://www.whitecolumns.org/">White Columns</a>.  I will add some links to some of my favorite works from the show once they put some up!</p>
<p>We also <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/002981.html">went to only two</a> (unfortunately not more) programs at the Mix Festival.</p>
