<p>Congratulations to Chris Caccamise and <a href="http://www.sixtysevengallery.com/">*sixtyseven gallery</a> for a fabulous review by <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/09/19/arts/design/19GALL.html">Roberta Smith</a> in Today&#8217;s NY Times.  It&#8217;s a fun show that made me giggle a bit when I walked into the room.</p>
<p>The other highlight of our tour on Monday of Williamsburg galleries with the &#8220;charming French boys&#8221; was Chris Doyle&#8217;s very strong show of watercolors at <a href="http://www.jessicamurrayprojects.com/currentExhibit.html">Jessica Murray Projects</a> &#8212; huge works based on video stills of his life with his partner and daughter.</p>

<p>__</p>
<p>Tonight is a good night to head out to Williamsburg.  All of the <a href="http://www.williamsburggalleryassociation.com/">Williamsburg Gallery Association</a> galleries will be open until 9, and many of them will have music, as part of their new &#8220;Third Friday&#8221; events.  Do not miss the opening of <a href="http://www.plusultragallery.com/">Plus Ultra</a>&#8217;s new, larger space, and make sure you go around the corner to check out Dam, Stuhltrager at 38 Marcy Ave.  The Plus Ultra show, titled &#8220;arts and letters,&#8221; features drawings and paintings by Jonathan Ames, Dave Eggers, Susan Minot, and Will Self.</p>
