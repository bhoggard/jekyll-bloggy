<p>From the <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/politics/6311097.stm"><span class="caps">BBC</span></a>:</p>
<blockquote>Downing Street has said there will be no exemption from anti-discrimination laws for Catholic adoption agencies.<br />
<br />
But Tony Blair said they would get 21 months to prepare for change, calling this a &#8220;sensible compromise&#8221;.<br />
<br />
Adoption agencies had warned they would close rather than place children with gay couples, saying that went against their beliefs. <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
The proposed measures are likely to face a vote in Parliament next month before coming into effect on 6 April.<br />
<br />
Mr Blair said he believed ministers had found a &#8220;way through&#8221; to prevent discrimination and protect the interests of children, which all &#8220;reasonable people&#8221; should be able to accept.<br />
<br />
&#8220;There is no place in our society for discrimination. That&#8217;s why I support the right of gay couples to apply to adopt like any other couple.<br />
<br />
&#8220;And that way there can be no exemptions for faith-based adoption agencies offering public funded services from regulations that prevent discrimination.&#8221; </blockquote>
<p>This sort of thing seems pretty unlikely in the <span class="caps">USA</span>, and we don&#8217;t even have a head of state who is also the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_england">head of the official church</a>!</p>
