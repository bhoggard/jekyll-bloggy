<p>The words &#8220;progressive&#8221; and &#8220;convervative&#8221; (except for the use of &#8220;fiscally conservative&#8221;) do not appear on <a href="http://www.peterhort.com/">Peter Hort&#8217;s web site</a>.  However, the word &#8220;progressive&#8221; is used in mailings sent out by his supporters (more on that in another post), and in his responses on this web site to my previous posts about him.</p>
<p>When I wrote about his campaign for Congress <a href="/2004/09/peter-hort-stea.html">here</a> and <a href="/2004/09/on-peter-hort-a.html">here</a>, I hadn&#8217;t realized that he was also running on the <a href="http://www.cpnys.org/">New York Conservative Party</a> line.  The Conservative Party is anti-choice and anti-gay.  Not only are they opposed to gay marriage, they are <a href="http://www.cpnys.org/memos/2004/LM2004-19.html">opposed to civil unions</a>.  Their Senate candidate, Marilyn O&#8217;Grady, is running a television advertisement portraying Senator Schumer and his Republican opponent as gay grooms <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/local/longisland/politics/ny-usogra05q3995622oct05,0,6697500.story?coll=ny-lipolitics-print">atop a wedding cake</a>.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Sen. Charles Schumer hit the airwaves yesterday with the first TV ad of his front-running re-election effort even as a challenger from Garden City launched a spot portraying the Democratic incumbent and another challenger atop a gay marriage wedding cake.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Citing support by Schumer and Republican challenger Howard Mills for gay civil unions and abortion rights, Conservative Party candidate Marilyn O&#8217;Grady&#8217;s TV spot featured male figurines representing the two men atop the cake.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;Schumer and Mills, the perfect liberal couple,&#8221; O&#8217;Grady&#8217;s 30-second ad states. &#8220;Only conservative Marilyn O&#8217;Grady stands with President Bush to defend marriage and protect the unborn.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Mr. Hort&#8217;s web site makes no mention of his being on the Conservative Party ticket. The campaign materials I&#8217;ve seen in my neighborhood of Chelsea don&#8217;t mention the Republican or Conservative parties.  I had to visit the <a href="http://www.vote.nyc.ny.us/candidatesList.jsp"><span class="caps">NYC</span> Board of Elections</a> to find that out.  His web site <a href="http://www.peterhort.com/Issues.asp">issues page</a> says nothing about civil rights, gay or otherwise, and abortion rights.</p>
<p>Isn&#8217;t it fair for New Yorkers to ask how someone can run on the Republican and Conservative Party lines and claim to be a progressive candidate?  Was it merely expedient to get on those ticket lines, and he doesn&#8217;t agree with their positions?  Or does he believe them but won&#8217;t say that in his campaign literature?  Neither answer is one that should win votes.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><span class="caps">UPDATE</span>:  James says that this post seems to let Mr. Hort off the hook for being a Republican if you don&#8217;t read the earlier posts.  I&#8217;m not saying the <span class="caps">GOP</span> isn&#8217;t an anti-gay, anti-abortion party.  I&#8217;m saying that Mr. Hort makes the argument, which many people do, that there is a place for moderates in the <span class="caps">GOP</span>.  I don&#8217;t happen to believe that.  I believe that a vote for any <span class="caps">GOP</span> candidate for the House is a vote to keep Tom DeLay in power.  I do not know how Mr. Hort justifies being on the Conservative Party ticket if he claims to be a moderate.  There is no &#8220;moderate&#8221; wing of the Conservative Party in New York.  I think that&#8217;s the point of the party.</p>
