<p>I did one of my only &#8220;holiday&#8221; things today.  <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> and I went to the <a href="http://www.nypl.org/">New York Public Library</a>&#8217;s annual holiday open house: free wine and food, musicians, stilt walkers, dancers, and magicians were scattered around the main building at 42nd and Fifth.</p>
<p>Of course the musical highlight was the <a href="http://www.usma.edu/uscc/dca/clubs/GLEE/glee_club_photo_gallery.htm">West Point Glee Club</a>:</p>
<p><img alt="usma-glee.jpg" src="/mt/archives/usma-glee.jpg" width="400" height="267" border="0" /></p>
<p>Mr. 3rd-from-the-left was wearing a Santa hat with little flashing lights &#8212; and had the most stripes, whatever that means.  He was a star, and he knew it.</p>
<p>The <span class="caps">NYPL</span> is a great organization, and a $40 <a href="https://www2.nypl.org/support/friends.htm">donation</a> gets you an invitation for two ($100 gets four people in).  It&#8217;s worth it!</p>
<p>There is a cool <a href="http://www.nypl.org/research/chss/events/chssex.html#eats">exhibit</a> at the moment on the history of New York restaurants and food.  It&#8217;s up through March 1, 2003 &#8212; and it&#8217;s free.</p>
