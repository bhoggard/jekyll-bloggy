<p>We&#8217;ve been to a few openings since we returned from Mexico City.  Recommendations:</p>
<p>East of the Sun and West of the Moon, curated by Amie Scally at <a href="http://www.whitecolumns.org/">White Columns</a> (no images up yet).  We were there for the opening, which is not the best way to judge a show, but my favorite pieces in the show were the paintings by Clare Rojas.  I found a web page with images of her work to give you an idea <a href="http://www.newimageartgallery.com/superpowers.html">here</a>.  The other highlight at White Columns is the White Room show by <a href="http://www.willcrow.com/">William Crow</a>.  We finally met this charming young artist in person, after having gotten in touch with him when we bough some of his work at the Cynthia Broan $99 <a href="/2003/02/art-acquisition.html">show</a>.</p>
<p>The other show we attended last night was David Hilliard at <a href="http://www.yanceyrichardson.com/">Yancey Richardson</a>.  His multi-image works are technically brilliant and somehow haunting, as if they have narrative content that we can&#8217;t quite guess.</p>
<p>The night before (Thursday) we went to the opening for Robert Beck at <a href="http://www.crggallery.com/"><span class="caps">CRG</span> Gallery</a>.  It&#8217;s a pretty conceptual show, so you will want to read the <a href="http://www.crggallery.com/press_release_pages/2004_02_05_beck_robert.htm">press release</a>, and maybe talk with Glenn McMillan, which is what I always do when I go.  Even without the conceptual content, the pieces in the show are beautifully constructed <a href="http://www.crggallery.com/artist_image_pages/beck_01.5.htm">objects</a>.</p>
