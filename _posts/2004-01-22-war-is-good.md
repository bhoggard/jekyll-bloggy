<p>Via the <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/3419715.stm"><span class="caps">BBC</span></a>:</p>
<blockquote>General Peter Schoomaker said in an interview with AP news agency that the wars had allowed the army to instil its soldiers with a &#8220;warrior ethos&#8221;.<br />
<br />
But the general, who became chief of staff in August, denied warmongering saying the army must be ready to fight.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
General Schoomaker said the attacks on America in September 2001 and subsequent events had given the US army a rare opportunity to change.<br />
<br />
&#8220;There is a huge silver lining in this cloud,&#8221; he said.<br />
<br />
&#8220;War is a tremendous focus&#8230; Now we have this focusing opportunity, and we have the fact that [terrorists] have actually attacked our homeland, which gives it some oomph.&#8221;<br />
<br />
He said it was no use having an army that did nothing but train.<br />
<br />
&#8220;There&#8217;s got to be a certain appetite for what the hell we exist for,&#8221; he said.<br />
<br />
&#8220;I&#8217;m not warmongering, the fact is we&#8217;re going to be called and really asked to do this stuff.&#8221;</blockquote>
