<p>A priest who says gays are ruining society and the church shows up in a divorce case.  He has a house in Amagansett?  So much for that humble servant of God concept.  That&#8217;s for losers.  Msgr. Clark hung out with the rich Catholics.  See the quote from Newsday below.</p>
<p>From a NY Times article, titled <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/08/12/nyregion/12rector.html">Accusation of an Affair Leads Priest to Resign</a>:</p>
<blockquote>The rector of St. Patrick&#8217;s Cathedral, Msgr. Eugene V. Clark, resigned yesterday amid accusations that he was having an affair with his longtime personal secretary, a married woman who is 33 years his junior.<br />
<br />
&#8220;It appears to me,&#8221; he said in a statement released by his lawyer, &#8220;that events and circumstances have been portrayed in such a false and sensational manner that I will no longer be able to effectively serve the archdiocese.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Monsignor Clark&#8217;s employer, the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York, said in a statement that he had resigned &#8220;for the good of St. Patrick&#8217;s and the archdiocese.&#8221;<br />
<br />
On Monday, Philip DeFilippo, who is married to Monsignor Clark&#8217;s secretary, Laura DeFilippo, filed court papers alleging the relationship as part of his divorce case against his wife, who has worked for Monsignor Clark for more than 25 years. Since then, newspapers and newscasts have carried videotape images provided by Mr. DeFilippo that show Monsignor Clark, 79, and Ms. DeFilippo, 46, entering a motel in the Hamptons last month and walking out five hours later in different clothing.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
In the pulpit, Monsignor Clark has sometimes been outspoken. In 2002 in a Sunday homily at St. Patrick&#8217;s, he attributed the epidemic of sexual abuse by priests in part to the immorality of American popular culture and to the willingness of seminaries to admit homosexuals into the ministry.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
Mr. DeFilippo, 46, an insurance investigator who lives in Eastchester, in Westchester County, said his wife frequently spent weekends with Monsignor Clark at his beach house in Amagansett on the South Fork of Long Island, sometimes bringing the DeFilippos&#8217; two children along. She would sometimes accompany her boss on vacations out of the country, even skipping vacations with her family, Mr. DeFilippo said.</blockquote>
<p>From <a href="http://www.nynewsday.com/news/local/newyork/ncy-prie0812,0,4201283.story?coll=nyc-homepage-breaking2">Newsday</a>:</p>
<blockquote>The charges have been particularly devastating since Clark has used the pulpit at St. Patrick, as well as his frequent appearances on Eternal World Television Network, to rail against a &#8220;sex-saturated&#8221; American culture and to defend the idea of mandatory priestly celibacy. He is a top player in the archdiocese, with close connections to Legatus, a group of wealthy Catholic businessmen, as well as to the Knights of Malta, and has worked as the private secretary of Cardinal Francis Spellman, as well as a spokesman for Cardinal Terence Cooke.</blockquote>
