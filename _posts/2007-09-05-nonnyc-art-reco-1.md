<p>For those not here in <span class="caps">NYC</span> to avail themselves of the 193 upcoming openings on <a href="http://www.artcal.net/">ArtCal</a>, here are some openings of friends or people I&#8217;ve written about coming up in other cities.</p>
<p><img alt="scottTreleavenHangingman.jpg" src="/mt/archives/scottTreleavenHangingman.jpg" width="374" height="600" /></p>
<p>Scott Treleaven, <em>buckskin (iii)</em>, 2007<br />
Watercolor and paper<br />
9.5 &#215; 5.75 inches</p>
<p>First, <a href="http://www.scotttreleaven.com/">Scott Treleaven</a> has an opening on Saturday, September 8, at <a href="http://www.marcselwynfineart.com/artists/treleaven/witchcraft/treleaven_wc_home.html">Marc Selwyn Fine Art</a> in Los Angeles.  James and I are <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004382.html">big fans</a> of his work.</p>
<p><a href="http://soapfactory.org/">The Soap Factory</a> in Minneapolis has a group show titled <em>Host</em> curated by Elizabeth M Grady that includes a number of artists whom I either know or have written about, including <a href="http://www.derickmelander.com/">Derick Melander</a>, <a href="http://www.carolsalmanson.com/">Carol Salmanson</a>, and <a href="http://www.caitlinmasley.com/">Caitlyn Masley</a>.  That opens September 8th.</p>
<p>In Washington, DC, artist JT Kirkland of the <a href="http://thinkingaboutart.blogs.com/">Thinking About Art</a> blog, has curated a show titled <em>The One Word Project Show</em>, a group show of 33 artists.  It will be held at <a href="http://www.artsclubofwashington.org/galleries.htm">The Arts Club of Washington</a>, the former home of President James Monroe and at one point the White House.  It opens September 7th.</p>
