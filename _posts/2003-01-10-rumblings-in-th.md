<p><span class="caps">CNN</span> of all places has a good <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2003/WORLD/meast/01/10/sproject.irq.blair.bush.oakley/index.html">article</a> on the fact that Blair is starting to distance himself from Bush a little bit, with his spokesman saying</p>
<blockquote>[Blair] underlined his view that the weapons inspectors in Iraq must be given the time and space they need to do their job and, in that sense, January 27, though an important staging post, shouldn&#8217;t be regarded in any sense as a deadline.</blockquote>
<p>Meanwhile&#8230;</p>
<blockquote>Train drivers yesterday <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/uk_news/story/0,3604,870967,00.html">refused to move a freight train carrying ammunition</a> believed to be destined for British forces being deployed in the Gulf.<br />
<br />
Railway managers cancelled the Ministry of Defence service after the crewmen, described as &#8220;conscientious objectors&#8221; by a supporter, said they opposed Tony Blair&#8217;s threat to attack Iraq.<br />
<br />
The anti-war revolt is the first such industrial action by workers for decades.</blockquote>
