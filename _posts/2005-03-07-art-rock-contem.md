<p>Yeah for Clementine Gallery!  After being rejected by The Armory Show, they took matters into their own hands and ended up with a contemporary show at the Rockefeller Center Plaza called <a href="http://www.clementine-gallery.com/artrock.html">Art Rock</a>.  It started today, and runs through March 14.  Some snapshots:</p>

<p>Rob Fischer&#8217;s glass &amp; metal house (Mary Goldman Gallery)</p>

<p><img alt="artrock_lounge.jpg" src="/mt/archives/artrock_lounge.jpg" width="500" height="375" /></p>
<p>Example of lounges at either end, designed by Todd Oldham.  Note the more attractive use of orange than we got from <em>The Gates</em>.</p>

<p><img alt="taylor_artrock.jpg" src="/mt/archives/taylor_artrock.jpg" width="500" height="375" /></p>
<p>Taylor McKimens has a wonderful paper house installation.  It was impossible to photograph, at least for me, so I&#8217;m showing you a detail of his little grass edge in the room.  His installation is outstanding.</p>
<p>Also, the Richard Aldrich show presented by Oliver Kamm/5BE is really good.  I just couldn&#8217;t get a good photo, otherwise I would have one here as well.  The same goes for the excellent photography of South African Trevor Appleson presented by the London-based Hales Gallery.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>We can relate to being rejected by the Armory Show.  Scope gave us a press invite, but we were cc-ed on a mass rejection letter with grammatical problems from an Armory press person.  I guess bloggers don&#8217;t count, when they can reserve space for magazines that will print articles a couple of months after the Armory Show is over.</p>
