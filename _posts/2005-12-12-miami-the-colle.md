<p>I wish New York had private collections such as the <a href="http://www.margulieswarehouse.com/">Margulies</a> and <a href="http://www.rubellfamilycollection.com/">Rubell</a> Collections that are open to the public.  I enjoyed aspects of the Rubell, particularly the exhibition of contemporary Polish work, and the paintings of Norbert Schwontkowski, but it doesn&#8217;t feel like the &#8220;personal&#8221; collection that the Margulies does.  It has smart choices, but there was very little that surprised me, and very little (other than the two things I just mentioned) that wasn&#8217;t already familiar.  It also felt rather odd to see gift shops inside (including t-shirt sales).</p>
<p>I found the Margulies Collection very moving.  I loved the focus on work with a social or political aspect, and the very intelligent curating of the works on display, with juxtapositions such as Peter Friedl&#8217;s <a href="http://coda.co.za/cv/teamscience/ica/projects/peter_friedl/kingkong/index.php">King Kong</a> video placed near work documenting the ruins of Detroit, the G8 riots in Genoa, and ruins of Kabul.</p>
