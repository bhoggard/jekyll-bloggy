<p>Earlier in the evening we (<a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> and I &#8212; he has more photos) saw <a href="http://www.kikiandherb.com/">Kiki and Herb</a> at the <a href="http://www.knittingfactory.com/">Knitting Factory</a> with <a href="http://glennalicious.blogspot.com/" title="sexy shirt!">Glenn</a>, <a href="http://www.ultrasparky.org/" title="smart=sexy in my book">Dan</a> and a few of their friends.  Oh my heavens!  Why didn&#8217;t someone drag me to see them earlier?  I <span class="caps">LOVE</span> <span class="caps">THEM</span>.</p>
<p>As I told Glenn, I think our drag sisters have <span class="caps">MUCH</span> better politics than the gay community in general.  They can&#8217;t really buy into the &#8220;but if I act like a straight white middle class male I&#8217;ll be OK&#8221; version of gay politics.  (I thought about linking Andrew Sullivan in that sentence, but I couldn&#8217;t bring myself to sully my web site with a link to that miserable excuse for a pundit.)</p>
<p>Where to begin?  As Dan said, it&#8217;s certainly not what comes to mind when one says &#8220;drag act&#8221; &#8212; it&#8217;s much more of a brilliant piece of theatre by two very talented people.  Kiki&#8217;s politics are great, and political theatre that works is my favorite thing in the world.  She hit on 9/11, the idiocy of Bush, his illegitimacy, our obsessions with kidnapped children, and probably some things I didn&#8217;t even catch in the whirling chaos that is Kiki and Herb.</p>
<p>Favorite excerpts included:</p>
<blockquote>Shitty things happen sometimes, but that&#8217;s not an excuse to do more shitty things.<br />
<br />
After a song in which she says she&#8217;s tired of crying for victims of this or that, she says: because crying doesn&#8217;t change anything.</blockquote>
<p>After she talked about the shitty things that happen in the world, and about the idiot that passes for our <a href="http://slate.msn.com/?id=2070565">President</a>, and received a lot of applause, she said she was glad to hear that she&#8217;s not alone, and when they round all of us up, she&#8217;s glad she will be with people like us at <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/americas/1766037.stm">Guantanamo</a>.</p>
<p>The opening act, of whom I had also heard, but never seen, was <a href="http://www.wauwausisters.com/">The Wau Wau Sisters</a>.  They <span class="caps">ROCKED</span>.  I feel like such a scrawny wimp &#8212; they both had bods of death.  They gave us rockin&#8217; songs, hilarious repartee, and acrobatics!  We bought the CD!</p>
<p>Heard on the way out of the Knitting Factory, from an <a href="http://www.righteousbabe.com/">Ani di Franco</a>-type young woman: &#8220;I&#8217;m wearing my new sweatshop free panties!&#8221;</p>
