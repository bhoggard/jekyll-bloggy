<p>I just got back from lunch at <a href="http://www.newyorkmetro.com/pages/details/4291.htm">The Odeon</a> with <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a>, <a href="http://neuroticjew.blogspot.com/">Scott</a>, and <a href="http://www.andyschest.com/">Andy</a> &#8212; too much wine so this won&#8217;t be eloquent.  Andy asked me why I started doing a blog apart from my current, more &#8220;public&#8221; web presence, and I think it&#8217;s because of what <a href="http://www.kissedme.com/">David Drake</a> referred to as &#8220;free floating rage&#8221;.  I started with <a href="http://shoulderchip.com">shoulderchip</a> rather than this page, because I had some things to say about the way the world is being run today.</p>
<p>A little bit of shoulderchip is about the Middle East, but a lot of it is about the theocracy that America has become.  The current administration didn&#8217;t even win a majority, and they&#8217;re acting as if they got 90% of the vote.  They don&#8217;t care what people think.  All they have to say is that this is a <a href="http://www.harpers.org/online/notebook/?pg=1">war to protect our civilization</a>, and the sheep that pass for the media and the public say, &#8220;OK&#8221;.</p>
<p>As Choire <a href="http://eastwest.nu/aview.shtml?/blogtests/2002_04_28_coryarchives.html#85046600">says</a> &#8212; what should we be doing?  I don&#8217;t post to shoulderchip that often these days, because I&#8217;m feeling pretty apathetic about changing anything.  I was never a <a href="http://www.actupny.org/">great</a> <a href="http://www.panix.com/~hoggardb/march_photos.html">activist</a>, but I feel really impotent these days.</p>
<p>So what&#8217;s the answer?</p>
