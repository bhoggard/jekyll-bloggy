<p>The Secret Forest of Princess Knight:<br />
Fantastic Landscapes from Japan</p>
<p>September 9 &#8211; October 16, 2004<br />
Reception: Thursday, Sept. 9, 6-8pm</p>
<p>Our exciting fall exhibition, curated by Taro Chiezo &#8211; the Tokyo &amp; New York-based artist whose &#8220;neo-pop&#8221; work shaped Japan&#8217;s 1990&#8217;s art scene along with Takashi Murakami and Yoshitomo Nara, sheds new light on Japanese landscape art. The exhibition will present works by six individual artists and two collaborative groups. International stars and up-and-coming artists, they all share a sensitivity influenced by the manga/animation subculture widely known as &#8220;Japanimation.&#8221;</p>
<p>The exhibition&#8217;s title &#8220;The Secret Forest of Princess Knight&#8221; originated from the popular manga series &#8220;Ribon no Kishi (Princess Knight)&#8221; by Tezuka Osamu (1928-1989). His work, like Walt Disney&#8217;s, has made a profound impact on pop culture and contemporary art even outside Japan.</p>
</blockquote>
