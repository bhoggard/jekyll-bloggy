<p>I feel the need to quote <a href="http://tbogg.blogspot.com/2002_10_06_tbogg_archive.html#82744127"><span class="caps">TBOGG</span></a>&#8217;s title for this:</p>
<p>We&#8217;d love to look into this&#8230;but we&#8217;ve got a war to fight&#8230;</p>
<blockquote>Harvard University&#8217;s financial relationship with President Bush&#8217;s former oil company was deeper than previously understood, with the university&#8217;s management fund creating a separate &#8216;&#8217;off the books&#8217;&#8217; partnership with Harken Energy Corp. that helped keep afloat the financially troubled company, according to a report to be released today.<br />
<br />
HarvardWatch, a student-alumni group that monitors the school&#8217;s investments, plans to issue the report and say that it has analyzed documents showing that the Harvard fund, an independent entity that manages the university&#8217;s endowment, formed a partnership in 1990 with Bush&#8217;s oil firm called the Harken Anadarko Partnership. The partnership effectively removed $20 million of debt from Harken&#8217;s books, relieving the Texas company&#8217;s short-term financial problems.<br />
<br />
About the same time, the Harvard fund invested about $30 million in Harken, which also helped keep the firm afloat. The partnership has not been mentioned in recent accounts of Bush&#8217;s financial dealings in the oil business.</blockquote>
