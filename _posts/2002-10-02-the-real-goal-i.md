<p>Jay Bookman, one of the editors of the Atlanta Journal-Constitution has a very good <a href="http://www.accessatlanta.com/ajc/opinion/0902/29bookman.html">column</a> from a couple of days ago about the real goal of war on Iraq.  It&#8217;s to further the goal of people like Cheney and Rumsfeld to finally assemble a global American empire.</p>
<blockquote>The official story on Iraq has never made sense. The connection that the Bush administration has tried to draw between Iraq and al-Qaida has always seemed contrived and artificial. In fact, it was hard to believe that smart people in the Bush administration would start a major war based on such flimsy evidence. <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
This war, should it come, is intended to mark the official emergence of the United States as a full-fledged global empire, seizing sole responsibility and authority as planetary policeman. It would be the culmination of a plan 10 years or more in the making, carried out by those who believe the United States must seize the opportunity for global domination, even if it means becoming the &#8220;American imperialists&#8221; that our enemies always claimed we were.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
Once that is understood, other mysteries solve themselves. For example, why does the administration seem unconcerned about an exit strategy from Iraq once Saddam is toppled?<br />
<br />
Because we won&#8217;t be leaving. Having conquered Iraq, the United States will create permanent military bases in that country from which to dominate the Middle East, including neighboring Iran.</blockquote>
<p>Bookman refers in the column to a report issued in 2000 called &#8220;Rebuilding America&#8217;s Defenses&#8221;, available for download from <a href="http://www.newamericancentury.org/publicationsreports.htm">New American Century</a>, which is a conservative think tank.  Its authors include quite a few people now in charge in the Bush administration:</p>
<blockquote>Paul Wolfowitz is now deputy defense secretary. John Bolton is undersecretary of state. Stephen Cambone is head of the Pentagon&#8217;s Office of Program, Analysis and Evaluation. Eliot Cohen and Devon Cross are members of the Defense Policy Board, which advises Rumsfeld. I. Lewis Libby is chief of staff to Vice President Dick Cheney. Dov Zakheim is comptroller for the Defense Department.</blockquote>
<p>I would think Israel would be rather concerned about this if they suspect it&#8217;s what we&#8217;re planning.  If Iraq becomes our &#8220;aircraft carrier&#8221; in the Middle East, Israel&#8217;s strategic importance for us will drop sharply.</p>
