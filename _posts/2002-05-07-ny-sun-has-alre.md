<p><a href="http://www.salon.com/news/feature/2002/04/25/nysun/">This amuses me</a>.  As one of the people involved with the new paper <a href="http://www.nysun.com/">The New York Sun</a> used to write <a href="http://www.smartertimes.com/">Smarter Times</a> (which critiqued the <a href="http://nytimes.com">NY Times</a>), there are already two similar sites for the Sun: <a href="http://www.flakmag.com/smartersun/">Smarter New York Sun</a>, courtesy of <a href="http://www.flakmag.com/">Flak Magazine</a>,  and <a href="http://parsol1789.blogspot.com/">Like Father, Like Sun</a>.</p>
<p>The two critical sites are more interesting than the Sun.  One of my favorite features of Smarter New York Sun is &#8220;Wire Watch&#8221; &#8212; in which they track the number of stories coming from the wire services.  It appears that $20 million doesn&#8217;t buy much of a news-gathering operation these days.<br />
.</p>
