<p>Yesterday we celebrated the holiday by going to Central Park for a performance of Inverse Theater&#8217;s <a href="http://www.inversetheater.com/Productions/current.htm">Washington: The American Revolution</a>.  They are an interesting theater company, specializing in contemporary plays in verse (more like Shakespearean blank verse).  I was very impressed by the writing, and the cast was charismatic and talented.  I was particularly impressed by <a href="http://www.inversetheater.com/spafford/index.html">Joshua Spafford</a> as Benedict Arnold.  Both the play and his performance provided a very nuanced look at one of the most famous but least understood Americans.  <a href="http://www.inversetheater.com/Company/laine.htm">Robert Laine</a> was hilarious in several roles, especially the hangman.  A few photos may be found <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/inverse">here</a>.</p>
<p>It will be at the <a href="http://www.fringenyc.org/">Fringe Festival</a> too.  I think it&#8217;s worth seeing.</p>
