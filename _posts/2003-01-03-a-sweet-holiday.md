<p>A mother to her daughter, on her friend whose parents don&#8217;t seem to love her:</p>
<blockquote>I know darling&#8230; One thing about unwanted children: they soon learn how to take care of themselves.</blockquote>
<p>&#8212; from <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Title?0058756">The World of Henry Orient</a> starring Peter Sellars, Paula Prentiss, and Angela Lansbury.  We got it from <a href="http://www.netflix.com">NetFlix</a> and watched it last night.</p>
<p>I had heard of the movie, and was reminded of it recently when the Times had an <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/12/28/obituaries/28HILL.html?ex=1041742800&en=242e8a2c77b4d079&ei=5062&partner=GOOGLE">obituary</a> of the director, <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Hill,%20George%20Roy">George Roy Hill</a>.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s a weird early 60s movie with worldly parents drinking scotch, and two 14-year-old girls who decide to stalk a concert pianist named Henry Orient.  The way the two girls joke about Chinese stereotypes and speak in funny accents probably helps explain why the film is so obscure today.</p>
<p>One of the two girls went on join the Dark Side as a <a href="http://www.speaking.com/speakers/merriespaeth.html">communications expert</a>, working for the likes of <a href="http://www.pbs.org/newshour/updates/webster_11-12-02.html">William Webster</a> and Ronald Reagan.</p>
