<p><a href="http://www.cohanandleslie.com/content.php?mode=current&scr_w=1118"><em>Much madness is divinest sense</em></a> at Cohan and Leslie, particularly the drawings of Mike Paré and the site specific wall drawing and other drawings by Dasha Shishkin.  We first saw Mike Paré&#8217;s work in a group show at <a href="http://www.atmgallery.com/"><span class="caps">ATM</span> Gallery</a>, and first encountered Dasha Shishkin in a <a href="/2004/07/3-chelsea-group.html">group show</a> at 5BE curated by Lital Mehr.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.brentsikkema.com/exhibition_jameshyde.html">James Hyde</a> at Brent Sikkema.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/377"><em>in words and pictures</em></a> at Murray Guy.  A smart group show (mostly) dealing with art incorporating texts.</p>
<p>Betty Woodman at <a href="http://www.maxprotetch.com/">Max Protetch</a>.  This show is the best work I have seen from her.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.5begallery.com/content.php?mode=gallery&amp;section_id=9">Don Doe</a> at Oliver Kamm.  Old Master-style skill applied to outrageous paintings and drawings of pirate women.</p>
<p>Aaron Spangler (large-scale intricate wood reliefs) and Ryan Johnson (compelling paper sculptures) at <a href="http://www.zachfeuer.com/">Zach Feuer</a>.</p>
