<p>I&#8217;m not interested in hearing about how Mayor Michael Bloomberg is a patron of the arts.  His response to the <a href="/2006/05/brooklyn-colleg-1.html">Brooklyn <span class="caps">MFA</span> fiasco</a> is appalling:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;Nobody&#8217;s suggesting that anybody shouldn&#8217;t be allowed to exhibit art,&#8221; Mr. Bloomberg said. &#8220;The issue here is this is not a museum. This is a war memorial.&#8221; He added, &#8220;There has been an understanding ever since art was put here that the art would be appropriate for families and respectful of and appropriate for a war memorial and this time it was not.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
<p>So every building that has &#8220;memorial&#8221; in the name can&#8217;t have art anymore?</p>
<p>Today Brooklyn College sent trucks and workers, accompanied by plain clothes <span class="caps">NYPD</span> officers, to remove the work from the space without informing the artists.  Apparently some of the work was damaged.  I suspect the &#8220;movers&#8221; weren&#8217;t art handlers.</p>
