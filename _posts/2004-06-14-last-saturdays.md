<p>We saw some good stuff last Saturday.  Unfortunately, it was the last day for some of it.  I&#8217;m a bad art journalist!</p>
<p>In no particular order:</p>
<p><img alt="tony_feher-mylar_bags.jpg" src="/mt/archives/tony_feher-mylar_bags.jpg" width="425" height="399" border="0" /></p>
<p>Tony Feher, <i>Untitled</i><br />
2004<br />
nine mylar bags and push pins</p>
<p>Tony Feher at <a href="http://www.damelioterras.com/">D&#8217;Amelio Terras</a>: Lovely show, up through July 2nd.  He is doing some new things with hanging works beyond what I have seen before.  The potato chip bags above had holes made by a hole puncher in them.  It was fun to see everyone light up with a smile as they walked into the gallery.  <a href="http://jameswagner.com/">James</a> took some photographs of the show that will probably show up on his site.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><img alt="fisk_endtable.jpg" src="/mt/archives/fisk_endtable.jpg" width="211" height="360" border="0" /></p>
<p>Lars Fisk, <i>End Table</i><br />
2004<br />
Cast rubber, steel, bamboo, ed. of 3</p>
<p>Lars Fisk at <a href="http://www.taxterandspengemann.com/">Taxter &amp; Spengemann</a>: this is one of the less-known galleries in Chelsea that should be on everyone&#8217;s list.  We first met Pascal Spengemann a few years ago at Scope Art Fair, representing his gallery in Vermont!  Lars Fisk plays with fake chinoiserie and other aspects of fetishized &#8220;foreignness.&#8221;  The show has closed.</p>
<p>The next show at the gallery looks great.  The image below doesn&#8217;t convey the glow and color of this painting, which we saw in the gallery:</p>
<p><img alt="schacte_ffpl.jpg" src="/mt/archives/schacte_ffpl.jpg" width="344" height="347" border="0" /></p>
<p>Anna Schachte, <i>Freedom Festival Plaza Parkinglot</i><br />
2003<br />
Oil on canvas, 70&#8243; &#215; 70&#8243;</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>You don&#8217;t need me to tell you that the Amy Cutler show, now closed, at <a href="http://www.tonkonow.com/">Leslie Tonkonow</a>, was brilliant.  Plenty of people have mentioned it as a great show.  I think she has hit a new artistic level in her work.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><img alt="tar_r_palace_imorgen.jpg" src="/mt/archives/tar_r_palace_imorgen.jpg" width="432" height="421" border="0" /></p>
<p>Tal R, <i>Palace imorgen</i><br />
2004<br />
oil , paper collage on canvas 78 3/4 &#215; 78 3/4</p>
<p>Tal R at <a href="http://lflgallery.com/tal.html"><span class="caps">LFL</span> Gallery</a>, up through June 19th.  The artist is Danish/Israeli (!), but in a way the work looks like it could be an African artist.  Great use of color, and smart use of collage in a way that looks like painting.</p>
<p>Updated:</p>
<p>I forgot to mention we also saw <a href="http://www.ericdoeringer.com/">Eric Doeringer</a> selling his bootlegs on 24th Street.  We bought a &#8220;Jack Pierson&#8221; letter piece that said &#8220;<span class="caps">STAY</span>&#8221;.  His <a href="http://www.ericdoeringer.com/bootlegs/btholstad.html">Christian Holstad</a>, <a href="http://www.ericdoeringer.com/bootlegs/btowens.html">Laura Owens</a>, and <a href="http://www.ericdoeringer.com/bootlegs/btreed.html">David Reed</a> pieces looked tempting as well.</p>
