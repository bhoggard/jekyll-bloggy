<p>From <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/columnists/ny-nysub223595715dec22,0,7760887.column?coll=ny-news-columnists">Ray Sanchez</a>, Newsday&#8217;s <span class="caps">MTA</span> watchdog:</p>
<blockquote>In approving its 2004 budget, the <span class="caps">MTA</span> cut the subway and bus workforce by 857 positions while increasing the workforces of Metro-North and Long Island Rail Road by 304. City subways and buses receive only 63 percent of state transit aid while moving 84 percent of the state&#8217;s riders. Metro-North and <span class="caps">LIRR</span> move just 5 percent of riders but get 23 percent of state transit funding.</blockquote>
