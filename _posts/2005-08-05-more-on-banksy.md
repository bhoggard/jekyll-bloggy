<p>The <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/arts/news/story/0,11711,1543171,00.html?gusrc=rss">Guardian</a> has an article and <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/arts/gallery/0,8542,1543331,00.html">photos</a> related to my earlier <a href="/2005/08/from-other-blog.html">mention</a> of Banksy in the West Bank.  A choice quote for those unfamiliar with the wall:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Although the paintings themselves are not overtly political, his feelings about the wall are apparent from his statement: &#8220;The Israeli government is building a wall surrounding the occupied Palestinian territories. It stands three times the height of the Berlin Wall and will eventually run for over 700km &#8211; the distance from London to Zurich. The wall is illegal under international law and essentially turns Palestine into the world&#8217;s largest open prison.&#8221;</p>
</blockquote>
