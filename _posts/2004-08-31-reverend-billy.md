<p>I&#8217;m pleasantly surprised by the New York Magazine <a href="http://thekicker.nymetro.com/archives/000420.html">weblog</a>&#8217;s coverage of the <span class="caps">RNC</span> protests.</p>
<p>Here is their coverage of Reverend Billy&#8217;s First Amendment protest at the <span class="caps">PATH</span> station at the <span class="caps">WTC</span> site.  I wasn&#8217;t there, but I found the description quite moving.</p>
<blockquote>6:31 &#8211; Inside the station everything seems normal enough. Passengers<br />
walk the long cement floor toward the escalator. Some seem to drift,<br />
mumbling into cell phones, reciting the first amendment.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
6:41 &#8211; Reverend Billy enters the station carrying a bullhorn, wearing a white collar, black shirt, a creme colored suit, and sporting an Eric Estrada haircut.<br />
<br />
6:42 &#8211; The people on the phones grow louder. &#8220;Congress shall make no law&#8230;&#8221;<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
6:52 &#8211; A young couple walks arm-in-arm reciting the first amendment.<br />
<br />
Count: Four reading from books, thirty speaking into cell phones.<br />
<br />
6:58 &#8211; Synchonization:<br />
Congress shall make no law respecting the establishment of religion , or prohibiting the free excercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peacably to assemble; and to petition the government for a redress of grievances.<br />
<br />
Their voices fill this loaded chamber.<br />
<br />
7:03 &#8211; Circle forms. Reverend becomes clear leader of the group. Bringing the chants to a whisper, then louder, and finally, &#8220;Really enjoy it this time!&#8221;<br />
<br />
7:10 &#8211; The choir starts above the site. &#8220;George Bush does not return to ground zero,&#8221; they sing in golden robes accompanied by a woman playing a small saxophone.<br />
<br />
7:12 &#8211; The police approach the choir, then walk away.<br />
<br />
7:15 &#8211; The Reverend speaks. &#8220;Take the first amendment anywhere. Take it on the subway. Remember the first amendment is a prayer. Send it to our friends in Rikers. Give them strength.&#8221;</blockquote>
