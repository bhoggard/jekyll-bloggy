<p>I&#8217;m happy that many people are fans of <a href="http://www.artcal.net">ArtCal</a>, and the so-called value of &#8220;accuracy&#8221; is occasionally mentioned.  ArtCal may have a mailing list now, but only <a href="http://dks.thing.net/">Douglas Kelley</a> brings writing like this to our inbox.</p>
<p>Here is Douglas on drugs, Jean-Michel Basquiat, and Glenn O&#8217;Brien:</p>
<blockquote>Glenn O&#8217;Brien, I believe, understood that The White Powder School is not<br />
for most people, probably 99.9% of most people, however there are rare<br />
individuals such as Keith Richards and Jean-Michel Basquait for whom<br />
certain Schedule IV prescription pharmaceuticals, as well as the<br />
traditionally universally contracinded in all cases contraband psycho<br />
stimulants and depressants are indispensable essential commodities of<br />
life like food, paint, canvas, or guitar strings. And history often<br />
remembers these individuals by the great periods of their most extended<br />
peak output wherein they so colossally dwarfed the production of anyone<br />
else, certainly in quantity, but more importantly in supreme sublime<br />
quality that all the slightly less talented are left scratching their<br />
heads, wondering what were we doing all that time?<br />
<br />
<strong>Jean-Michel Basquait: King of The White Powder School<br />
</strong><br />
<br />
If you ever meet an artist as talented as Jean-Michel Basquait with such<br />
a chemical romance, just put the drugs under the door and leave the art<br />
supplies in the hall, and come back in the morning and collect the art,<br />
no questions asked. It&#8217;s highly unlikely that you ever will, so with<br />
that understanding, always stay away from drugs kids and stay in school!</blockquote>
