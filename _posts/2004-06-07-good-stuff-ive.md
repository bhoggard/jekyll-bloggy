<p>OK.  Not going to talk about dead presidents or war anymore for a bit.  I may not even read the news except for arts coverage for a while.</p>
<p>Recently seen work of note:</p>
<p>Vicki Sher at <a href="http://www.jessicamurrayprojects.com/">Jessica Murray Projects</a>.  The show ends June 13.  I don&#8217;t think this image, or the other on the web site, do her work justice.  They are beautiful,  disturbing pencil and watercolor works.</p>
<p><img alt="vicki-sher-kristin.jpg" src="/mt/archives/vicki-sher-kristin.jpg" width="504" height="341" border="0" /></p>
<p>Vicki Sher, <i>Kristin</i>, 2004<br />
graphite, colored pencil and watercolor on paper, 13 1/2 &#215; 18 1/4&quot;</p>
<p>While you&#8217;re at the gallery, you might want to ask to see the prints that <a href="http://www.jessicamurrayprojects.com/artistsBradyDollarhide.html">Brady Dollarhide</a> has been working on.  They&#8217;re great.  Also, check out the latest work by <a href="http://www.jessicamurrayprojects.com/artistsJackieGendel.html">Jackie Gendel</a>.  She&#8217;s making some interesting wax and oil (and maybe graphite?) works.  I&#8217;ve been watching her work since seeing it in a <a href="http://www.artnet.com/magazine/features/finch/finch11-25-02.asp">group show at White Columns</a>.</p>

<p><img alt="joy-garnett-Air_Strip.jpg" src="/mt/archives/joy-garnett-Air_Strip.jpg" width="720" height="378" border="0" /></p>
<p>Joy Garnett, <i>Air Strip</i> (2003) 44 &#215; 84 inches. Oil on canvas.</p>
<p>There is a group show called <a href="http://www.leewells.org/activeduty/"><i>Active Duty</i></a>, described as</p>
<blockquote>Armed Artists of America (<span class="caps">AAA</span>)<br />
Armed with ideas and the tools to create a rapid response agenda<br />
to stop the global progression towards world war three.</blockquote>
<p>organized by Lee Wells at Studio 84 (84 South First Street between Berry and Wythe in Williamsburg) with some great work.  <a href="http://www.firstpulseprojects.com/joy.html">Joy Garnett</a> is among the artists in the show, which is up through July 4.  It&#8217;s wonderful to see a painter of great skill doing politically-inspired work.  I love everything I&#8217;ve seen by her, including her show at <a href="http://www.debsandco.com/garnett.php">Debs &amp; Co.</a>  Check out the <a href="http://www.artnet.com/magazine/reviews/green/green3-17-04.asp">review</a> of that show by Tyler Green.  Joy also has work in a good group show at <a href="http://www.giganticartspace.com/">Gigantic Artspace</a> called <i>Tactical Action</i>, up through June 10, and <i>Terrorvision</i> at <a href="http://www.exitart.org/">Exit Art</a>, up through July 31.</p>
<p>Updated:  Thanks to <a href="http://www.artsjournal.com/man/">Tyler Green</a> I now know that Joy Garnett has a <a href="http://newsgrist.typepad.com/">weblog</a>.</p>
