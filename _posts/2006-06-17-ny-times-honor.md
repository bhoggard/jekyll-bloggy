<p>One would think a newspaper that gave the world Jason Blair and Judith Miller as &#8220;trusted sources&#8221; would be more careful, but it appears they don&#8217;t even bother with verifying facts for theater productions.  In a city where they are still the newspaper that can make or break a theatrical production, that can be deadly.</p>
<p>I know the difference between <a href="http://www.13p.org/">13P</a> and the <a href="http://www.newgeorges.org/">New Georges</a>, having written about both.  The <em>New York Times</em> apparently does not.  The recent show <em>Dead City</em>, about which I <a href="/2006/06/dead-city-by-sh-1.html">wrote</a> on June 1st, was attributed to 13P rather than the New Georges, which really isn&#8217;t fair to a small theater company trying to get its name out there.  Culturebot has <a href="http://www.culturebot.org/archives/2006/06/12/DeadCitygate.php">more on the story</a>.</p>
<p>Related: <a href="http://www.13p.org/">13P</a> has a new play by Kate E. Ryan, titled <em>Mark Smith</em> running through June 24 at 46 Walker Street in Tribeca.</p>
