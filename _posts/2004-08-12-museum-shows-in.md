<p>We saw some great museum shows while in LA.  I really enjoyed the fact that we could see interesting modern and contemporary work in the museums, as the <span class="caps">NYC</span> ones haven&#8217;t been great about that lately with the exception of the <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/004193.html">Whitney</a>.</p>
<p>I already mentioned the (now closed) minimalism show at <span class="caps">MOCA</span>, which was brilliant.  Other shows we saw of note:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.hammer.ucla.edu/exhibitions/45/">Made in Mexico</a>, <a href="http://www.hammer.ucla.edu/exhibitions/57/">Rob Voerman</a>, and <a href="http://www.hammer.ucla.edu/exhibitions/46/">Tara Donovan</a> at the <span class="caps">UCLA</span> Hammer.  I especially liked the work by Anton Vidokle and Andrea Fraser&#8217;s video.  It&#8217;s one of my favorite works by her that I&#8217;ve seen.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.lacma.org/info/press/beyondgeomPR.htm">Beyond Geometry</a> at <a href="http://www.lacma.org/"><span class="caps">LACMA</span></a>.  I agree with <a href="http://www.artsjournal.com/man/">Tyler Green</a> that the premise is a bit baffling, but I loved the show.  We didn&#8217;t make it to the <a href="http://www.lacma.org/info/press/castapr.htm">show</a> on race and 18th century colonial Mexican painting, but we wanted to.  <span class="caps">LACMA</span> is free after 5PM all summer so you get a cooler mix of people at the shows than a museum might normally have.  I like the open terraces between the buildings there.</p>
<p>If you like Tara Donovan&#8217;s work, you should also check out Sharon Louden.  See <a href="http://www.kemperart.org/exhibits/CatalogEssays/loudensharon.asp">here</a> and <a href="http://www.artnet.com/artist/10750/Sharon_Louden.html">here</a>.</p>
