<p>I rarely make it to midtown, and the excuse last week was a visit to the dentist.  I&#8217;m certainly glad I did visit a couple of shows.  First, the Donald Judd/Joseph Albers show at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/3/3954">Pace Wildenstein</a> is the kind of show I would expect from one of our city&#8217;s museums, but it seems galleries like Pace, Cheim and Read (with the <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/1/2715">Soutine show</a>), and even <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/005574.html">auction houses</a> seem more likely to present them.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="/upload/2007/02/ALBERS_JUDD_inst_v2.jpg" width="510" height="305" alt="ALBERS_JUDD_inst_v2.jpg"/></p>
<p>installation view at Pace Wildenstein</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>When the elevator doors opened on the gallery, I actually gasped slightly.  It&#8217;s that beautiful, feeling a bit like a temple.  The contrast with the large windows viewing a busy 57th Street is quite wonderful.</p>
<p>The other show I saw was &#8220;The Nightly News&#8221; at <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/3/3888">Luxe Gallery</a>.  It is curated by Kathleen Goncharov (whom I met at a Momenta benefit last year) and Stephan Stoyanov.  Any show that includes <a href="http://jameswagner.com/mt_archives/005509.html">Robert Boyd</a> and <a href="http://jsalloum.org/">Jackie Salloum</a> would attract me, but the new discovery for me was the work of a Turkish artist, <a href="http://ahmetogut.blogspot.com/">Ahmet Ö&#287;üt</a>.  He was represented by a set of videos, including &#8220;Cut it Out&#8221;, in which a young man dressed in American flag pants tries to recreate a hostage video in Iraq or Afghanistan, but keeps messing up and laughing.  Keep your eye on Mr. Ö&#287;üt.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="/upload/2007/02/ahmet-ogut-cut-it-out.jpg" width="450" height="300" alt="ahmet-ogut-cut-it-out.jpg"/></p>
<p>Ahmet Ö&#287;üt, <em>Cut it Out</em>, 2004, <span class="caps">DVD</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>There was a surreal moment as I turned to leave Luxe Gallery.  With the music from Robert Boyd&#8217;s video on the subject of suicide cults playing in the backround, I spotted a sheet of paper from a notepad on the floor, shaped like a yellow star.</p>
