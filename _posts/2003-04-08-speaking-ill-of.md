<p>After reading all of the puff pieces on the death of Michael Kelly, David E gives us a welcome antidote, titled <a href="http://fablog.ehrensteinland.com/archives/000054.shtml#000054">Speaking Ill of the Dead</a>.  Michael Kelly, assuming homosexuality was some kind of decadence of the elite, gave us this as an example of what is wrong with liberalism:</p>
<blockquote>One of those cultural interests is stamping out discrimination against gays. The problem is, all the people who are for this don&#8217;t have their children in those schools anymore. The sons and daughters of editorial writers at the New York Times haven&#8217;t been in those schools for generations. The children who are in those schools are the sons and daughters of working-class people, many of them immigrants, many of them Catholics, and they don&#8217;t want their children propagandized against their wishes.</blockquote>
<p>The working class don&#8217;t have any homosexuals, right?  It&#8217;s only rich lefty pansies, and no working class man ever killed a homosexual who didn&#8217;t deserve it.</p>
<p>Mourn for the dead Iraqis, Americans, British, and journalists of many nationalities who have died in the war, but not for those who helped send them there with writings like these:</p>
<blockquote> The depth of denial here is stunning. Lieven concedes that the militarily superior United States probably could topple Saddam&#8217;s regime. But what then? He writes: &#8220;The &#8216;democracy&#8217; which replaces it will presumably resemble that of Afghanistan&#8212;a ramshackle coalition of ethnic groups and warlords, utterly dependent on U.S. military power and utterly subservient to U.S. (and Israeli) wishes.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Yes, I suppose what exists in Afghanistan is only (so far, at least) a &#8220;democracy,&#8221;&#8217; not a democracy. And it sure is ethnic. And ramshackle. And, sure, post-Saddam Iraq would probably be the same.<br />
<br />
But isn&#8217;t Afghanistan after America&#8217;s rescue a better place to live than it was before? I mean, again, from the liberal point of view: no more throwing homosexuals off buildings, whipping women, banning kites, that sort of thing. No more fascists.</blockquote>
<p>and</p>
<blockquote> These people could be liberated from this horror&#8212;relatively easily and very quickly. There is every reason to think that an American invasion will swiftly vanquish the few elite units that can be counted on to defend the detested Saddam; and that the victory will come at the cost of few&#8212;likely hundreds, not thousands or tens of thousands&#8212;Iraqi and American lives. There is risk here; and if things go terribly wrong it is a risk that could result in terrible suffering. But that is an equation that is present in any just war, and in this case any rational expectation has to consider the probable cost to humanity low and the probable benefit tremendous. To choose perpetuation of tyranny over rescue from tyranny, where rescue may be achieved, is immoral.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
To march against the war is not to give peace a chance. It is to give tyranny a chance. It is to give the Iraqi nuke a chance. It is to give the next terrorist mass murder a chance. It is to march for the furtherance of evil instead of the vanquishing of evil. </blockquote>
<p>For those who think Afghanistan is fine now, and we&#8217;re going do the same for Iraq, I recommend reading <a href="http://digbysblog.blogspot.com/2003_04_06_digbysblog_archive.html#92178847">this post</a> from <a href="http://digbysblog.blogspot.com/">Digby</a> &#8212; direct link might not work.  The Taliban is organized enough in Afghanistan to be killing Red Cross workers, and remember <a href="/2003/02/of-course-well.html">this story</a> from February 14:</p>
<blockquote>The United States Congress has stepped in to find nearly $300 million in humanitarian and reconstruction funds for Afghanistan after the Bush administration failed to request any money in the latest budget. </blockquote>
<p>A Tomahawk missile, of which we have dropped hundreds on Iraq, <a href="http://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomahawk+missile">cost $1.4 million each</a>.</p>
