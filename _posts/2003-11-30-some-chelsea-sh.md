<p>We spent all afternoon walking around Chelsea galleries.  The highlights:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.cjsstudio.com/">Carolyn Swiszcz</a> at <a href="http://www.myartprospects.com/">M.Y. Art Prospects</a> &#8212; an incredibly  beautiful show of works on paper using various techniques including collage and painting.</p>
<p><img alt="cjs-camelot.jpg" src="/mt/archives/cjs-camelot.jpg" width="428" height="330" border="0" /><br />
<i>Camelot Cleaner</i>, 15 X 17, acrylic, pencil, and ink on paper, 2003</p>
<p>__</p>
<p>Henry Horenstein: Honky Tonk, Portraits of Country Music 1972 &#8211; 1981 at <a href="http://www.sarahmorthlandgallery.com/">Sarah Morthland</a> &#8212; getting in touch with my heritage</p>
<p><img alt="playingForTips.jpg" src="/mt/archives/playingForTips.jpg" width="220" height="219" border="0" /><br />
Playing For Tips<br />

printed 2003</p>
<p>__</p>
<p>I can&#8217;t get an image from her site, since it still shows the last show, but <i>Framing Architecture</i>, a show of architecture-related art curated by Daniel Marzona at <a href="http://www.elgawimmer.com/">Elga Wimmer</a> is a great show.  I especially liked the paintings and sculpture of Simon Aldridge, the works on paper by Eric Brown, and  the styrofoam sculptures by Patrick Meagher.  We saw Eric&#8217;s work at the <a href="http://www.whitecolumns.org/cheap/gallery.html">Cheap</a> show at White Columns, which was where we picked up our <a href="/2003/01/new-art-acquisi.html">Matthew Callinan</a> piece.</p>
<p>__</p>
<p>Nancy Spero: <i>The War Series 1966-70</i> at <a href="http://www.artnet.com/ag/galleryhomepage.asp?gid=652">Galerie Lelong</a> &#8212; it is pretty rare to see such beauty in angry, political work like this</p>
<p><img alt="spero-love,peace,glory.jpg" src="/mt/archives/spero-love,peace,glory.jpg" width="640" height="431" border="0" /><br />
Love, Peace, Glory<br />
1966<br />
Gouache and ink on paper</p>
<p>__</p>

<p><i>Contra/Post</i> at <a href="http://www.jamesgrahamandsons.com/?fuse=showing&id=57&department=c&all=1&return=current">JG Contemporary</a> &#8212; luckily the initials all worked out.  The former Jay Grimm gallery on 28th Street has been acquired by Madison Avenue stalwart James Graham and Sons.  They have a nice show of work by Joe Fyfe, James Hyde, Nancy Shaver, and others.</p>
