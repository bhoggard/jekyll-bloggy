<p>I love this <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=536&ncid=536&e=10&u=/ap/20040825/ap_on_el_pr/gop_convention_protest_signs">story</a>.  I heard about it on the radio yesterday.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Bright blue tarps, painted with glaring yellow letters, are going up on dozens of rooftops in Brooklyn, under the flight paths into busy New York airports. Thousands of delegates and convention guests peering down at the city might see messages like &#8220;No more years&#8221; and &#8220;Re-defeat Bush.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8220;We just hope that they&#8217;ll look down and ask themselves, &#8216;Why, why do they feel so strongly? Why is it that New York feels this way?&#8217;&#8221; said Genevieve Christy, who has painted more than 80 banners since thinking of the idea a few weeks ago.</p>
<p>The movement is so popular in her neighborhood that Christy, a 57-year-old consultant, is putting orders on a waiting list. She even brought supplies with her on vacation so she could keep working.</blockquote></p>
