<p>I got back a little while ago from the big march from Times Square to Washington Square Park.  It took over two hours to march the whole route, once it finally started moving around 1 or so.  The crowd has huge, with each block quite crowded.  Given that it was at least 30 blocks of people marching for hours, and typical estimates are 1000-1500 people/block, that&#8217;s in the 100,000-250,000 range.</p>
<p>We marched in a group including <a href="http://www.actupny.org/"><span class="caps">ACT</span> UP</a>, <a href="http://www.housingworks.org/">Housing Works</a>, <a href="http://www.echonyc.com/~bpcn/demo10-09-99.html">Church Ladies for Choice</a>, and an ad-hoc group assembled a few days ago of queer activists with the working title &#8220;Flaunting Peace&#8221;.</p>

<p>I also hate the way all of the coverage &#8212; including NY1 and <span class="caps">CNN</span> &#8212; spend a lot of time talking about how nice it is that there was so little violence.  Hello?  They think that the people opposed to the war are the violent ones?  NY1 also kept reminding viewers that the permit said the march would end at 4, but there were thousands still marching down Broadway, so &#8220;it should get interesting.&#8221;  Are they hoping the riot police (there were a lot around Washington Square) would give them some interesting footage?</p>
<p>On TV <span class="caps">CNN</span> also contrasted the anti-war demonstrations with smaller &#8220;support our troops&#8221; demonstrations near various bases around the country.  It&#8217;s offensive to say that anti-war people are showing hatred for our troops.  No wonder <span class="caps">CNN</span> was expelled by the Iraqis as a &#8220;propaganda tool of the government.&#8221;</p>
