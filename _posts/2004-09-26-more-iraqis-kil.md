<p>I mentioned <a href="/2004/09/theyll-get-tire.html">earlier</a> that Donald Rumsfeld said &#8220;At some point the Iraqis will get tired of getting killed.&#8221;  The Detroit Free Press <a href="http://www.freep.com/news/nw/iraq25e_20040925.htm">reports</a> that our military is killing more Iraqis than the insurgents, with many of those being civilians.</p>
<blockquote>Operations by U.S. and multinational forces and Iraqi police are killing twice as many Iraqis &#8212; most of them civilians &#8212; as attacks by insurgents are, according to statistics compiled by the Iraqi Ministry of Health.<br />
<br />
According to the ministry, which provided the Free Press with the figures Friday, the interim Iraqi government recorded 3,487 Iraqi deaths in 15 of the country&#8217;s 18 provinces from April 5 &#8212; when the ministry began compiling the data &#8212; until Sept. 19. Of those, 328 were women and children. Another 13,720 Iraqis were injured, the ministry said.<br />
<br />
While most of the dead are believed to be civilians, the data include an unknown number of police and Iraqi national guardsmen. Many Iraqi deaths, especially of insurgents, are never reported, so the actual number of Iraqis killed in fighting could be higher.<br />
<br />
During the same period, 432 U.S. soldiers were killed.<br />
<br />
Iraqi officials said the statistics proved that U.S. air strikes targeting insurgents also were killing large numbers of civilians. Some of the officials say these casualties are undermining popular acceptance of the U.S.-backed interim government.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
The Health Ministry is the only organization that attempts to track deaths through government agencies. The U.S. military said it kept estimates, but refused to release them.</blockquote>
