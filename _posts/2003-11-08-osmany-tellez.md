<p>This entry is kind of a reminder note:  remember the name of Osmany Tellez, a Cuban-born choreographer I saw last night at <a href="http://www.dtw.org/2003fall/split_stream1_fall_03.htm">Dance Theater Workshop</a> in a work titled &#8220;Descending Matter (Illusion Course).&#8221;  I noticed in his biography that he has performed with <a href="http://www.circusamok.org/">Circus Amok</a>!</p>
<p>He and three women dancers &#8212; Astrud Angarita, Sigal Bergman, and Rebecca Serrell &#8212; danced the piece to music by Guy Yarden with beautiful costumes by Astrud Angarita and lighting by Chloe Z. Brown.</p>
<p>I know even less about describing dance than I do about visual art, so I&#8217;ll just say it was a beautiful mix of sexy, smart, original movement.  I heard him talking to someone afterward about how creates works as collaborations with the dancers, and acts as choreographer <strong>and</strong> a kind of director/facilitator for improvisatory performers.  Watch for him.</p>
<p>&#8220;command/shift/delete&#8221; by <a href="http://www.dancespacecenter.org/air.html#flomin">Ori Flomin</a> on the same program was a very smart piece as well, with an interesting mix of intimacy and distance between the all-male cast.  Very appropriate for a work inspired by our technology-soaked world.</p>
<p>There was another work on the program, by Nia Love.  Let&#8217;s agree not to talk about it.</p>
