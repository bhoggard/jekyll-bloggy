<p>I just watched a video interview of <a href="http://www.artnet.com/">Artnet</a> founder and <span class="caps">CEO</span> Hans Neuendorf and Thomas Eller, the Editor-in-Chief of Artnet Magazine, on <a href="http://vernissage.tv/blog/2006/12/19/artnet-interview-with-hans-neuendorf-and-thomas-eller-part-13/">VernissageTV</a>.  The biggest story I found in the interview was the discussion of Artnet planning to launch an online art auction platform, a kind of eBay for the art world.  The reason this would be a big deal for the auction houses, should they pull it off, is that they are talking about commissions in the 5-10% range.  Neuendorf suggested that artists might use it, not just collectors and dealers.</p>
<p>Other highlights:</p>
<ul>
	<li>Hans Neuendorf is very interested in the idea of market transparency, so that artists, dealers, and collectors have a better idea of how certain artists and works are really being priced.</li>
	<li>The importance of online platforms for art, since there are a large number of artists without access to the market.  In an age with very high real estate prices, galleries can&#8217;t afford to rent more space to show more artists and works even if they wish to.</li>
	<li>Liquidity is a problem for the art market &#8211; galleries with big inventories have significantly more financial risk than auction houses.</li>
	<li>At least 40,000 artists graduating from art schools in <span class="caps">USA</span> each year.</li>
</ul>
<p>I also like this point (paraphrasing): &#8220;There are easier ways to make money than being an art dealer, despite all the talk of money &#8212; they&#8217;re idealists.&#8221;</p>
