<p>We all knew the 30-hour Senate debate was just show biz, but we hadn&#8217;t realized quite how much.</p>
<p>Via <a href="http://www.thehill.com/story.asp?id=139">The Hill</a>:</p>
<blockquote>The 30-hour debate on President Bush&#8217;s judicial nominees began on a testy note Wednesday night.<br />
<br />
After Republicans walked into the Senate chamber together to begin the extraordinary session, Democrats argued that their move was not a show of unity but rather a television stunt orchestrated for Fox News. They pointed to a memo from Manuel Miranda, a staffer for Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist (R-Tenn.), which said:<br />
<br />
&#8220;It is important to double efforts to get your boss to S-230 on time &#8230; Fox News Channel is really excited about this marathon and Brit Hume at 6 would love to open with all our 51 senators walking onto the floor &#8212; the producer wants to know will we walk in exactly at 6:02 when the show starts so they get it live to open Brit Hume&#8217;s show? Or if not, can we give them an exact time for the walk-in start?&#8221;<br />
<br />
Democrats had unsuccessfully attempted to delay the debate until 8 p.m. to allow the Senate to first complete its work on the VA/<span class="caps">HUD</span> appropriations bill.</blockquote>
<p>Democrats have confirmed 168 of the president&#8217;s judicial nominees since 2001 and have only blocked four.  Republicans <a href="http://wireservice.wired.com/wired/story.asp?section=Breaking&storyId=798410&tw=wn_wire_story">blocked</a> 63 of former Democratic President Bill Clinton&#8217;s nominees, denying many of them even a confirmation hearing while in the majority.</p>
