<p>Via <a title="Eschaton" href="http://atrios.blogspot.com/2003_07_27_atrios_archive.html#105940140351785819">Eschaton</a> I see there is a Washington Post article that says we are now taking hostages in Iraq as part of our &#8220;tactics&#8221;.  I think the headline is a bit weak given the item about taking families of Iraqi officials hostage that&#8217;s buried deep in the article.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/articles/A54345-2003Jul27.html">	<br />
U.S. Adopts Aggressive Tactics on Iraqi Fighters</a></p>
<blockquote>Col. David Hogg, commander of the 2nd Brigade of the 4th Infantry Division, said tougher methods are being used to gather the intelligence. On Wednesday night, he said, his troops picked up the wife and daughter of an Iraqi lieutenant general. They left a note: &#8220;If you want your family released, turn yourself in.&#8221; Such tactics are justified, he said, because, &#8220;It&#8217;s an intelligence operation with detainees, and these people have info.&#8221; They would have been released in due course, he added later.<br />
<br />
The tactic worked. On Friday, Hogg said, the lieutenant general appeared at the front gate of the U.S. base and surrendered.</blockquote>
