<p>I&#8217;m reading a great book at the moment: Roger Shattuck&#8217;s <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0394704150/tristanmedial-20">The Banquet Years</a>, about the origins of the avant garde in France in the period from the 1880s to the beginning of World War I.  It&#8217;s the era of Rousseau, Alfred Jarry, Apollinaire, and near the end, Picasso and Gertrude Stein.  After I finish it I intend to re-read Stein&#8217;s <a href="http://www.powells.com/cgi-bin/partner?partner_id=26418&cgi=product&isbn=067972463x">The Autobiography of Alice B. Toklas</a>, since it describes events around this time.  Powell&#8217;s has just put up Edmund Wilson&#8217;s <a href="http://www.powells.com/cgi-bin/partner?partner_id=26418&html=review/2003_07_31.html">1933 review</a> of the book on its web site.   Go check it out.</p>
