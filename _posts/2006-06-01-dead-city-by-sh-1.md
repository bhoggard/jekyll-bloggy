<p>While Mom was visiting, we took her to see a wonderful new play by Sheila Callaghan, titled <em>Dead City</em>.  We saw the first preview, but the cast and production were so good I wouldn&#8217;t have guessed that we weren&#8217;t well into the run.  The title is a reference to a song by Patti Smith, and the form of the play is inspired by James Joyce&#8217;s <em>Ulysses</em>.  It&#8217;s presented by the <a href="http://www.newgeorges.org/">New Georges</a>, a theater group dedicated to producing works by female playwrights.  Our friend the director Anne Kauffman is a member.</p>
<p>From their website:</p>
<blockquote><span class="caps">DEAD</span> <span class="caps">CITY</span> is a contemporary riff on Joyce&#8217;s Ulysses, set 100 years after the novel, in New York City, with a woman protagonist.  Just as Ulysses is a story of Dublin, <span class="caps">DEAD</span> <span class="caps">CITY</span> is consumed with the feel of our city, in our time.</blockquote>
<p>A play which riffs on <em>Ulysses</em>, Patti Smith, and Rimbaud is worth a visit to downtown Manhattan, and the wondefully space-age <a href="http://www.3leggeddog.org/">3 Legged Dog Art and Technology Center</a> is a great place to see a work that uses quite a bit of video projections and other multimedia.  The video and  production design is by William Cusick.  I <a href="/2004/01/glamericans-mee.html">wrote about</a> another project of his, The Big Art Group&#8217;s <em>House of No More</em>.  The Village Voice has an <a href="http://www.villagevoice.com/theater/0621,reidy,73321,11.html">interview</a> with the playwright if you want to learn more.</p>
<p>It opens tonight at 7, and runs through June <strike>24th</strike> 30th.  Tickets are only $19, but if you use the code &#8220;<span class="caps">NGWEB</span>&#8221; on the <a href="http://www.smarttix.com/show.aspx?showCode=DEA9">Smarttix</a> site, it&#8217;s only $12.  How can you go wrong at that price?</p>
