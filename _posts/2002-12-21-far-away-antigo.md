<p>Caryl Churchill&#8217;s latest play, &#8220;Far Away,&#8221; is at <a href="http://www.nytw.org/">New York Theater Workshop</a>, and it&#8217;s brilliant.  I don&#8217;t feel like I can write a good review of it, but the people that call it a &#8220;tiny epic&#8221; are correct.  It&#8217;s about an hour long, but it has more substance than most plays I&#8217;ve seen lately.  Frances McDormand is brilliant, as are the other members of the cast.  The equally fabulous Kathleen Chalfant will take over in McDormand&#8217;s role soon.  The sexy Chris Messina, last seen (by us at least) in &#8220;&#8221;http://www.jameswagner.com/index.php?m=200205#58&quot;&gt;This Thing of Darkness</a>,&quot; by Craig Lucas and David Schulner, also stars.</p>
<p>I know most of my friends in NY don&#8217;t have a lot of money to spend on culture, so check out the &#8220;Cheap Tickets&#8221; link at the top of the <span class="caps">NYTW</span> site for information on rush tickets or ushering.</p>
<p><a href="http://query.nytimes.com/search/full-page?res=9F0CE5D71331F931A25752C1A9649C8B63">NY Times review</a></p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p>Tonight we saw Mac Wellman&#8217;s &#8220;Antigone&#8221; at <a href="http://www.dtw.org/">Dance Theater Workshop</a>, a collaboration with Annie-B Parson.  It&#8217;s great fun, and it was one of the most beautiful &#8212; visually, musically, and verbally &#8212; evenings of theatre I&#8217;ve had in years.  The cast is awesome, and the music and sound design are by <a href="http://www.gloriadeluxe.com/">Cynthia Hopkins</a>.  The <a href="http://www.villagevoice.com/issues/0250/chaudhuri.php">Village Voice</a> review seems pretty fair to me.</p>
