<p>All hail the power of Google.  Someone from Casey Kaplan gallery found my <a href="/2003/04/art.html">mention</a> of the Jeff Burton show &#8220;Kevin&#8221; in April and sent me some images and the press release.  Here is the description from the press release:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>For his fifth solo exhibition in New York, Jeff Burton will present &#8220;Kevin,&#8221; a new series of portrait photographs taken from a brief exchange with a young male hustler.</p>
<p>This is the first exhibition that the artist will devote entirely to portraiture. Burton&#8217;s previous shows have explored the cultural and sexual landscape of southern California through his involvement in Hollywood&#8217;s adult film industry. The photographs portray elements of both voyeurism and anonymity.</p>
<p>In contrast to Burton&#8217;s earlier work, &#8220;Kevin&#8221; demonstrates a shift in focus by the artist where we are presented with eight photographs recording an individual&#8217;s performance. Burton&#8217;s own significant history within the adult film industry contributes to the powerful images of &#8220;Kevin&#8221; that unfold. The artist&#8217;s approach to photographing this character communicates the tension of a highly private moment that developed publicly in a crowded New Orleans bar. &#8220;Kevin&#8221; offers himself in a spontaneous performative act. The resulting photographs demonstrate an intimate moment shared between photographer and model making this Burton&#8217;s boldest work to date.</blockquote></p>
<p>My understanding is that the whole set of images was photographed in less than half an hour.</p>
<p>They&#8217;re not particularly work-safe, so click on &#8220;More&#8221; to see them.  We saw it while my mom was here and she liked it too!</p>
