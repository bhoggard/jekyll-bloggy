<p>Via <a href="http://edition.cnn.com/2003/US/10/16/rumsfeld.boykin.ap/"><span class="caps">CNN</span></a>:</p>
<blockquote>Defense Secretary Donald H. Rumsfeld and the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff are defending a new deputy undersecretary of defense &#8220;who has reportedly cast the war on terror&#8221; in religious terms.<br />
<br />
Lt. Gen. William G. Boykin, whose promotion and appointment was confirmed by the Senate in June, has said publicly that he sees the war on terrorism as a clash between Judeo-Christian values and Satan, the Los Angeles Times reported Thursday.<br />
<br />
Appearing in dress uniform before a religious group in Oregon in June, Boykin said Islamic extremists hate the United States &#8220;because we&#8217;re a Christian nation, because our foundation and our roots are Judeo-Christians. &#8230; And the enemy is a guy named Satan.&#8221; <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
Discussing a U.S. Army battle against a Muslim warlord in Somalia in 1993, Boykin told one audience, &#8220;I knew my god was bigger than his. I knew that my god was a real god and his was an idol.&#8221;</blockquote>
