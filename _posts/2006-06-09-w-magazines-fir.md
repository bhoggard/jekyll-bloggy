<p>Related to my earlier <a href="/2006/06/what-now-for-bo.html">post</a> on the rich and their hunger for art/bohemia, I learn that <em>W Magazine</em> will have its <a href="http://www.nypost.com/business/64889.htm">first-ever art issue this fall</a>.  I believe the <span class="caps">LTB</span> Magazine will be called <em>Culture and Travel</em>, not <em>Art &amp; Culture</em> as the NY Post says.</p>
<blockquote>W magazine&#8217;s first-ever art issue is slated to hit newsstands in October, and will cover major artists, auction houses and dealers.<br />
<br />
Some may chalk this one up to a simmering rivalry between James Truman, the ex-editorial director of Conde Nast, and Patrick McCarthy, the editorial director of stablemate W.<br />
<br />
Truman is now the <span class="caps">CEO</span> of <span class="caps">LTB</span> Media, a company that has as its flagship Art &amp; Auction. He&#8217;s also introducing his newest magazine, Art &amp; Culture, in September, but is said to be struggling to find ads.<br />
<br />
McCarthy denies any rivalry with Truman. &#8220;It has nothing to do with that. We&#8217;ve had this on the drawing board for nine months,&#8221; McCarthy said of W&#8217;s art number.<br />
<br />
Still, he concedes, it is a first for W. And while he&#8217;ll be covering the art world, he said he&#8217;ll be going to the same high-end fashion advertisers. </blockquote>
