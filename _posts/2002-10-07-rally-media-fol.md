<p>The New York Times <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/10/07/nyregion/07PEAC.html">article</a> is horrible.  My feeble post was as journalistic as their report, which mostly quotes people with the most tangential messages to show that it was &#8220;just a bunch of kooks &#8212; nothing to worry about&#8221;.  A lot of people at the rally itself spoke very well on the use of 9/11 as an excuse for the Bush administration to do what it likes anyway in terms of stifling dissent and shredding the Bill of Rights.  They also talked about how talk of war is being used to distract the country from the problems of corporate crime, the bad economy, and people&#8217;s worries about their retirement.  Here&#8217;a a good Alternet <a href="http://www.alternet.org/story.html?StoryID=14217">column</a> on this topic, whose thesis is that we may not even go to war.  War talk is being used to win the election.</p>
<p>The Daily News&#8217;s <a href="http://nydailynews.com/front/story/24929p-23591c.html">coverage</a> is better.  They and the other media outlets, such as the <span class="caps">BBC</span>, also use higher numbers than the Times.</p>
