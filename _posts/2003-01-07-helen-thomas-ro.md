<p><a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/news/releases/2003/01/20030106-1.html">Press Briefing by Ari Fleischer &#8211; January 6, 2003</a></p>
<blockquote>Q At the earlier briefing, Ari, you said that the President deplored the taking of innocent lives. Does that apply to all innocent lives in the world? And I have a follow-up. <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: I refer specifically to a horrible terrorist attack on Tel Aviv that killed scores and wounded hundreds. And the President, as he said in his statement yesterday, deplores in the strongest terms the taking of those lives and the wounding of those people, innocents in Israel. <br />
<br />
Q My follow-up is, why does he want to drop bombs on innocent Iraqis? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: Helen, the question is how to protect Americans, and our allies and friends &#8212; <br />
<br />
Q They&#8217;re not attacking you. <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: &#8212; from a country &#8212; <br />
<br />
Q Have they laid the glove on you or on the United States, the Iraqis, in 11 years? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: I guess you have forgotten about the Americans who were killed in the first Gulf War as a result of Saddam Hussein&#8217;s aggression then. <br />
<br />
Q Is this revenge, 11 years of revenge? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: Helen, I think you know very well that the President&#8217;s position is that he wants to avert war, and that the President has asked the United Nations to go into Iraq to help with the purpose of averting war. <br />
<br />
Q Would the President attack innocent Iraqi lives? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: The President wants to make certain that he can defend our country, defend our interests, defend the region, and make certain that American lives are not lost. <br />
<br />
Q And he thinks they are a threat to us? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: There is no question that the President thinks that Iraq is a threat to the United States. <br />
<br />
Q The Iraqi people? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: The Iraqi people are represented by their government. If there was regime change, the Iraqi &#8212; <br />
<br />
Q So they will be vulnerable? <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: Actually, the President has made it very clear that he has not dispute with the people of Iraq. That&#8217;s why the American policy remains a policy of regime change. There is no question the people of Iraq &#8212; <br />
<br />
Q That&#8217;s a decision for them to make, isn&#8217;t it? It&#8217;s their country. <br />
<br />
MR. <span class="caps">FLEISCHER</span>: Helen, if you think that the people of Iraq are in a position to dictate who their dictator is, I don&#8217;t think that has been what history has shown. <br />
<br />
Q I think many countries don&#8217;t have &#8212; people don&#8217;t have the decision &#8212; including us. </blockquote>
