<p>Given my lack of time for blogging, and knowing more people would see it and discuss it there, I shared my notes from my rant on the last night of <a href="http://jameswagner.com/2010/03/class.html">#class</a> with <a href="http://www.artfagcity.com/2010/03/30/why-the-fuck-do-you-get-up-in-the-morning-class/">Art Fag City</a>.  Don&#8217;t miss the comments.</p>
<p>Part of the point of #class was to propose solutions, not just whine, so here are my thoughts.  As the number of culture critics and writers decline in the printed media, the online world is replacing them, but getting paid enough to write is a big problem, even for relatively well-known writers such as Paddy Johnson of Art Fag City.  As the co-founder of <a href="http://www.culturepundits.com/">Culture Pundits</a> and <a href="http://idiommag.com/">Idiom</a>, it&#8217;s something I worry about quite often, and both were founded to find some support for good writing.</p>
<p>My proposal: arts organizations such as <a href="http://www.artdealers.org/">The Art Dealers Association of America</a> and the <a href="http://newartdealers.org/" title="NADA">New Art Dealers Alliance</a> should use a portion of their membership dues to fund arts writing.  I&#8217;m sure similar groups exist for theater and dance as well, but the area I know best is the visual arts.  In the long run, they need people to write about art, including their artists and exhibitions, and if people are too broke or busy freelancing to do so, no one wins.  For a fraction of the cost of attending even a single art fair, the pooled resources could make a big difference in the quality and quantity of art criticism.  Heck, perhaps some of this money could even fund some good editors to work with bloggers and other writers who would like that assistance!</p>
<p>Implementation details, such as an advisory committee for handing out the money, can be discussed.  I would strongly recommend against a big proposal process, as I think that takes away from the time writers could use for better purposes.  Writers who are interested in being considered could fill out a simple web form with a link to some samples of their writing for a committee to consider.  In the interest of smoothing cash flow for all parties involved, the awards could even be monthly payments rather than lump sums.  PayPal works very nicely for that.</p>
