<p>Brenda Fassie, the South African singer, has <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/3700309.stm">died</a> at the age of 39.  At some point in the 90s I heard of her and bought a CD.  She had a great voice, and a difficult life.</p>
<p>Most people reading this have probably never have heard of her.  For several reasons I tend to pay attention to South Africa and its culture.  One reason: James lived there eight months in the 70s.  Another: we know people from South Africa, such as Gary and Lisa who run <a href="http://www.axisgallery.com/">Axis Gallery</a>.  I think I have always found it an amazing, inspiring story &#8212; the end of apartheid without total civil war.  I realize it&#8217;s still far from perfect, but it is a more hopeful story than one of bombing people into &#8220;liberation.&#8221;</p>
<p>I remember the 1990 parade for Nelson Mandela in lower Manhattan.  At that time I was working on the 100th floor of 2 <span class="caps">WTC</span> for Lehman Brothers.  Only one person in the whole office left to join me to watch the parade.</p>
