<p>Bloomberg is turning into the new Giuliani.  Not only is he talking about a <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/story/31568p-29932c.html">West Side stadium</a>, the Police are <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/local/story/31567p-29942c.html">starting</a> to treat homeless people as a &#8220;quality of life issue&#8221;:</p>
<blockquote>Police Commissioner Raymond Kelly said yesterday that the homeless have been targeted since February in Operation Clean Sweep, a crackdown that focuses on quality-of-life crimes using the same computer-assisted strategy that the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> uses to drive down major crimes.<br />
<br />
Kelly said that while &#8220;being homeless is not a violation of the law,&#8221; trespassing and sleeping in city parks are against the law.<br />
<br />
&#8220;Basically, what our outreach program does is contact homeless people and offer them assistance, shelter and hospitalization,&#8221; he said.<br />
<br />
That was certainly not the policy on E. Fourth St. yesterday, where several witnesses watched officers from the Ninth Precinct verbally abuse a pair of homeless men and throw their Styrofoam shelter into a city garbage truck.<br />
<br />
&#8220;Don&#8217;t talk to me like I have a heart, because I don&#8217;t!&#8221; one beefy cop told the pair, according to Joseph Esposito, 37, who witnessed the exchange on his way to work.<br />
<br />
At no time were any services offered to the men, said Esposito, an assertion later backed up by the pair in an interview with the Daily News.<br />
<br />
&#8220;Instead of just saying, &#8216;Hey, listen, you have to leave,&#8217; it was, &#8216;I don&#8217;t ever want to see you in my precinct again,&#8217;&#8221; said one of the men, who refused to give his name out of fear of reprisal. &#8220;They gave us nothing.&#8221;</blockquote>
<p>Good for the Daily News!  These two articles were on the same page today.</p>
