<p>I apologize for writing about a show that just closed, but it&#8217;s been hard to stay on the mailing list of Pierogi for some reason.  We saw a great group show there, <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/998">Reconfigure</a>, on Sunday.  In addition to great work from some people we own (James Esber and Reed Anderson), we also saw some work by people new to us.  I really loved the enamel-esque paintings of <a href="http://www.pierogi2000.com/flatfile/talbotl.html">Lynn Talbot</a>, and two funny, brilliant paintings by Andrew Moszynski.</p>

<p>Also, don&#8217;t miss the current show at Jack the Pelican, titled <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/989">No Apology for Breathing</a>.  It&#8217;s curated by Matthew Lusk, who curated last year&#8217;s <em>Some Exhaust</em> show at Lehmann Maupin.  It&#8217;s a great political show with an ambitious installation meant to evoke <a href="http://www.poolproject.org/">McCarren Park Pool</a>.  The pool was a <span class="caps">WPA</span> project that has been allowed to deteriorate and is no longer usable.  The city was proposing to refurbish it for the Olympics bid.  Can&#8217;t afford to maintain it now, but we might find the money if it&#8217;s part of the Olympics!  Typical.</p>
