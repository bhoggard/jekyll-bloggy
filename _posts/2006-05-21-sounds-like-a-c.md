<p>&#8220;Sounds like a culture war&#8221; was James&#8217;s reaction when I told him about the <em>New York Daily News</em> editorial on the <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/10/2557">Brooklyn College <span class="caps">MFA</span> show</a>.</p>
<blockquote><strong>&#8216;Plan B&#8217; from outer space</strong><br />
<br />
The mewling you hear in Brooklyn emanates from Brooklyn College art students whose education obviously did not include informing them that, no, you can&#8217;t do anything you want anywhere you please.<br />
<br />
The young artistes are up in arms that borough Parks Commissioner Julius Spiegel closed their exhibit, &#8220;Plan B,&#8221; at the Brooklyn War Memorial after some works &#8211; including a penis sculpture &#8211; were deemed inappropriate for display in a hall that is open to the general public and, just as important, part of a commemoration of valor. How dare Spiegel close the show without asking them? the students fume. And how dare the Brooklyn College administration fail to defend their rights to show their stuff just where they please?<br />
<br />
Reality check, children: Not only can someone tell you &#8220;no,&#8221; but &#8211; shocking! &#8211; being told &#8220;no&#8221; is not a violation of your First Amendment rights. Spiegel showed backbone, and the college made the wise choice in moving the show onto campus, where students, faculty and assorted Chardonnay sippers can muse about its merits. Get used to not being the center of the universe, kids.</blockquote>
<p>Chardonnay sippers?!  Everyone knows real men don&#8217;t have anything to do with education and going to college!</p>
