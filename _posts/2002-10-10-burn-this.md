<p>&#8220;Make it personal, tell the truth and then write &#8216;Burn this&#8217; on the bottom.&#8221;</p>
<p>We saw <a href="http://www.signaturetheatre.org/F_playing.html">Burn This</a> last night, and it was awesome.  I&#8217;ve been going to Signature Theatre for years, and this was <b>not</b> the typical crowd for one of their plays.  This was much more like a Broadway audience, and believe me that&#8217;s not a compliment:  plenty of scary suburban New York Magazine types.  It was a gayer crowd than usual, but I suspect that was as much a function of having Ed Norton in it (who was great despite <a href="http://www.revolutionfreedom.com/0902.html#090402">someone&#8217;s opinion</a> of him), rather than the fact that it was a gay-themed play by a great playwright.  I don&#8217;t typically see that many pretty, blank Chelsea boys at a serious play &#8212; perhaps they were brought as arm candy by more serious playgoers.  I much prefer <a href="http://www.ultrasparky.org/">smart</a> <a  href="http://www.mrswill.com/">pretty</a> <a href="http://www.eastwest.nu/blog.shtml">boys</a>.</p>
<p>I first saw <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Norton,+Edward">Ed Norton</a> in 1994, in the world premiere of Eward Albee&#8217;s &#8220;Fragments&#8221; at Signature.  I still remember how he looked and talked, and not just because he was hot.  Apparently this was <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Name?Keener,+Catherine">Catherine Keener</a>&#8216;s stage debut, and she was excellent, with a great presence on stage.  I would not have guessed that she had concentrated only on film before.  I&#8217;ve only seen her in one movie, &#8220;Being John Malkovich&#8221;.  Given that Mr. Malkovich played the Ed Norton role in the play&#8217;s debut, there is a nice symmetry at work here.  The entire cast of four was pretty amazing.</p>
<p>There were a few celebrities in the crowd.  The ones I spotted:  Monica Lewinsky (I think I was cruised by her!), <a href="http://www.ny1.com/ny/AboutNY1/StaffProfiles/index.html?topicintid=9&subtopicintid=37&contentintid=344">Paul Lombardi</a> (yes <a href="http://isobars.blogspot.com/2002_06_01_isobars_archive.html">Sam</a> he&#8217;s quite hot in person too), and <a href="http://www.bard.org/SectionEducate/rudnick.html">Paul Rudnick</a>.</p>
