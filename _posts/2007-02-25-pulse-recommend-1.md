<p>I feel dreadful &#8212; probably got sick standing outside Pulse waiting for the shuttle to the Armory that never arrived, so here are my recommendations sans links before I&#8217;m off to bed.  Images will be on flickr soon.</p>
<p>Top pick: Ivin Ballen&#8217;s sculptures at Winklemen.  Ed has made quite a discovery.</p>
<p>Also, go to the men&#8217;s room to witness Jade Townsend&#8217;s installation in a bathroom stall.  I hope to upload a video with sound later.</p>
<p>Others:</p>
<ul>
	<li>Bitforms &#8211; Mark Napier inkjet print plus a software-driven video</li>
	<li>Ruben Kindermans&#8217;s minimalist performance videos &#8212; like a stripped-down John Bock &#8212; at Annie Gentils.  You might have to ask to see them, so do that when you visit the booth.</li>
	<li>Chad Robertson (I wrote about his sexy zombies in the past) oils on paper, Heather Cantrell photos, and Wendy Heldmann (especially the works on paper) at sixspace</li>
	<li>Bari Ziperstein collages at Bank</li>
	<li>Shaun O&#8217;Dell&#8217;s gouache on paper work, Brad Tucker&#8217;s sculpture, and Gilad Efrat&#8217;s painting at Inman</li>
	<li>Pretty much everything at Pavel Zoubok &#8212; May Wilson, Christopher Tanner, Stefan Saffer, Ginnie Gardner, Thomas Lanigan-Schmidt &#8212; the most shiny and baroque booth!</li>
	<li>Tilman Peschel photos at Anne de Villepoix</li>
	<li>Chris Duncan painting and editioned zine at Jeff Bailey</li>
	<li>Stephen Brandes at Rubicon</li>
	<li>Elana Blasco mixed media on acetate sheets at Galeria Fucares</li>
	<li>Sam Dargan&#8217;s political/conspiratorial paintings at Rokeby</li>
	<li>David Humphrey paintings at Keith Talent</li>
	<li>Brody Condon&#8217;s video game meets Flemish religious painting work at Virgil de Voldere</li>
</ul>
