<p><a href="http://jameswagner.com/">James</a> and I attended the Rhizome <a href="http://rhizome.org/events/blogging_and_the_arts/">Blogging and the Arts</a> event tonight.  During the discussion, Lisa from <a href="http://culturekitchen.com/">CultureKitchen</a> mentioned the concern about intellectual property and just letting art or writing be out there that scares so many artists and writers.  I was reminded of the wonderful and generous playwright Charles Mee.  His <a href="http://charlesmee.org/">website</a>, called the <em class="re">making project</em>, has the complete texts of his plays.  If you wish to perform them as written, or essentially as written, you have to get permission as they are copyrighted.  Otherwise, if you want to use elements of them in your own work, they are free.  To quote from the site:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Please feel free to take the plays from this website and use them as a resource for your own work: cut them up, rearrange them, rewrite them, throw things out, put things in, do whatever you like with themÂ—don&#8217;t just make a few cuts or rewrite a few passages, but pillage the plays and build your own entirely new piece out of the ruinsÂ—and then, please, put your own name to the work that results.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>But, if you would like to perform the plays essentially or substantially as I have composed them, they are protected by copyright in the versions you read here, and you need to clear performance rights with&#8230;</p>
</blockquote>
