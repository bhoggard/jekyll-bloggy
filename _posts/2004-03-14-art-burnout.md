<p>Yes, I have been to both <a href="http://www.scope-art.com/">Scope</a> and <a href="http://www.thearmoryshow.com/index2.php">The Armory Show</a>, this weekend (one day for Scope, two for Armory).</p>
<p>At the moment I&#8217;m too exhausted for a blog post, other than saying that I was really impressed by the quality of the San Francisco and Los Angeles galleries I encountered at both fairs.  The SF galleries were also much braver about showing political art dealing with the War on Terror and 9/11 than most <span class="caps">NYC</span> galleries (except for a few like Barbara Gladstone, the only blue chip <span class="caps">NYC</span> gallery to do such a thing with its Thomas Hirschhorn installation).</p>
<p>What was with the food at The Armory Show?  It wasn&#8217;t so bad last year, and this year it was horrible, expensive, and accompanied by enough tables to seat about 5% of the people getting food at any one time.  Also, <span class="caps">BUY</span> <span class="caps">SOME</span> <span class="caps">DAMN</span> <span class="caps">BENCHES</span> <span class="caps">FOR</span> <span class="caps">THE</span> <span class="caps">ATTENDEES</span>.</p>
<p>One more thought: No one came up to me to tell me how much they loved my weblog, which cannot be said about <a href="http://jameswagner.com/">James</a>.</p>
