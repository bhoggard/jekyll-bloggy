<p>I thought <a href="http://www.ultrasparky.org">Dan</a> and others would appreciate this quote from the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/09/obituaries/09BEHA.html">obituary</a> of Brian Behan in today&#8217;s NY Times:</p>
<blockquote> His first play, &#8220;Boots for the Footless,&#8221; which played at a London theater, was picketed by Irish groups objecting to what they considered its stereotypical portrayals of Irishmen as drunk and violent and Irish women as contradictory and conniving.<br />
<br />
He was unrepentant. &#8220;I regard being stage-Irish as something of a trade like any other,&#8221; he said. &#8220;It&#8217;s something we Irish are particularly good at. After all, we have no other natural resource that I know of.&#8221;</blockquote>
