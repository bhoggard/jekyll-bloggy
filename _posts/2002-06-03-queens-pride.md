<p>We went to Jackson Heights for Queens Pride today &#8211; photos <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/qnspride2002">here</a> (automated slide show <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/slideshow.php?set_albumName=qnspride2002">here</a>).  I think the thing that moved me the most was Colega.  Colega (Colombian Lesbian and Gay Association) had people dressed as angels, marching in memory of <a href="http://www.lgny.com/eddiegarzon167.html">Edgar Garzon</a>.</p>
<p>The noise level of the crowd was less than I expected, and I think it was because it was the straightest crowd I&#8217;ve ever seen at a queer parade &#8211; a lot of Latin families out with the kids to see the parade, plus a smattering of South Asians (Pakastani, India, and Bangladeshi).  It was cool to see people of all kinds mixing in the crowds for the parade and the festival stages and booths.</p>
<p>We had a late lunch/early dinner afterward at <a href="http://newyork.citysearch.com/profile/7411723/">Jackson Diner</a>.  It was quite the crowd, thanks to the parade &#8212; including <a href="http://www.rochester.edu/college/AAH/people/crimp.htm">Douglas Crimp</a>.  As we told <a href="http://www.pennyarcade.com/">Penny Arcade</a> when we talked with her and <a href="http://www.churchofbetty.com/">Chris Real</a>, I don&#8217;t think I&#8217;ve ever been to a restaurant where she and Crimp were both present!</p>
<p>After picking up a few Hindi CDs and DVDs, we headed back home to rest for a few minutes hopping back on the subway to see Tom Donaghy&#8217;s new play <u>boys and girls</u> at <a href="http://www.playwrightshorizons.org/">Playwrights Horizons</a>&#8217; fabulous new space.  I like his plays &#8212; I&#8217;ve seen <u>Minutes from Blue Route</u> and <u>The Beginning of August</u>.  I&#8217;m too tired to write more right now, but I <b>recommend</b> going to see it.   Good play, great cast, and great direction.</p>
