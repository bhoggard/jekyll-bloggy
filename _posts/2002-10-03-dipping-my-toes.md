<p>I should be doing work, but several shows were about to close, so we went to a few Chelsea galleries this afternoon.  I&#8217;m writing this while I wait for <a href="http://www.apache.org/">Apache</a> to compile on a few clients&#8217; machines &#8212; security release!</p>
<p>I wasn&#8217;t that excited by some of the shows, but there were a couple I would recommend going to see before they close:<br />
<ul><li><a href="http://www.jameshyde.com/pages/BrentSikkema2.html">James Hyde</a> at Brent Sikkema</li><br />
<li><a href="http://www.artdealers.org/exhibitions/newyork.downtown.html#cooper">Group show</a> at Paula Cooper, especially the Dan Flavin</li><br />
<li>Group show at <a href="http://www.kravetswehbygallery.com/">Kravets/Wehby</a> (web site not updated in a year!), especially <a href="http://www.lflgallery.com/raja.html">Kanishka Raja</a></li></ul><br />
Paula Cooper gets extra points for having the flyer for the <a href="http://www.notinourname.net/">Not In Our Name</a> rally on Sunday in Central Park taped to the gallery door.</p>
<p>As I walked home, I noticed that 17th Street between Eighth and Ninth Avenues certainly is gentrifying!  There is a <a href="http://www.karimrashid.com/">Karim Rashid</a> store, plus an upscale hair salon, where people were waiting out front drinking sparkling water from wine glasses &#8212; glass ones, not plastic.</p>
