<p>We saw this painting at the Metropolitan Museum&#8217;s <a href="http://www.metmuseum.org/special/se_event.asp?OccurrenceId={5BC2299D-FC6B-11D6-94C7-00902786BF44}">El Greco</a> exhibit yesterday.  Among the best works in the show, this was one of the few I had never seen before, even in reproduction.  It&#8217;s haunting and so human, not &#8220;iconic&#8221; even though it was inspired by his early career as a Byzantine icon painter.</p>
<p>The show ends on the 11th, and is definitely worth a visit.  Take a day off and do not try to go on the weekend.  It was pretty crowded even on a Tuesday afternoon with a crowd of all ages.  I really liked hearing all of the secular Jewish New Yorkers asking each other questions.  Given that El Greco&#8217;s career was mainly spent in Philip II&#8217;s Spain, it is mostly religious and filled with Catholic themes.  Not exactly the area of expertise for a lot of people in the crowd.  I wasn&#8217;t raised Catholic, so most of what I know about Catholicism is from European art too.</p>
