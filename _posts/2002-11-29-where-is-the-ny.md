<blockquote>Arguing that this city faces a far more perilous world than once imagined, New York&#8217;s police commissioner wants to toss aside a decades-old federal court decree governing the limits on police spying and surveillance of its own citizenry.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
To infiltrate lawful political and social organizations, police must establish a suspicion of criminal activity and gain the permission of a special three-person authority.<br />
<br />
This three-person authority consists of two high-ranking police officials and a civilian appointed by the mayor. Civil libertarians argue this is hardly an onerous burden for law enforcement.</blockquote>
