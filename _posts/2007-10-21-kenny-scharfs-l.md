<p>Here are 3 videos from 26th Street yesterday in Chelsea.  The <a href="http://www.paulkasmingallery.com/current/2007-10-18_kenny-scharf/press-release/">press release</a> describes the golf cart thusly:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Carzy Roy-Al is a customized golf cart that will be on display in our second gallery space. The cart will revolve tirelessly on a spinning platform, evoking a car showroom or late-night infomercial. &#8220;Enough to satisfy all your consumer whims and needs!&#8221; Scharf explains. Don&#8217;t miss Carzy Roy-Al and Kenny Scharf, <span class="caps">KING</span> of the 3rd annual Art Parade, on September 8, 2007 in Soho. Scharf will also take the car out in Chelsea on Saturdays, handing out donuts to all.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In the last video, the guys in the cart are engaging some people upstairs who are yelling at them for making so much noise.  I know there are some recording studios on that street, so maybe they were trying to record some music.</p>
<p>I wonder if the artist and his minions had a permit for that?  If someone at a political demonstration uses a bullhorn, the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> throws the person with it in jail right away.  They make an exception if the person talks about religion a lot, somehow reasoning that it&#8217;s more &#8220;first amendment-y.&#8221;</p>
