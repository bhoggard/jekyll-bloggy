<p>From <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/pages/ShArt.jhtml?itemNo=273910&contrassID=2&subContrassID=1&sbSubContrassID=0&listSrc=Y">Ha&#8217;aretz</a>:</p>
<blockquote><span class="caps">GAZA</span> &#8211; Palestinians held a symbolic funeral yesterday for U.S. student Rachel Corrie, who was killed by an Israel Defense Forces bulldozer as she protested the demolition of a house in the southern Gaza Strips&#8217;s Rafah refugee camp.<br />
<br />
Holding a stretcher draped with an American flag, some 1,000 Palestinians marched through the refugee camp as a sign of mourning for the 23-year-old American who was killed on Sunday.<br />
<br />
&#8220;We fly a U.S. flag today to show our support to all American peace lovers &#8211; those like Rachel,&#8221; said Palestinian farmer Hassan Abu Toa&#8217;ma, 24.<br />
<br />
It was a rare change of pace for Rafah, where American flags have more often been burned than held in reverence since the Palestinian uprising began in late September 2000. </blockquote>
