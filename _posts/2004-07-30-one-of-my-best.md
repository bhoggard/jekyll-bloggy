<p>We had dinner tonight at a pretty new restaurant here in Portland: <a href="http://www.ripepdx.com/clarklewis.html">clarklewis</a>.  I would describe it as creative Italian using local ingredients.  Our meal:</p>
<p>&#8220;Peasant Salad&#8221; &#8211; chicory, radicchio, and other greens with balsamic vinegar, house-cured pancetta, walnuts, and grated cheese (Pecorino?  not sure)</p>
<p>&#8220;Arrabiata&#8221; &#8211; spicy grilled Monterey Bay calamari with arrugula</p>
<p>[above with Prosecco]</p>
<p>Spelt pasta with house-cured anchovies, fennel pollen and fronds, and red pepper &#8211; one of the best pastas I have ever had in my life, including in Italy</p>
<p>[Oregon Pinot Bianco (sorry didn&#8217;t write down maker) and Arneis from Ponzi]</p>
<p>Roasted Squab with plums, plus wax beans with braised tomatoes</p>
<p>[a Chianti Classico, plus a Barbera, then a red from Calabria and one from Bolzano]</p>
<p>Frozen almond torta with a peach semifreddo-like filling, and strawberry moscato granita (two separate desserts)</p>
<p>I was really dazzled by the food.  The atmosphere is a little annoying, with very dim lighting and lots of noise.  The food made up for it however.</p>
