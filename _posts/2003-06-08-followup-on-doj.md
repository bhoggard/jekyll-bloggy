<blockquote>&#8220;The president believes everybody ought to be treated with dignity and respect, but he does not believe we should be politicizing people&#8217;s sexual orientation,&#8221; said Scott McClellan, a White House spokesman.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
 Mr. Ashcroft, socially conservative and deeply religious, was known for his strong views against homosexuality during his days in the Senate. He said then that he considered homosexuality a sin, and he opposed legislation to protect gays.<br />
<br />
But in his confirmation hearings in 2001, he pledged not to tolerate discrimination against gays in the Justice Department. Critics said today that the decision to bar the gay pride event amounted to his backpedaling.<br />
<br />
Senator Russell D.
