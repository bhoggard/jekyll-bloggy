<p>Apparently it has become illegal to wear an anti-Bush t-shirt in a public place if he is nearby.</p>
<p><a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=694&e=4&u=/ap/20040705/ap_on_el_pr/bush">Story</a> via <a href="http://atrios.blogspot.com/">Atrios</a>:</p>
<blockquote>&#8220;Our immediate task in battle fronts like Iraq and Afghanistan (news &#8211; web sites) and elsewhere is to capture or kill the terrorists &#8230; so we do not have to face them here at home,&#8221; Bush told a cheering crowd outside the West Virginia Capitol. An enthusiastic audience estimated by state capitol police at 6,500 people waving American flags chanted, &#8220;Four more years.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Regarding Saddam, the deposed Iraqi president, Bush said: &#8220;Because we acted, the dictator, the brutal tyrant, is sitting in a prison cell.&#8221;<br />
<br />
Two Bush opponents, taken out of the crowd in restraints by police, said they were told they couldn&#8217;t be there because they were wearing shirts that said they opposed the president.</blockquote>
<p>Restraints?  They handcuffed them?</p>
