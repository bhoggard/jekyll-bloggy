<p>If we go to war with Iraq and any Americans actually die, <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/nationworld/nation/ny-usreac193095402jan19,0,6626118.story?coll=ny%2Dnationalnews%2Dheadlines">it&#8217;s not going to be pretty</a>.  I&#8217;m also baffled by the idea that the number of people who think Bush hasn&#8217;t provided adequate proof of Iraq&#8217;s crimes has <b>decreased</b>.</p>
<blockquote> A poll released Thursday by the Pew Research Center for the People &amp; the Press indicated 76 percent of Americans support a war if United Nations inspectors find evidence of nuclear, biological or chemical weapons. That support drops to 29 percent if no such weapons are discovered, even if the Iraqi government cannot prove it doesn&#8217;t have them. And if U.S. soldiers were to suffer thousands of casualties, then 48 percent would oppose the war, compared with the 43 percent who would support it. And slightly more than half do not believe Bush has sufficiently explained reasons for war.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
 According to the Pew poll, the number of respondents who say they believe Bush has clearly justified an attack on Iraq has dropped significantly since his September address at the UN. Back then a majority (52 percent to 37 percent) said they believed he had adequately explained his reasons for using military force. In the latest poll, a majority (53 percent to 42 percent) said they believe he has not clearly voiced his reasons.</blockquote>
