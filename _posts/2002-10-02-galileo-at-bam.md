<p>We went to the <span class="caps">NYC</span> premiere of Philip Glass&#8217;s new opera &#8220;Galileo Galilei&#8221; at <a href="http://www.bam.org/"><span class="caps">BAM</span></a> last night.  It was very disappointing.  The music wasn&#8217;t that interesting, the libretto wasn&#8217;t so hot, and the directing was terrible.  It seemed like a good idea:  Galileo, Philip Glass, and director/co-librettist Mary Zimmerman (Metamorphoses).</p>
<p>As <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> said, it was more like a masque than an opera, but it just didn&#8217;t work.  The direction at times drove me crazy.  At one point several people are moving in a gondola.  It moves because a person at the front pulls it, but there is a gondolier there making the motions.  That&#8217;s fine, but once they get to the middle of the stage and the boat isn&#8217;t actually moving, both the gondolier and the person with the rope at the front continue to move as if it is.  I get the idea, but I was so distracted by the fake motion of the guy pulling the boat I couldn&#8217;t listen to the music.  At another point in the opera, Galileo refuses a drink from a servant when invited to share some wine with an important cardinal in the garden of his villa.  I don&#8217;t think so.  Even if he didn&#8217;t drink, there&#8217;s no way he would have refused the glass.</p>
<p>Bad art is so depressing.</p>
