<p>James and I just bought tickets to see David Gordon&#8217;s <a href="http://www.thekitchen.org/k04_calendar.html#gordon">&#8220;Uncivil Wars: Collaborating with Brecht &amp; Eisler&#8221;</a> at The Kitchen.  We try to see everything David Gordon does, and when a work includes his wife/partner Valda Setterfield, John Kelly, and Estelle Parsons as performers, how could you miss it?  It&#8217;s also a great chance to hear some of the music of <a href="http://eislermusic.com/">Hanns Eisler</a>, one of the best and sadly underknown leftist composers of the 20th Century.    If you like the Kurt Weill/Bertolt Brecht collaborations, you&#8217;ll like the Eisler even more I suspect.</p>
<p>Speaking of Weill/Brecht, we recently watched the new <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000SFJ4KE/tristanmedial-20">Criterion <span class="caps">DVD</span></a> of &#8220;The Threepenny Opera&#8221; (1931).  Highly recommended!</p>
