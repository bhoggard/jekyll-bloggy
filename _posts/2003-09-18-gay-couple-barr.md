<p>A [Canadian] married gay couple say they were <a href="http://www.cbc.ca/stories/2003/09/18/gay_customs030918">refused entry</a> into the U.S. because an American customs officer wouldn&#8217;t accept their clearance forms as a family.</p>
<p>Kevin Bourassa and Joe Varnell said they ended their trip to Georgia because the customs official at Toronto&#8217;s Pearson airport insisted they fill out separate forms as single people.</p>
<p>Bourassa said he complained to a customs supervisor and was told the couple wouldn&#8217;t be allowed to enter the U.S. as a family because the country doesn&#8217;t recognize same-sex marriages.</p>
