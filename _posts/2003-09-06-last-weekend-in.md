<p>The last thing I&#8217;m posting is a sad one that makes me angry.  When something happens like the seawall crumbling in a Manhattan park across the river, it gets fixed.  We don&#8217;t put up chain link fences to cut off people from the water for years, hoping it will eventually be restored before the whole park falls into the river.  The predominantly non-white people who visit the Queensbridge Park don&#8217;t get such treatment.  The parks along the water were full of families having cookouts and enjoying themselves, but most of the park was cut off from the water.  A study, not an actual repair plan, <a href="http://www.licbdc.org/news/queens-chron-3-17-03-seawall.htm">has been announced</a> regarding what&#8217;s going to happen.  The park was built in the 1930s, when our country was better able to find money to fund public amenities <a href="/2003/09/money-for-schoo.html">than it is now</a>.</p>
