<p>I not a big fan of marriage &#8211; gay or otherwise.  I would prefer a world where we Americans all get to have health insurance and a decent retirement and the ability to leave our estates to someone we love regardless of our domestic arrangements.  However, if we&#8217;re going to provide lots of benefits to married people that single people can&#8217;t have, then damn it same sex couples should be able to have them too.</p>
<p>The NY Times <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/pages/fashion/weddings/index.html">&#8220;Weddings &amp; Celebrations&#8221;</a> page on Sunday made sure we remember who really counts.  On a day when they reported on the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/12/21/fashion/weddings/21MCNA.html">marriage</a> of Terrence McNally (a quite commercially if not always artistically successful playwright) and Thomas Kirdahy, they stick it down on the page without a photo, after the heteros.  I would have expected that to be the featured event on the page, given that he lives in <span class="caps">NYC</span> and his plays are regularly produced in big theatres.</p>
<p>Which ceremony did they choose for the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2003/12/21/fashion/weddings/21VOWS.html">&#8220;Vows&#8221;</a> feature?  Senator John Warner&#8217;s third marriage, in DC, to an Alexandria, VA real estate agent.</p>
<p>Nice priorities.</p>
