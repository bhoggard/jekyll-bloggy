<blockquote>However, not everyone is impressed with Williamsburg&#8217;s economic potential. Some, like <a href="http://www.bellwethergallery.com/">Bellwether Gallery</a>&#8216;s director, Rebecca Smith, see more limitations than possibilities. &#8220;A lot of people think Williamsburg is cutting edge,&#8221; Smith says. &quot;It&#8217;s not, it&#8217;s just low risk, without being particularly experimental.&quot;<br />
<br />
Frustrated with her inability to get Manhattan collectors to travel across the bridge, Smith is relocating to Chelsea, even though she runs one of the better-known galleries in the neighborhood.<br />
<br />
Not surprisingly, many disagree with both her claims of poor foot traffic and her opinion of Williamsburg.<br />
<br />
&#8220;She&#8217;s going where the money is, and the power,&#8221; artist Powhida says. &#8220;I don&#8217;t blame her for wanting to sell her artists, but how good they are is up for debate. She&#8217;s not all that cutting edge either.&#8221;</blockquote>
