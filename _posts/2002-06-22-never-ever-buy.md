<p>Welcome to the end of internet radio.  I don&#8217;t buy top 40 albums, and I certainly don&#8217;t listen to the radio stations on offer in <span class="caps">NYC</span>.  The US government has decided that webcasting music is the equivalent of publishing, so that a webcast station is now subject to royalties <b>and</b> publishing fees, unlike radio broadcasts, which are only subject to the former.</p>
<p>Here is the homepage of <a href="http://www.saveinternetradio.org/default.asp">Save Internet Radio</a>, and here is a <a href="http://www.saveinternetradio.org/90seconds.asp">90-second version</a> that explains what&#8217;s going on.  For more information, I recommend reading the relevant articles on <a href="http://slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=02/06/20/2143244&mode=thread&tid=141">slashdot</a>, <a href="http://www.plastic.com/article.html?sid=02/06/21/01304849;mode=nested;threshold=1">Plastic</a> and <a href="http://www.kuro5hin.org/story/2002/6/21/73329/8778">kuro5hin</a>.</p>
<p>I think one of the important points to make here is that even broadcasters like <a href="http://www.somafm.com">soma fm</a> or <a href="http://www.tagstrance.com/home.php">Tag&#8217;s Trance Trip</a> that rarely play <a href="http://www.riaa.com/"><span class="caps">RIAA</span></a>-associated musicians, will still be subject to the fees.  The RIAA&#8217;s position is that they will sue anyone who doesn&#8217;t pay, and it will be up to the &#8220;victim&#8221; to document and prove that they don&#8217;t play any music that has fees attached.</p>
<p>This ruling is also <b>retroactive to 1998</b>.  Webcasters can be present with a bill for the <b>last four years</b> if the <span class="caps">RIAA</span> so desires.</p>
<p>One other nice item contained in the <span class="caps">DMCA</span> that our lovely congress and president (Clinton) passed in 1998, just as an example of how stupid politicians can be:  It is illegal to play more than 3 songs by the same artist in 2 hours.  Radio stations that broadcast over the air and online can&#8217;t play a full album anymore.  They can do it on their radio broadcast, but they have to replace it with something else for the online stream.  If you have a web station and want to play an hour of John Lennon&#8217;s music on the anniversary of his death, you can&#8217;t.  It&#8217;s illegal.</p>
<p>If you can find the MP3s on Kazaa or Limewire, do it.  If you can buy it at a used CD store, do it.  Remember: many of those &#8220;indy&#8221; labels are still owned by one of the biggies.  Here is RIAA&#8217;s own <a href="http://www.riaa.com/About-Members-1.cfm">members list</a>.</p>
