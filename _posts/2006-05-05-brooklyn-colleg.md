<p>I just got a news alert from Crain&#8217;s.  Link is <a href="http://newyorkbusiness.com/news.cms?newsId=13577">here</a>, but I&#8217;ll excerpt the parts I find interesting:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Commissioner Julius Spiegel ordered the exhibit &#8212; located inside the Brooklyn War Memorial Building in Cadman Plaza &#8212; shut down Thursday afternoon after receiving an unknown number of complaints, according Marni Kotak, a participating artist and spokeswoman for the exhibit, called Plan B.</p>
<p>..</p>
<p>Ms. Kotak says that three graduate students who were monitoring the exhibits, which included a watercolor featureing gay male sexuality and a sculpture of penises in a lightbox, were ordered to leave the premises and were locked out of the building on Thursday.</p>
<p>The exhibit had been approved by Brooklyn College faculty and the Wednesday night opening was attended by the president and Brooklyn College Provost Roberta Matthews. &#8220;The faculty knew ahead of time what was going to be in the exhibit,&#8221; said Ms. Kotak. Still, Brooklyn College has opted to move the exhibit rather than fight the shutdown.</p>
<p>&#8220;In keeping with the public nature of the space, as well as its position as an honored war memorial, Brooklyn College has respectfully decided to move the entire student exhibit to our campus,&#8221; said Ms. Matthews in a statement. </blockquote></p>
<p>I think moving an art show because we can&#8217;t have sexuality in &#8220;public&#8221; is bullshit.</p>
