<p>We saw <a href="http://www.bam.org/asp/performance.asp?perfID=NaharinsVirus">Naharin&#8217;s Virus</a> by the Batsheva Dance Company last night at <a href="http://www.bam.org"><span class="caps">BAM</span></a> &#8212;  Fabulous, theatrical work and brilliant dancing by a beautiful international troupe of dancers.  It&#8217;s a collaboration from the outspokenly political (and anti-war) choreographer Ohad Naharin and the Arab Palestinian musician Habib Allah Jamal, and uses the text of <a href="http://impact.civil.columbia.edu/~fawaz/handke.html">Peter Handke</a>&#8217;s <u>Offending The Audience</u>.  There was an amazing lightness, and animal energy, to the movements.  I think the NY Times <a href=http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&cid=82&ncid=82&e=2&u=/nyt/20020502/en_nyt/ambiguity_as_text__a_blackboard_as_backdrop">review</a> is pretty good.</p>
<p>There was a Q&amp;A with Naharin in the Times also, but it doesn&#8217;t show up in the archives.  But thanks to the magic of Google, <a href="http://college4.nytimes.com/guests/articles/2002/03/16/908242.xml">here</a> it is.<br />
<blockquote> You know, I&#8217;m very informed about what&#8217;s going on. And I have very clear opinions. Right now, there&#8217;s a real clash between my politics and my country&#8217;s politics. It&#8217;s very tragic what is going on because it&#8217;s obvious that eventually there will be one of two possibilities: total disaster in the region or a big compromise and peace treaty. So, if we don&#8217;t want to choose the total disaster, then it will be a peace treaty. And if the peace treaty, why wait? Why make all these mistakes? Why not just compromise now?</p>
<p>Obviously there are millions of people in this region who don&#8217;t particularly wish well for us. But the present acts of the Sharon government don&#8217;t make it safer. It&#8217;s not a government that seeks negotiations. It&#8217;s a government that has an illusion of power. And that&#8217;s something that interests me.</p>
<p>I think a lot of people in Israel live in an illusion, and that Sharon has infected a lot of people with his phobia. The phobia is really a lack of guilt. It&#8217;s blaming everybody but yourself. It actually causes you to lose any kind of sensitivity to the suffering of other people. And it&#8217;s kind of a chronic thing. That&#8217;s the illusion that I&#8217;m talking about. So maybe what I&#8217;m trying a little bit to evoke an awareness, just the ability to look at ourselves from a little bit of distance and perspective.</blockquote><br />
The crowd was a very interesting mix, with sexy Israelis, <span class="caps">NYC</span> arty people &#8212; <a href="http://www.villagevoice.com/obies/cocktail2001d.html">Jackie Hoffman</a> was in my row, and families with children.  There was a smart Jewish family in front of me &#8212; mom, dad, and two young daughters &#8212; who seemed to get a kick out of the section where a young woman tells her mother, on the day of her Bat Mitzvah, &#8220;God is an invention, just like pizza.&#8221;</p>
<p>We had to go through metal detectors to enter <span class="caps">BAM</span>, and one of the security guards told <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> that he liked his anti-war button.  James told him he would give him one if he would wear it, and he said, &#8220;certainly!&#8221;  James always carries extras for such an occasion, and recently gave one to a man walking with a rescue dog in midtown.</p>
