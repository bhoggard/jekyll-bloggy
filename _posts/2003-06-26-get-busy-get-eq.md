<p>On the heels of the Supreme Court decision killing sodomy laws, the <span class="caps">ACLU</span> isn&#8217;t going to just celebrate and party, like most homosexuals.  They realize the fight isn&#8217;t over and they&#8217;re &#8220;getting busy&#8221;.  I like the play on words.</p>
<A HREF="http://www.aclu.org/getequal/" onClick="window.open" target="ACLU"><IMG SRC="http://www.aclu.org/getequal/buttons/142x93.gif" ALT="Get Busy. Get Equal." WIDTH="142" HEIGHT="93" BORDER="0"></A><P>
<p>From the <a href="http://www.aclu.org/getequal/campaign.html">site</a>:</p>
<blockquote>After this decision, it is unconstitutional for states to make same-sex relationships a crime.  That does not mean that governments, businesses and other institutions have to treat <span class="caps">LGBT</span> people and <span class="caps">LGBT</span> relationships equally.  But it does mean that the law no longer gives them an excuse to refuse.<br />
<br />
That means we are in a better position than we have ever been before to insist that governments, business and schools treat us with dignity and respect, and give us equal treatment in day to day life.<br />
<br />
This website provides tools to: 1) get better treatment for same-sex relationships; 2) get safe schools; 3) get civil rights/discrimination policies that include <span class="caps">LGBT</span> people.  The tools include simple but important steps that will take only a few minutesÂ–like sending an electronic message to your representatives in Congress supporting a law to protect <span class="caps">LGBT</span> people from discrimination.  The tools also include more ambitious things, like a step-by-step guide showing how to get a an anti-harassment policy from your school district or a domestic partnership policy in your town or your workplace.  There are also tools to help you protect your own relationship, or to get a gay/straight alliance at your school.  And more.<br />
<br />
No one who cares about equality and dignity for <span class="caps">LGBT</span> people should let the opportunity this Supreme Court decision has given us pass without doing something to bring us a step closer to equality.  So go to the tools.  Take the easy steps, and check out the rest.<br />
<br />
Equality is up to us.</blockquote>
