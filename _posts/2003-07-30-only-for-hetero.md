<p>Amazing.  It&#8217;s the <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2003/ALLPOLITICS/07/30/bush.gay.marriage/index.html">top story</a> on <span class="caps">CNN</span> right now.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not a big fan of the institution of marriage.  I would rather live in a society where we think people are valuable and should be able to have healthcare or be taken care of in their old age or able to leave their estates to someone of their choosing without regard to their marriage status.</p>
<p><img alt="only-for-hetero.jpg" src="/mt/archives/only-for-hetero.jpg" width="419" height="543" border="0" /></p>
<blockquote>Bush said it is &#8220;important for society to welcome each individual,&#8221; but administration lawyers are looking for some way to legally limit marriage to heterosexuals.<br />
<br />
&#8220;I believe marriage is between a man and a woman, and I think we ought to codify that one way or another,&#8221; Bush told reporters at a White House news conference. &#8220;And we&#8217;ve got lawyers looking at the best way to do that.&#8221; </blockquote>
<p>Regarding Mr. Bush&#8217;s so-called tolerance, fuck that!</p>
<blockquote>Even as he made it clear that he did not support the idea of gay marriage, Bush appeared to issue a call for tolerance.<br />
<br />
&#8220;Yes, I am mindful that we&#8217;re all sinners,&#8221; the president said Wednesday when asked for his views on homosexuality. &#8220;And I caution those who may try to take the speck out of the neighbor&#8217;s eye when they&#8217;ve got a log in their own.&#8221;<br />
<br />
&#8220;I think it&#8217;s very important for our society to respect each individual, to welcome those with good hearts, to be a welcoming country,&#8221; Bush added. &#8220;On the other hand, that does not mean that somebody like me needs to compromise on an issue such as marriage.&#8221; </blockquote>
