<p>I don&#8217;t want to write only about <a href="https://bloggy.com/mt/archives/cat_war.html">war</a>.  Here are some recent art exhibits I&#8217;ve seen, plus a future recommendation.  Some of the shows have already closed.<br />
<ul><br />
<li>Jeff Burton at <a href="http://www.caseykaplangallery.com/">Casey Kaplan</a>: Trust me it was great.  Casey Kaplan has a lousy website without images.  There was a good article on it in <a href="http://www.hx.com/">HX</a> of all places &#8212; but I can&#8217;t find it online.  The whole photo shoot consisted of him spending 10 minutes with a go-go boy, Kevin, in a gay strip club in New Orleans.  Kevin is wearing a red velveteen thong, white socks, and Bass Weejuns.</li><br />
<li>Larry Mantello &#8211; &#8220;Box Rap&#8221; at <a href="http://www.huagallery.com/main.html">Henry Urbach</a> &#8212; an installation of kitsch, patriotic and otherwise</li><br />
<li>Type A at <a href="http://www.artnet.com/ag/fineartthumbnails.asp?gid=739&cid=21317">Sara Meltzer</a></li><br />
<li>Group show at John Connelly, including Wade Guyton &#8212; no web site!</li><br />
<li>Öyvind Fahlström at <a href="http://www.feigencontemporary.com/index.php?mode=exhib_current&object_id=72">Feigen</a> &#8212; brillliant Scandinavian/Brazilian conceptualist whose work seems decades ahead of its time</li><br />
<li>Doug Wada at <a href="http://www.elizabethdeegallery.com/artists/doug_wada/">Elizabeth Dee</a> &#8212; semi-photo-realistic paintings, especially for the fans and air conditioners</a></li><br />
<li>Holly Coulis at <a href="http://www.lflgallery.com/holly.html"><span class="caps">LFL</span> Gallery</a> &#8212; This gallery is a consistent favorite, especially for work of interesting young painters</li></p>
</ul>
<p>The future recommendation is <a href="http://www.lflgallery.com/jules.html">Jules de Balincourt</a>, opening at <span class="caps">LFL</span> in May.  When we were there on Saturday to decide on buying a couple of works, having seen the work when visiting to see the Holly Coulis exhibit, the back was buzzing with people looking at his work.  While we were there a collector/critic and a <a href="http://www.artnet.com/ag/fineartthumbnails.asp?gid=264&aid=7167">good artist</a> stopped by to check out his work.</p>
