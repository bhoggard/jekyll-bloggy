<p>This is my favorite paragraph of her obituary, written by Roberta Smith, in the <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2007/08/13/arts/design/13murray.html?_r=1&hp=&pagewanted=all"><em>New York Times</em></a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Born in Chicago in 1940, Ms. Murray had a hardscrabble childhood that included bouts of homelessness caused in part by the ill health of her father. Ms. Murray traced her interest in art to watching a nursery-school teacher cover a sheet of paper with thick red crayon, an experience that she said gave her an indelible sense of the physicality of color. She drew constantly from an early age, inspired mostly by newspaper comic strips, and once sent a sketchbook to Walt Disney asking for a job as his secretary. By the fifth grade she was selling erotic drawings to classmates for a quarter.</p>
</blockquote>
