<p>I just saw this story, as I was looking through news upon my return.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.salon.com/ent/movies/2002/09/27/kiarostami/index.html">Director Abbas Kiarostami, one of international cinema&#8217;s biggest names, is blocked from attending the New York Film Festival and speaking at Harvard.</a></p>
<p>This is terrible.  We&#8217;re sending the message that the only way for countries to communicate with each other is through war.  He had visited the U.S. seven times in the last ten years.</p>
<blockquote>New York Film Festival spokeswoman Ines Aslan said that festival organizers, along with the two universities involved, tried &#8220;very, very hard&#8221; to convince officials at the U.S. Embassy in Paris, where Kiarostami had applied for a visa, to make an exception for the filmmaker. &#8220;It wasn&#8217;t that they could not make an exception,&#8221; Aslan said. &#8220;It was that they did not choose to. It is very sad.&#8221; Officials at the embassy told the festival that they would require at least 90 days to investigate Kiarostami&#8217;s background &#8212; which is well known to film scholars and fans, and contains little in the way of political activity &#8212; and process the visa paperwork.</blockquote>
