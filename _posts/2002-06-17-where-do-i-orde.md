<p><a href="http://sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/n/a/2002/06/14/international1321EDT0639.DTL&nl=fix">Nude farmers creating stir in Sweden</a><br />
<blockquote>A calendar featuring nude farmers armed with pitchforks, shovels and wheelbarrows is creating a stir in Swedish agriculture circles.</p>
<p>A national farming organization, which presented a similar, hugely successful, calendar last year, is worried that the new version made by a local group of farmers could defeat the purpose of the original calendar.</p>
<p>&#8220;They&#8217;re surfing on our wave of success,&#8221; said Ann Linden, one of the creators of the national 2002 calendar.</p>
<p>Linden said the first version, featuring waist-up, black-and-white photographs of nude, young farmers in farm settings, was designed to change the image of Swedish farmers as &#8220;grumpy old men with hats.&#8221;</p>
<p>Linden said her poster boys were &#8220;attractive guys about 25-30 years old, who breed chickens, cattle or work in forestry.&#8221; </blockquote></p>
