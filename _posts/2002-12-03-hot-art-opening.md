<p>John Connelly Presents:<br />
K48-3: Teenage Rebel &#8212; The Bedroom Show<br />
Curated by Scott Hug</p>
<p>526 W 26th Street, Suite 1023<br />
New York, NY  10001</p>
<p>Exhibit Dates: December 3, 2002 &#8211; January 19, 2003<br />
Opening: Tues, December 3, 6 &#8211; 9 pm<br />
Gallery Hours: Tues &#8211; Sat, 11am &#8211; 6pm</p>
<p>&#8220;Teenage art is not an attempt to reclaim youth<br />
after it has passed, but an effort to expose the<br />
dishonesty of the domesticated adult experience,<br />
which is best served by reviving the emotional and<br />
psychological convictions of teenage-hood.&#8221;<br />
â?? Excerpt from Rachel Howe&#8217;s Teenage Art Manifesto</p>
<p>K48 is proud to present their first gallery<br />
installation, coinciding with the release of their<br />
third issue, Teenage Rebel.</p>
<p>The Bedroom Show brings together over 50 artists,<br />
fashion designers, musicians, and graphic designers<br />
into the realm of Hug&#8217;s ultimate teenage hangout.<br />
Some of the work in the show actually belongs to<br />
Hug: &#8220;These are my friends, this is what inspires<br />
me&#8221;.</p>
<p>Scott Hug created K48 as his thesis project for the<br />
Graduate Design Department at Pratt Institute.<br />
Launched in December of 2000, K48, named after a<br />
generic form of aspirin, continues Hug&#8217;s interests<br />
into information flux, allowing him to express<br />
himself through the popular form of a magazine.<br />
Collaborating with other artists, writers and<br />
musicians, Scott designs, edits, and curates each<br />
issue giving a personal touch to a medium, which in<br />
today&#8217;s corporate mediascape, only wants to sell you<br />
something.</p>
<p>&#8220;Magazines are dead,&#8221; claims Hug. &#8220;K48 is like my<br />
traveling exhibit. I want to get art into the hands<br />
of teenagers across America&#8221;.</p>
<p>The bedroom will feature customized wall paper by<br />
Eli Sudbrack, a complete entertainment system<br />
featuring The Kid America TV Show, Robert Melee&#8217;s<br />
High School Wrestling and video games; music; and a<br />
wall collage by the Providence, RI, collective<br />
Dearraindrop. Artists include Lily van der Stokker,<br />
Lucky Debellevue, Jesse Bransford, Sam Gordon, Tyson<br />
Reeder, Ryan McGinley, Slava Mogutin, Michael Meads,<br />
Lucien Samaha, Tracy Nakayama, Mike Pare, Debbie<br />
Attias, David West, Keila Lopez, Amy Steiner,<br />
Timothy Dowling, Aida Ruilova, Emily Sundblad and<br />
many others.</p>
<p>The Bedroom Show is an interactive experience. Scott<br />
is inviting people to hangout with him, play some<br />
Atari, watch some videos or just read K48.</p>
