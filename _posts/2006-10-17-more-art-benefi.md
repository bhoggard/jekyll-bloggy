<p>My previous <a href="/2006/10/two-art-benefit.html">post</a> on the subject seemed to be popular, so here is a new list of upcoming art benefits:</p>
<ul>
	<li>The Kitchen has a <a href="http://www.thekitchen.org/auction2006/">benefit art auction</a> on November 2.  That link already has images of many of the works to be auctioned.  Impressive.  The lovely and talented Christopher Gaillard of Sotheby&#8217;s, who always helps out at Momenta&#8217;s benefit, will be the auctioneer.</li>
	<li>The Lower Manhattan Cultural Council (<span class="caps">LMCC</span>) has a <a href="http://www.lmcc.net/us/benefits/benefit2006.11.8/index.html">benefit</a> on November 8 aimed at a bit richer crowd than some &#8212; tickets start at $250.  I don&#8217;t see any kind of art auction in the description, but the snacks are provided by <a href="http://www.ushgnyc.com/">Danny Meyer</a>, which is a good thing.</li>
	<li>Winning points for having a <a href="http://www.myspace.com/bettejeanbenefit">MySpace Page</a>, the &#8220;BetteJean&#8221; benefit to raise money for research on <span class="caps">ALS</span> (Amyotrophic Lateral sclerosis), often referred to as &#8220;Lou Gehrig&#8217;s disease,&#8221; takes place on November 9th at Galapagos.  There will be a silent auction that includes work by <a href="http://www.jamesnares.com/">James Nares</a> and others, plus musical performances.  They&#8217;re still encouraging submissions of works for the silent auction.  Artists, this might be your chance to get noticed by some new collectors.  Tickets are only $10.</li>
</ul>
