<p>Aargh.  It&#8217;s bad enough that Bush wants to <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/news/releases/2004/02/20040224-2.html">&#8220;protect&#8221; marriage</a>:</p>
<blockquote> Marriage cannot be severed from its cultural, religious and natural roots without weakening the good influence of society. Government, by recognizing and protecting marriage, serves the interests of all. Today I call upon the Congress to promptly pass, and to send to the states for ratification, an amendment to our Constitution defining and protecting marriage as a union of man and woman as husband and wife. The amendment should fully protect marriage, while leaving the state legislatures free to make their own choices in defining legal arrangements other than marriage.<br />
<br />
America is a free society, which limits the role of government in the lives of our citizens. This commitment of freedom, however, does not require the redefinition of one of our most basic social institutions. Our government should respect every person, and protect the institution of marriage. There is no contradiction between these responsibilities. We should also conduct this difficult debate in a manner worthy of our country, without bitterness or anger.<br />
<br />
In all that lies ahead, let us match strong convictions with kindness and goodwill and decency. </blockquote>
<p>But we also have to listen to the press <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2004/02/24/politics/24CND-GAY.html">lying</a> about what the amendment would do:</p>
<blockquote>Mr. Bush was not specific today about the wording he would like to see Congress adopt in beginning the constitutional-amendment process. He did not, for instance, mention legislation proposed by Representative Marilyn Musgrave, Republican of Colorado.<br />
<br />
The amendment that Ms. Musgrave and other lawmakers are backing in the House says: &#8220;Neither this Constitution or the constitution of any state, nor state or federal law, shall be construed to require that marital status or the legal incidents thereof be conferred upon unmarried couples or groups.&#8221; The White House spokesman, Scott McClellan, said just before the president&#8217;s announcement that Mr. Bush believed the Musgrave measure &#8220;meets his principles.&#8221;</blockquote>
<p>Most reasonable analyses of the Musgrave language state that it would ban civil unions as well, overturning even existing law.  Here is a writeup from <a href="http://balkin.blogspot.com/2003_11_16_balkin_archive.html#106944325583265936">Yale Law professor Jack Balkin</a>, and one from <a href="http://www.fair.org/activism/marriage-amendment.html"><span class="caps">FAIR</span></a>.</p>
<p><b>Do not trust the mainstream media to tell you the truth about this.</b></p>
<p>One last thought.  The Democrats&#8217; (including Kerry and Edwards but not Kucinich) position on this, one of &#8220;we don&#8217;t support gay marriage but we don&#8217;t support the amendment either&#8221; is bullshit.  This kind of splitting hairs is revolting when we&#8217;re talking about civil rights, and they&#8217;re going to be painted as homo-loving liberals by the <span class="caps">GOP</span> no matter what they do.  Why not take a principled position rather than some stupid focus group-created one?  I will hold my nose and vote for the Democratic candidate, but I can&#8217;t say I&#8217;m excited about it, unless a miracle happens and we get Kucinich.</p>
