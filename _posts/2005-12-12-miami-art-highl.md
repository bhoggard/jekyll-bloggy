<p>In no particular order, with an emphasis on art for which I have images&#8230;</p>
<p>We did not make it to the &#8220;main&#8221; fair (Art Basel Miami Beach), or Scope (unfortunately), or the containers, but we did hit the Rubell and Margulies collections, plus <a href="http://www.newartdealers.org/"><span class="caps">NADA</span></a>, <a href="http://www.aquaartmiami.com/">Aqua</a>, and a tiny bit of <a href="http://www.frisbeeisfun.com/">Frisbee</a>.</p>
<p><strong><span class="caps">NADA</span></strong></p>
<p>Remember how I <a href="/2005/11/performances-in.html">recommended</a> Elena Kovylina&#8217;s <em>Waltz</em> before we left?  I did dance with her one round, but passed on drinking vodka with her.  It was an intense and heart-breaking performance, and it felt odd to see such a work in the bright Miami sun.</p>
<p>Her performance was presented by <a href="http://www.schroederromero.com/">Schroeder Romero</a>.  They presented work by an artist new to the gallery (and new to me) named Ken Weaver.  An exhibition of his work will be their inaugural show in Chelsea in January.  They really stood out as one walked by the booth at <span class="caps">NADA</span> &#8211; dazzling red and white images.</p>

<p>Several galleries impressed with their overall programs.  One was <a href="http://www.electrografica.com/galeria%5Fcomercial/">Galería Comercial</a>, which is located in San Juan, Puerto Rico.  It exists somewhere between the non-profit and commercial gallery worlds &#8212; but on purpose unlike some galleries!  At <span class="caps">NADA</span>, they showed posters and other works by Pedro Vélez, plus a number of other artists working in media ranging from painting to video.</p>
<p>Another space with an impressive booth was the artist-run <a href="http://www.transmissiongallery.org/">Transmission Gallery</a> in Glasgow, Scotland.  The experience reminded me a little of when we were at the Affordable Art Fair, and the non-profit <a href="http://www.cfeva.org/">Center For Emerging Visual Artists</a> (Philadelphia) stood out in a big way.  I see plenty of great work from commercial galleries, but several of the non-profit/artist-run spaces really stand out at fairs like this.  Transmission will be at <a href="http://www.thearmoryshow.com/">The Armory Show</a> in March.  Here is an image of the wall installation by Lorna MacIntyre in their booth:</p>
<p><img alt="transmission lorna macintyre" src="/mt/archives/transmission-lorna-macintyr.jpg" width="500" height="375" /></p>

The work included sculpture, works on paper, and fabric works incorporating paint and sewing.  Here is one of her sculptures, from the gallery web site:</p>
