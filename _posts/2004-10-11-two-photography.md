<p>They both close Saturday, so get on over there.  <a href="http://www.yanceyrichardson.com/">Yancey Richardson</a> is on 22nd Street in Chelsea.</p>
<p>Mitch Epstein&#8217;s show, titled <em>Family Business</em>, documents the collapse of his father&#8217;s furniture and real estate business in Holyoke, Massachusetts.  It also becomes a document of the collapse of small businesses and old city downtowns.  A number of the images would be moving without the background story, but they&#8217;re devastating as soon as you know it.</p>

<p>Appropriately, the other show is Oraien Catledge&#8217;s <em>Cabbagetown</em>, with images from his <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0292710941/tristanmedial-20">book</a> of the same name.  Catledge photgraphed the people of Cabbagetown, a small, impoverished milltown not far from downtown Atlanta.  The images are from the 80s, but look like <span class="caps">WPA</span> photographs of sharecroppers during the Depression.</p>
