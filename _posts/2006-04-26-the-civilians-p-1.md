<p>Regular readers of <em>bloggy</em> know that I&#8217;m a fan of this wonderful theater group known as The Civilians.  Here is a <a href="http://www.google.com/custom?domains=bloggy.com&amp;sitesearch=bloggy.com&amp;q=%22the+civilians%22&amp;sa=Search&amp;client=pub-5677245260484261&amp;forid=1&amp;channel=9421574984&amp;ie=ISO-8859-1&amp;oe=ISO-8859-1&amp;cof=GALT%3A%23008000%3BGL%3A1%3BDIV%3A%23336699%3BVLC%3A663399%3BAH%3Acenter%3BBGC%3AFFFFFF%3BLBGC%3A336699%3BALC%3A0000FF%3BLC%3A0000FF%3BT%3A000000%3BGFNT%3A0000FF%3BGIMP%3A0000FF%3BFORID%3A1%3B&amp;hl=en">search</a> to give you an idea of all I&#8217;ve written in the past.  There a few theater companies doing this mix of fun and political work.  We have attended pretty much every production they&#8217;ve done since we first met them.</p>
<p>Their next benefit, titled <a href="http://www.thecivilians.org/patriotacts.html"><em>Patriot Acts: An American Vaudeville</em></a> is co-sponsored by <a href="http://www.thenation.com/"><em>The Nation</em></a> and includes an amazing cast of performers.  It takes place on May 8th at 7:30, and tickets start at $35 dollars.</p>
<p>We&#8217;ll be attending the concert and the after-party.  Join us if you can!</p>
<p>Here is the blurb from them:</p>
<blockquote>The Civilians Announces One-Night-Only<br />
<span class="caps">PATRIOT</span> <span class="caps">ACTS</span>: AN <span class="caps">AMERICAN</span> <span class="caps">VAUDEVILLE</span><br />
with DJ Spooky, Ollabelle, Jackie Hoffman and More.<br />
May 8th at The Skirball Center, <span class="caps">NYU</span>.<br />
 <br />
The Civilians theater company, The Nation magazine and the Skirball Center for Performing Arts present <span class="caps">PATRIOT</span> <span class="caps">ACTS</span>: AN <span class="caps">AMERICAN</span> <span class="caps">VAUDEVILLE</span> a one-night-only celebration in words and music of a great American tradition, proving there&#8217;s another side to patriotism, the show revives the forgotten progressive history of many icons of American culture. Artists of The Civilians are joined by guest artists Paul D. Miller aka DJ Spooky, Mary Testa, and Jackie Hoffman; guest speakers David Rakoff, Air America&#8217;s Sam Seder and Victor Navasky, musical groups Ollabelle, Girlyman, Tom Kitt Band, Pierce Woodward Band, Atomic Grind Show and the acrobatic comediennes The Wau Wau Sisters.  Visit <a href="http://www.thecivilians.org/patriotacts.html">www.thecivilians.org</a> for more information.<br />
 <br />
<span class="caps">PATRIOT</span> <span class="caps">ACTS</span> brings the cultural legacy of the left back to life, revealing another side to songs like <em>America The Beautiful</em>, <em>This Land is Your Land</em>, and <em>Born in the <span class="caps">USA</span></em>. From these classics, the show moves into contemporary songs by the guest performers and selections from The Civilians recent hit show <em>(I Am) Nobody&#8217;s Lunch</em>.<br />

<span class="caps">THE</span> <span class="caps">CIVILIANS</span> is a New York-based, non-profit theater company that creates original work from investigations into real life, touring both nationally and internationally. Recent shows include the hit (I Am) Nobody&#8217;s Lunch, a cabaret about how we know what we know in the age of (mis)information.  Reviewing Lunch, The New York Times wrote, &#8220;Snappy, scrappy and performed with deadpan razzmatazz by a young cast of six, the latest model is a funny, searching, at times plaintive look at the dangerous blurring of fact and myth in American culture and the unease that is its natural by product.&#8221;<br />
 <br />
<span class="caps">THE</span> <span class="caps">NATION</span>  is America&#8217;s oldest weekly magazine and most read regularly published opinion journal.  Established by abolitionists in 1865, The Nation has long been regarded as one of the country&#8217;s definitive journalistic voices on politics, culture, books and the arts. </blockquote>
