<p>I have to admit I hadn&#8217;t heard of this before, but <a href="http://www.greg.org">Greg Allen</a> just pointed me to <a href="http://www.greencine.com/">Greencine</a>, the &#8220;little guy/gal&#8221; alternative to the ever-growing <a href="http://www.netflix.com/">Netflix</a>.</p>
<blockquote>GreenCine carries a select collection of over 10,000 titles, with an accent on indie, art house, classics, foreign, documentary, anime and Asian cinema. You can check out three titles at a time, with no due dates, no late fees&#8230;and we pay the postage!  Hang on to them as long as you like.  Return one&#8230;we send you the next one on your list.  Only $21.95 a month, flat fee, with a portion of proceeds benefiting film arts organizations. </blockquote>
<p>I suspect the member reviews are more useful to me than those on Netflix, which tends to get plenty of &#8220;Why is this film so boring &#8212; no action!&#8221; comments on a lot of interesting indie films.</p>
<p>Hmm&#8230; What about my 310-disc queue on Netflix?</p>
