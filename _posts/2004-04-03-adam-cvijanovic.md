Adam Cvijanovic&#8217;s mural of <i>Osage Avenue</i> in &#8220;Ideal City&#8221; at the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts</p>
<p>We have seen every almost every show in New York of <a href="http://www.bellwethergallery.com/cvijanovic.html">Adam Cvijanovic</a>&#8217;s work since Richard Anderson first showed him in the early 90s.  I think he is a very smart artist and great painter, and, like so many artists we know, is a sweet person who has managed to not be chewed up by the Art World.  He has a show at the <a href="http://www.pafa.org/pressTemplate.jsp?ID=2106">Academy of Find Arts in Philadelphia</a> titled &#8220;Ideal City.&#8221;</p>
<p>Roberta Fallon, of <a href="http://www.fallonandrosof.com/artblog">artblog</a> fame, has a great <a href="http://www.artnet.com/magazine/features/fallon/fallon4-2-04.asp">interview</a> with him on Artnet.com.</p>
<p>His choice of thematic material for the show is interesting, playing off two aspects of <a href="http://www2.coloradocollege.edu/Dept/EN/Utopus/links.html">Utopian</a> thinking in Phildelphia history:  the Quakers and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MOVE"><span class="caps">MOVE</span></a>.</p>
