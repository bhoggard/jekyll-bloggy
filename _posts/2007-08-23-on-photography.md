<p>I love <a href="http://www.303gallery.com/">303 Gallery</a> and its program, but given the fact that we&#8217;ve been told not to take photographs in the gallery, I find it funny that founder Lisa Spellman is quoted in <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0815412452/tristanmedial-20"><em>The Art Dealers</em></a> saying,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>As a student I had been immediately been touched by the generation of appropriationist art and by the intensity of political discussions centering on the new notions of Postmodernism.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The show in question was <a href="http://www.303gallery.com/artists/heilmann/index.html">Mary Heilman&#8217;s exhibition in 2005</a>.  At the time, there were only work images on the website, and no installation views.  How can you write about a Mary Heilman show without talking about how the various works relate to each other in the space?</p>
