<p>And better yet, why does anyone consider her a Democrat or a liberal or even a rational human being?  As <a href="http://www.boomantribune.com/story/2008/8/13/10433/1626">BooMan</a> says:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I still run into old friends occasionally that not only think Maureen Dowd is a good columnist but that she is a liberal and uses her column to advocate for the Left. Without fail, these friends are busy people that have not spent the Bush years reading alternative media and blogs. They&#8217;re somewhat like Democrats that were trapped in amber sometime in 2000-2001, just before the rise of the Blogosphere. They have puzzled about why Dowd spent the last three months of the 2000 campaign writing about how horrible Al Gore was as a candidate, but then they remember those zingers she put down on Dick &#8216;Big Time&#8217; Cheney and his geographically-impaired sidekick. Ooh&#8230;that felt sooo good.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>This is a message to my old friends trapped in amber. Maureen Dowd doesn&#8217;t root for Democrats. She uses her column to mock Democrats, drive wedges between Democrats, and to reinforce negative stereotypes about Democrats. Yes, she is somewhat irreverent and she does her share of blasting Republicans. Occasionally, when her righteous ire is up, she can really let the Republicans have it. But you can start a count now. It&#8217;s August 13th. Dowd does two columns a week. If she doesn&#8217;t take any time off, she&#8217;ll write thirty-three more columns between now and the election. I guarantee you that the majority of them will not be helpful to the cause of Barack Obama. Today&#8217;s <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2008/08/13/opinion/13dowd.html?partner=rssnyt&amp;emc=rss">column</a> is about as unhelpful as it gets.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Dowd is also the columnist that <a href="http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9405E1DB1E38F935A25755C0A96F958260">said</a>, in the run-up to the 2000 election:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Al Gore is so feminized and diversified and ecologically correct, he&#8217;s practically lactating.</p>
</blockquote>
