<p>We attended the press preview of the Armory Show today.  It was called a press preview, but we often had trouble looking at work because of roaming packs of coifed collector women.  I&#8217;m sure all the big name collectors were there, and I spotted the Rubells and Horts right away. I had heard from at least one gallery that they had had some difficulties with getting a reasonable number of passes for their collectors, but I guess plenty of them didn&#8217;t have too much trouble.</p>
<p>On to the positive!</p>
<p>My favorite galleries in the fair so far:</p>
<ul>
	<li>David Kordansky &#8211; Patrick Hill, Mark Flores, Aaron Curry</li>
	<li>Zeno X &#8211; Jenny Scobel, Anne-Mie Van Kerckhoven</li>
	<li>Stuart Shave Modern Art &#8211; Tom Burr, Collier Schorr</li>
</ul>
<p>We only made it through most of one pier, so we have to go back.</p>
<p>Strangest overheard utterance at Armory:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Commercial.  It&#8217;s the new non-profit.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>I have no idea to what it referred.  Perhaps the presence of non-profit art spaces selling work at the fair?</p>
<p>We tried to go to the press preview for Scope, but that <a href="http://fromthefloor.blogspot.com/2006/03/scope-0-fdny-100-pissed-off-writers.html">didn&#8217;t work out</a>.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.fountainexhibit.com/">Fountain</a> had some fun stuff from several galleries, and a good mixed crowd, ranging from grungy art collective types to prosperous collectors.</p>
<p>I just put up a bunch of photos of things I liked, and could get decent photos of, on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/bloggy/">Flickr</a>.</p>
