<p>We visited <a href="http://www.whitecolumns.org/">White Columns</a> on Saturday, and I have to say the current set of shows is the best work so far of Matthew Higgs&#8217;s new administration.  <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/11/25/arts/design/25wall.html">Roberta Smith</a> appears to agree.  Congratulations to White Columns for such a great review.</p>
<p>The group show in the main space, titled <em>Open Walls</em> and curated by Mr. Higgs, includes great work, with only two artists (Alexandre Singh and Robin Graubard) whose work I&#8217;ve seen before.  The other artists in that show are Tariq Alvi, Colter Jacobsen, Christopher Russell, and Pam Servatius.  The wall collage/painting/etc. by Colter Jacobsen and the collage wheelchair on the ceiling by Tariq Alvi are particularly impressive.</p>
<p><img alt="elizabeth-peyton.jpg" src="/mt/archives/elizabeth-peyton.jpg" width="247" height="300" /></p>
<p>Elizabeth Peyton, <em>Ladovico Capponi</em></p>
<p>In one of the White Rooms is a fun show called <em>The Early Show</em>, curated by  Elysia Borowy-Reeder, Scott Reeder, and Tyson Reeder of the General Store in Milwaukee.  The premise is a show of early work by artists who are now well-known, ranging from Cory Arcangel to David Reed.  Some are works from the artists&#8217; teens, but some are from childhood.  It appears Ms. Peyton already had developed a specific style by the time she was 16 or 17.  I also loved another opportunity to see the video of a performance by Insecticide, the band of a very young Cory and Jamie Arcangel.</p>
<p><img alt="alba ballard soft and dry" src="/mt/archives/alba-ballard-soft-and-dry.jpg" width="300" height="210" /></p>
<p>Alba Ballard, <em>Soft and Dry</em></p>
<p>While you&#8217;re there, don&#8217;t miss Arne Svenson&#8217;s images of Alba Ballard&#8217;s costumed parrots.</p>
