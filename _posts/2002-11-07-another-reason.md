<p>After the teacher-parent council decided to allow a Gay-Straight Alliance to meet at a high school in Boyd County, Kentucky, 420 of the school&#8217;s 990 students <a href="http://story.news.yahoo.com/news?tmpl=story&ncid=101&e=4&cid=101&u=/po/20021107/co_po/students_boycott_school_due_to_gay_group">boycotted school</a>.</p>
<p>Those 19 kids in the <span class="caps">GSA</span> are very brave.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s a another nice bit:</p>
<blockquote>  The Rev. Tim York, a Baptist minister and president of the Boyd County Ministerial Association, said the association is planning a community rally against the gay-straight alliance on Sunday.<br />
<br />
&#8220;The community is really upset,&#8221; York said about the alliance meeting in the school. &#8220;There&#8217;s a moral issue here that&#8217;s brought us to a place of discussion.&#8221; </blockquote>
<p>That&#8217;s like all of the &#8220;religious&#8221; people in my home state of Arkansas who couldn&#8217;t be bothered to help all of the hungry or homeless people that needed help, but they couldn&#8217;t wait to protest restaurants serving alcohol, or &#8220;special rights for queers&#8221;.</p>
