<p>As I&#8217;m sure my readers have noticed, I rarely post about politics anymore.  I&#8217;m too disgusted.  A political system that allows the subways go begging for enough funds <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/columnists/nyc-sub0616,0,7504451.column?coll=ny-news-columnists">to keep token booths open</a>, even in high profile locations like Rockefeller center, and <span class="caps">NYC</span> schools to crumble, but lets our elected leaders spend public money subsidizing sports stadiums for rich team owners, is one too ridiculous to comment upon.  I&#8217;ve had it.  The current proposals are for the state and city to spend at least $180 million directly on infrastructure for the new Mets stadium in Queens, and that doesn&#8217;t count things like property tax diversions and tax-exempt bond borrowing.  The Yankees got a similar deal.  A good source for this info is a site called <a href="http://www.fieldofschemes.com/">Field of Schemes</a>.</p>
<p>As a free-lancer, I paid my quarterly estimated taxes yesterday to the feds, the state, and the city (including a tax <span class="caps">NYC</span> has called the Unincorporated Business Tax just for people like me).  I can&#8217;t stand the idea that it&#8217;s going to subsidize professional sports when so much that really matters is going begging.</p>
<p>And by the way, the next time the <span class="caps">NYPD</span> complains about their pay, suggest that their corporate bosses should kick in some money.  I guess the War on Terror is under control if they have time to <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/06/16/arts/music/16sann.html?pagewanted=print">raid Kim&#8217;s Video looking for mix CDs</a>, and bring along a &#8220;representative&#8221; of the Recording Industry Association of America to help them round up and arrest five people working at Kim&#8217;s.  They kept them all in jail overnight.</p>
