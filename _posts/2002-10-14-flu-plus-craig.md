<p>I might not post much for a little bit.  I think I have the flu, or at least something unpleasant with a cough.</p>
<p>We saw a reading of a new screenplay called &#8220;Adam and Steve&#8221; tonight, written by <a href="http://www.craigchester.com/">Craig Chester</a>, at Fez.  The cast included Ileana Douglas, Nora Burns, <a href="http://www.mikealbo.com/">Mike Albo</a>, and Bryan Batt.  It also featured the <a href="http://www.dazzledancers.com/">Dazzle Dancers</a>.  I half expected to see <a href="http://www.revolutionfreedom.com/now.html">Dan&#8217;l</a> there.  It was very funny, and the cast really got into it.</p>
<p>Two favorite moments:</p>
<p>Two men go into a store in Chelsea, looking to buy a ring.  The salesman asks, &#8220;Nipple, cock, pride, or finger?&#8221;</p>
<p>When asked what it was like to be in the city on 9/11, someone responds, &#8220;Oh, you know.  The sky was all purple, there were people running everywhere.&#8221;</p>

<p>I once had Craig Chester as a waiter for <a href="http://www.restaurantflorent.com/">Florent</a>&#8217;s big Bastille Day celebration outside on Gansevoort Street.  This was after <a href="http://us.imdb.com/Title?0105508">Swoon</a>, not before, and he said he was helping out a friend for the day.  I remember this was the year that there were a lot of protests over the French resuming nuclear testing in the South Pacific.  Chi Chi Valenti, during her performance, unfolded a big sign that said, &#8220;<span class="caps">STOP</span> <span class="caps">FRENCH</span> <span class="caps">BOMBS</span>!&#8221;  The crowd went wild.</p>
