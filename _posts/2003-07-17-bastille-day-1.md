<p>Work stuff, plus <a href="http://www.nyu.edu/deutscheshaus">German class</a> twice per week is killing my schedule.  That&#8217;s why I haven&#8217;t had time to post about our lovely <a href="/2003/07/bastille-day.html">Bastille Day</a> evening at Florent with <a href="http://www.glennalicious.org/archives/2003_07.html#001305">Glenn</a> and (as we all refer to him) Chris From Texas.</p>
<p>I left my class early on Monday, making sure everyone realized I was leaving to celebrate Bastille Day (festen Bastille Tag) and hopped in a cab.  I had a very cool cabdriver name Khalid, who had one of the longer cabs, good air conditioning, was listening to <span class="caps">WBAI</span>, offered me one of his apples, and found Gansevoort without me having to give any directions.</p>
<p>We all sat outside for hours and drank lots of red and sparking wine.  It was a bit more low-key than some years, and they didn&#8217;t close off Gansevoort as they sometimes do.  The burlesque performer below is <a href="http://www.palomaragency.com/talent_dirty.htm">Dirty Martini</a>, whom I&#8217;ve seen a number of times, including at a contemporary music concert on Valentine&#8217;s Day.  If <a href="http://www.uffish.com/archives/001611.html">Chris of Uffish</a> were a stripper, this would be her.  She is quite fabulous, and the the first burlesque performer to  appear in Sarajevo as soon as the peace accord was signed.</p>
<p>Yes, that is <a href="http://www.betseyjohnson.com/">Betsey Johnson</a> in the yellow hat in the back of the Dirty Martini pictures.</p>
