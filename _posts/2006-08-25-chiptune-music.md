<p>Pulsewave is a monthly event at  downtown space <a href="http://www.thetanknyc.org/">The Tank</a>, showcasing &#8220;low-bit, video game influenced musicians and video artists&#8221; and &#8220;people getting down to the sounds of some rockstar jamming away on nintendos, gameboys, and ataris.&#8221;</p>
<p>The performers will be:</p>
<ul>
	<li><a href="http://www.myspace.com/anamanaguchi">Anamanaguchi</a> &#8211; Releasing their debut album Power Supply through 8bitpeoples. Powerpop meets <span class="caps">NES</span>, Weezer meets Triforce, Ratatat meets Dr. Wily.</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.myspace.com/thedepreciationguild">The Depreciation Guild</a> &#8211; The Guild steps out of hibernation to rock you into a trance with their astral chiptune melodies. However, at the same time they&#8217;ll wake you up with some pumping and crushing drum-heavy anthems.</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.myspace.com/nullsleep">Nullsleep</a> &#8211; The King of 8bit (and quite possibly the universe) is at it again. Watch as he wields the GameBoy as if the d-pad was used to make the entire crowd dance simultaneously.</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.voltagecontrolled.com/index.html">Voltage Controlled</a> makes the show with his jarring and colorful broken <span class="caps">NES</span> visuals.</li>
</ul>
<p>Saturday, August 26 @ 10pm sharp &#8211; $6<br />
The Tank, 279 Church Street, between Franklin and White</p>
<p>If you schedule yourself well, you can start that evening in the East Village for the <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/6/2909">Smitten</a> show at Giant Robot and then head downtown for The Tank.</p>
