<p>Yes, sometimes their owner/publisher, and columnists like Zev Chafets (blaming Jerrold Nadler for not caring about the safety of American Jews) make me want th throw the paper across the room.  But then they do something like <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/ideas_opinions/story/26758p-25423c.html">this</a>:</p>
<blockquote>Gay rights overdue<br />
<br />
State Republicans are signaling that a gay rights bill stalled in Albany for three decades may finally come to a vote. Why now? It&#8217;s obvious. Election Day is a month away, and Gov. Pataki&#8217;s efforts to get the legislation passed this year have come to naught. Gay and lesbian voters comprise a sizable, focused chunk of the voting public.<br />
<br />
And so the <span class="caps">GOP</span> is scrambling: Pataki&#8217;s angling for a preelection Senate vote on the bill. State Senate Majority Leader Joe Bruno hints that a vote could actually happen &#8211; after the November election. Mayor Bloomberg insists the measure will be passed before the end of the year, thanks (of course) to Pataki&#8217;s support.<br />
<br />
Pataki&#8217;s name was booed by many when Bloomberg mentioned it recently at a major gay event, and the frustration is understandable. After 30 years, you&#8217;d think that adding two little words &#8211; sexual orientation &#8211; to the list of personal qualities protected from discrimination under state law would be a no-brainer. Twelve states and 20 New York localities, including the city, have managed to do it. The holdup in Albany? While Pataki has supported the bill for several years, though initially he opposed it, Bruno, who is allied with some of the state Senate&#8217;s most conservative Republicans, hasn&#8217;t yet struck a deal.<br />
<br />
The bill, like so many pieces of legislation that languish in Albany limbo, should have been approved long ago. It should be passed as soon as a new session can be convened. But meanwhile, let&#8217;s hear &#8211; pardon us &#8211; straight talk from state leaders, starting with Bruno: Will the gay rights bill, a matter of simple decency, be passed or won&#8217;t it? If they can&#8217;t deliver, surely they understand: Voters, gay or straight, can swing both ways.</blockquote>
