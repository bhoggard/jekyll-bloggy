<p>Buried in a <em>New York Times</em> <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2005/06/09/arts/design/09vecchi.html?pagewanted=print">obituary</a> titled <em>Floriano Vecchi, 84, Publisher With Literary and Artistic Bent, Dies</em>, is this interesting tidbit:</p>
<blockquote>Mr. Vecchi was born near Bologna and came to New York in 1952. He and a partner, Richard Miller, opened a successful business, Tiber Press, the next year, and Mr. Vecchi operated it until 1977. Together with Daisy Aldan they also started a small literary magazine, Folder, which became a vehicle for young writers of the period.<br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
Mr. Vecchi may have left another lasting impression on the New York art scene in 1962 when Andy Warhol visited Tiber to have a screen made of a dollar bill he had drawn on paper, the first of his United States currency compositions.<br />
<br />
According to Riva Castleman in the June 2004 issue of Print Quarterly, Mr. Vecchi told Warhol to redraw it on Mylar for refinement and instructed him in the screen-printing process, which Warhol promptly introduced in his studio, passing it on to Robert Rauschenberg as well.</blockquote>
