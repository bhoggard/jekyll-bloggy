<p>Now that I have the new blog working, I can talk about last weekend and put up a few pictures.  You&#8217;ll remember that it was 95 degrees and very humid &#8212; a lovely <span class="caps">NYC</span> August weekend.  If we hadn&#8217;t had visitors, I would not have left my a/c-blessed apartment at all.  I would have ordered in everything, including ice cream.  But we did have visitors &#8212; two sets!</p>
<p>The first set was my friend David from high school, visiting from Chicago &#8212; a fellow &#8220;band fag&#8221; &#8212; and his friend Stacy (a charming Atlanta girl).  Here is a picture of David wilting in a subway station.   That orange blur on the left is <a href="http://jameswagner.com">James</a> jumping out of the way to prevent having his picture taken.</p>

<p>Let&#8217;s just say David has grown up a bit since I knew him in high school, and I mean that in the best possible way.  He was two classes behind me, and I think I was probably about 5&#8217;7&quot; or 5&#8217;8&quot; during my senior year.  David was smaller.  Now we&#8217;re about the same height, but he works out more than I and is &#8220;hunkier&#8221;.</p>
<p>He looked me up via Google using my real name about six months ago, and sent me an email.  He was thrilled to find out that the moniker of &#8220;band fag&#8221; was truer for both of us than it was for many of the other nerdy band people.  It was somewhat strange to be two homos in <span class="caps">NYC</span>, chatting about the world, 18 years after we last saw each other in a backward little town in Arkansas.</p>
<p>We went to Big Cup to eat lunch before heading uptown to the <a href="http://www.metmuseum.org/special/se_event.asp?OccurrenceId={C9AAB347-859E-11D5-93FE-00902786BF44}">Eakins show</a> at the Met &#8212; which I highly recommend.  Big Cup managed to surprise me, as if often does.  I didn&#8217;t see <a href="http://www.edmundwhite.com/">Edmund White</a> there this time, but there were two men at the next table discussing the recent news that the <a href="/2002/08/im-surprised.html">U.S. won&#8217;t be increasing Egypt&#8217;s foreign aid</a> because of their persecution of a democracy activist.   One of them mentioned (I didn&#8217;t put this in my post), that Bush couldn&#8217;t be bothered to say anything when Egypt <a href="http://www.hrw.org/press/2001/11/egypt1114.htm">tried and jailed a large number of men for homosexuality</a>.   Foreign aid discussions at Big Cup!</p>
<p>Our other visitors for the weekend were James&#8217;s nephew Paul and his girlfriend Elizabeth, visiting from DC.  She was <i>fabulous</i>.  Imagine a family member dating someone who can chat about Donald Judd, British explorers in Antarctica, and is incredibly beautiful as well!  I was ready for her to move in with us.  We spent some time walking around downtown near Ground Zero, had lunch along the water at Southwest, and visited the <a href="http://www.castlebar.ie/news/article_236.shtml">Irish Hunger Memorial</a>.  I hadn&#8217;t realized that it talked about hunger on a more global level, and doesn&#8217;t just concentrate on the Irish Potato Famine alone.  There are even quotes from reports on starvation in Afghanistan in 2001 (before 9/11).  It&#8217;s an interesting piece of architecture, including the ruins of a stone cottage brought over from Ireland.  I love the provenance of the cottage:  it belonged to the family of the memorial designer&#8217;s (gay) partner.  Elizabeth took a couple of pictures of us with Paul, so unfortunately she&#8217;s not in these:</p>
