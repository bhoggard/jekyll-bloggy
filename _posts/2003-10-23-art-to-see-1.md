<p>I&#8217;m still busy coding, so here are some art recommendations:</p>
<p><img alt="tom-sanford-defosition.jpg" src="/mt/archives/tom-sanford-defosition.jpg" width="379" height="288" border="0" /><br />
Tom Sanford<br />
The Defosition, 2002<br />
Oil and acrylic on panel<br />
60&#8243; &#215; 80&#8243;</p>
<p>Tom Sanford at <a href="http://www.31grand.com/">31 Grand</a> in Williamsburg.  He has a weblog called <a href="http://www.thug4life.org/">thug4life.org</a> dedicated to his transformation to Tupac, but it seems a bit messed up at the moment.  We started following him after picking up a work on paper by him at a benefit for <a href="http://www.groundswellmural.org/">Groundswell</a>.</p>
<p><img alt="paul-mullins-sugar.jpg" src="/mt/archives/paul-mullins-sugar.jpg" width="397" height="399" border="0" /><br />
Paul Mullins<br />
Sugar (Hood Up)<br />
acrylic on panel<br />
48&#8243; &#215; 48&#8243;</p>
<p>Paul Mullins at <a href="http://www.lyonswiergallery.com/">lyonswier</a> &#8211; think Lucien Freud meets Nascar.  Really brilliant painting technique.</p>
<p><img alt="chris-ballantyne-split-double.jpg" src="/mt/archives/chris-ballantyne-split-double.jpg" width="500" height="380" border="0" /><br />
Chris Ballantyne<br />
Untitled (Split Double), 2003<br />
acrylic and ink on paper<br />
12 &#215; 16&#8243;</p>
<p>Chris Ballantyne at <a href="http://www.dcktcontemporary.com/"><span class="caps">DCKT</span> Contemporary</a> &#8211; beautiful suburban/landscapish show recommended to me by <a href="http://www.andrewlavallee.com/">Andrew LaVallee</a>.</p>
<p>Joe Ovelman&#8217;s wall piece (search my site for other discussions of his work) at <a href="http://www.apartment5begallery.com/">Apartment 5BE Gallery</a>.  Also, I have a <a href="https://bloggy.com/gallery/joe-ovelman-2walls">gallery</a> of one of his outside wall installations.</p>
<p>The inaugural exhibition at a new new media-oriented gallery called <a href="http://www.brycewolkowitz.com/">Bryce Wolkowitz</a> is definitely worth a visit.  Here is a work by <a href="http://www.numeral.com/">John F. Simon</a> made from an Apple Titanium PowerBook:</p>
