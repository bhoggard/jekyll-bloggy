<p>From the invaluable Ray Sanchez at <a href="http://www.newsday.com/news/columnists/nyc-sub0708,0,7701061.column?coll=ny-rightrail-columnist">Newsday</a></p>
<blockquote>
<p>Since 9/11, the aviation industry has received $18.1 billion for security from the federal Department of Homeland Security, according to the Washington-based American Public Transportation Association. By contrast, mass transit has received just $250 million.</p>
<p>Americans take public transportation 32 million times a day &#8212; 16 times more often than they travel on domestic airlines. About 4.5 million riders use the New York subway every weekday.</blockquote></p>
<p>Of course, the <span class="caps">MTA</span> is probably too incompetent to spend the money they are given.  Since 9/11, they have been given $600 million in state and federal funds for security improvements.  They have spent $30 million, with very little to show for it.  Most has been spent on planning and consultants.</p>
<p><strong>Updated:</strong>  I fixed the $300 million number.  It is $600 million.</p>
