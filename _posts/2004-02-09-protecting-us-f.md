<p>Several Cuban musicians were nominated for Grammies (Grammys?)  Most of them couldn&#8217;t attend the ceremony because the <span class="caps">USA</span> <a href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/3464801.stm">denied</a> them visas.</p>
<blockquote>US authorities have refused to let five Cuban Grammy Awards nominees travel to Sunday&#8217;s ceremony in Los Angeles.<br />
<br />
Musicians up for best tropical Latin album award &#8211; including veteran star Ibrahim Ferrer &#8211; have not got visas.<br />
<br />
Ferrer, 77, told press in the capital Havana: &#8220;I am not a terrorist. I couldn&#8217;t be one. I am a musician.&#8221;<br />
<br />
A US diplomat in Havana said the US administration could suspend the entry of people deemed to be &#8220;detrimental to the interest of the United States&#8221;. <br />
<br />
&#8230;<br />
<br />
Ferrer is the best-known of the nominees after appearing in 1999&#8217;s Buena Vista Social Club film. He recently won <span class="caps">BBC</span> Radio 3&#8217;s world music award for best artist from the Americas.<br />
<br />
The other artists to be refused visas are Guillermo Rubalcaba, Amadito Valdes, Barbarito Torres and the group Septeto Nacional Ignacio Pineiro.<br />
<br />
But pianist Chucho Valdes, nominated for best Latin jazz album, has been granted a visa. </blockquote>
