<p>In an <a href="/2003/10/two-more-art-me.html">earlier post</a> I mentioned that the Rivington Arms people didn&#8217;t think they needed a website.</p>
<blockquote>I once asked whether they were going to get one, and they said, &#8220;that&#8217;s not really the audience we&#8217;re aiming for.&#8221; This comes from people who were chatting with trucker-hatted visitors who were explaining that one simply cannot live comfortably in <span class="caps">NYC</span> with an apartment worth less than $1 million.</blockquote>
<p>Now I understand why, but I still think it&#8217;s cheesy to act as if they&#8217;re demeaning art by making it easier for people to find the gallery or learn more about their artists.  The Friday NY Times, in its <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2004/01/23/arts/design/23DEAL.html">article</a> about young art dealers, tells us that one of the partners is the daughter of Brice Marden.</p>
