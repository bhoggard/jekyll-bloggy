<p>Welcome, <a href="http://www.mteww.com/mtaaRR/news/twhid/on_james_wagner_amp_barry_hoggard.html"><span class="caps">MTAA</span>-RR readers</a>.  I&#8217;ll see if I can do better given such praise.  Bloggy has been rather quiet as I do work for several free-lance clients at once.</p>
<p>My favorite thing from yesterday&#8217;s humid Williamsburg gallery crawl was the <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/782">group show</a> at Sarah Bowen, particularly the work of Joyce Pensato and Susan Wanklyn.  We have work by both, and know Susan pretty well.  We&#8217;ve met Joyce several times, and I am always struck by the reaction of other artists when I mention her &#8212; that she is incredibly generous and kind to fellow artists, and a brilliant artist.  The photo below doesn&#8217;t do her wall pieces at the gallery justice, but here it is to give you some idea:</p>
<p><img alt="joyce-pensato-wall.jpg" src="/mt/archives/joyce-pensato-wall.jpg" width="375" height="500" /></p>
<p>Joyce Pensato<br />
wall drawing, charcoal on a roughed-up gallery wall</p>
<p>I was told she brought in an electric sander.  You can&#8217;t see it too well in the photo, but there is a pile of charcoal on the floor.  I would have put up a photo of Susan&#8217;s work, but I had trouble getting the colors right.  You&#8217;ll have to go see them yourself.</p>
<p>Other highlights of the day:  shows at Dam, Stuhltrager by <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/812">Mark Andreas</a> and <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/813">Brian Zimmernam</a>, and the charcoal on paper drawings by Rex Enseki in Black and White&#8217;s <a href="http://www.artcal.net/event/view/2/816">group show</a>.</p>
